Eagles Shake-Up, Lemon Hill Gets World Cup-Ready, and Philly’s New Wine Scene

Big news for Birds fans, World Cup buzz, and your shortcut to the city’s latest happenings.

🏈 Eagles: End of an Era (and Beginning of a Big Rebuild)

The A.J. Brown era is officially over. The Eagles finally traded the superstar WR to the Patriots in exchange for New England’s 2028 first-round pick and a 2027 fifth-rounder (yes, that’s not a typo). Brown posted a note to Philly thanking fans—so yeah, time to get sentimental. If you want to daydream about what the future holds, peep the team’s updated 2026 depth chart and mountain of draft picks.

⚽ World Cup Warm-Up: FIFA Fan Fest at Lemon Hill

The city’s getting in formation for the World Cup crowd. Lemon Hill is prepped to host FIFA’s official Fan Festival, and the kickoff’s going big with a free concert from Luis Fonsi (aka the "Despacito" guy). Expect crowds, music, and your feed to be flooded with selfies and fútbol hype very soon.

🏠 City Living: Real Change on Housing

Twenty-two new affordable homes are breaking ground in North Philly (Venango Views), and City Council’s “Turn the Key” program launched its first project in the 8th District (details here). It won’t fix the housing crunch overnight, but it’s something tangible (and long overdue).

🍷 Try a Wine Tour (for Real this Time)

If “Philly wine country” sounds like a punchline, Fishtown is out to prove you wrong. Check out one writer’s field test of the new Philly wine tour: multiple stops, natural wines, and neighborhood vibes. (Yes, there are snacks.)

🎉 Dates to Watch

  • Pride Party: Riot Nerd is hosting an all-out Pride dancefest at City Winery on June 19 (details).
  • Juneteenth Jam: African American Museum’s block party lands June 15 with DJ Jazzy Jeff and Slick Rick.
  • Summer Shows: The Mann Center is racing to finish renovations for summer concerts, if live music is your thing.
