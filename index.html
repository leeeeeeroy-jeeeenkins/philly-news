Birds Dominate, SEPTA Upgrade, Road Closures

Eagles Break the Slide — and Vegas

If you tuned out after the last couple birds games, good news: The Eagles smashed the Raiders 31-0 and actually looked like the team we remember. The defense held Vegas to just 75 total yards — nine NFL teams have one play for more than that — and Jalen Hurts delivered big with the national spotlight glaring. Seriously, this was a historical beatdown, and it might just be the reset we needed. More breakdown here.

Funeral for Officer Andy Chan: Expect Closures

Philadelphia is saying goodbye to Officer Andy Chan, who died years after a duty-related crash. Major road closures are in effect Tuesday around the Cathedral Basilica and 20th/Callowhill. If you drive or take SEPTA through Parkway territory, check your route before heading out. More on Chan’s story here.

SEPTA: A Bright Spot in Transit

Some rare good SEPTA news: the upgraded Wissahickon Transit Center in Northwest Philly is officially open, with a bigger, more weatherproof setup for the 7,000 daily riders who shuffle through. Get ready — tomorrow the Center City trolley tunnel closes after 9pm through early Tuesday morning. Bus shuttles are filling the gap, so plan accordingly.

  • A Land Rover went full drive-thru into the Wynnewood Starbucks (yes, again). No injuries, but don't expect your coffee fix there today.
