Yesterday in Philly

Speed Traps, Water Woes, and Fall Art Kicks Off

Here’s what you missed in Philly, 9/10–9/11—street cameras revving, pipes bursting, the Wanamaker organ's making noise (in a good way), and the MLK Bridge is finally almost back. Stay caught up, skip the endless headlines:

🛑 Traffic Just Got Real

  • Speed Cameras Go Live Monday on Broad: Philly is turning dozens of speed cams on along Broad Street starting Monday. If you haven’t gotten that parking ticket paid off yet, maybe also keep foot off the gas. Read what to expect and where the cameras are going.
  • MLK Drive Bridge Reopening After Two Long Years: The MLK Drive Bridge is set to reopen later this month after a massive, over-two-year repair. River drive commutes are about to get (slightly) less painful. Details here.
  • Water Main Break Shuts Down Stout Center: The massive downtown court building had to close yesterday thanks to a sudden water main break. Bad day for anyone with a court date (but maybe a good day for their nerves). More info.

🎭 Fall Arts & Free Stuff

  • Wanamaker Organ Awakens: After being pretty quiet for a while, the Wanamaker is hosting a new series of live performances this fall—think huge pipe organ and cool, immersive shows downtown. If you like culture, weird history, or simply feeling small under high ceilings: get the show schedule.
  • Free Arts Fest at the Kimmel: Mark your calendars for the free Philadelphia Fall Arts Fest at the Kimmel Center this weekend, kicking off the Ensemble Arts Philly season with a bang. What’s on tap.
  • Dilworth Park Hay Maze Returns: Dilworth’s annual Maze Days are back soon, so if you want massive city hay bales and peak fall vibes, you know where to go. Details here.

🍺 Closings & Openings

  • Iron Hill Brewery Shutting Three Locations: The regional chain is closing a few outposts, including Chestnut St. in Philly. If that’s your usual stop, time to find a new taproom. See which ones are closing.
  • Mawn’s New Cambodian Seafood Sibling: Oysters, crudo, and more—Chef Phila Lorn just opened Sao in West Philly, a new spot if you want serious seafood. Take a peek inside.

🎟️ Quick Hits

  • Library Upgrades: Richmond Library just reopened post-renovation. Go get your AC and WiFi fix. Here’s what’s new.
  • Sixers Tix on Sale: Single game tickets dropped if you’re feeling brave/hopeful for this season. Info here.

That’s the big stuff. If you’re driving, seriously watch your speed next week. If you’re looking for something to do, fall arts season just brought the city back to life. And if you had jury duty downtown—lucky.
