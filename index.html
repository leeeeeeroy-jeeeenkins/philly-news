Philly Clinches, Scams Hit, and a Classic Bar Returns

Good morning. While the Phillies partied out west, we got everything from warnings about new scams to a beloved bar re-appearing on the scene. Here’s what happened in Philly yesterday—no nonsense, just what you actually need to know.

⚾ Phillies Clinch, October Gets Real

The Phillies clinched their second straight NL East title in a nail-biter against the Dodgers (extra innings, obviously). You’ve got options for playoff tickets, and ‘Red October’ is officially on. They still have playoff seeding to lock down, so don’t check out yet.

🏙️ Money, Jobs, and Office Space Woes

Philly’s job market keeps bouncing back post-COVID—employment’s up, and we’re doing better than plenty of big cities. But office space sits empty, especially downtown. So, don’t expect empty Center City towers turning into condos overnight, but it’s a conversation that isn’t going away.

🍔 Grace Tavern’s Comeback

People are hyped: Grace Tavern is reopening after a shutdown that felt way too long. Burgers, craft beer, and that classic city-bar energy are back—if you know, you know.

🚨 Scams & Safety Stuff

Two different city warnings to have on your radar:

  • Phishing Alert: The Revenue Department is warning about scammy texts pretending to be from the city and asking for bank info. Don’t click or reply to anything sketchy.
  • Porch Violence: This won’t surprise anyone but it still sucks—a woman was shot and killed on her front porch in Strawberry Mansion. Major police presence, investigation ongoing.

🎉 Events & Philly Culture

  • Wanamaker Christmas Light Show could see cutbacks this year if fundraising doesn’t come through. If that show’s your holiday thing, stay tuned or consider chipping in.
  • The Philadelphia Cycling Classic is coming back in 2026 after a decade away. Ex-Mayor Nutter’s behind the push, and it’ll be hitting the Manayunk Wall again.
  • Calder Gardens just opened, bringing weird, modern sculpture energy to Ben Franklin Parkway. It’s not your usual museum—worth checking out if you want to see something new.

🏈 Some Quick Sports Hits

  • Eagles drama: national outlets are still obsessed with the "Tush Push" ban debate. For now, we can still shove with the best of them.
  • Flyers made it official: Carter Hart isn’t coming back—Brière says it’s a done deal, no matter the case outcome.

That’s the biggest stuff for Wednesday. Phillies are playoff-bound, watch your texts, and grab a booth at Grace before it’s slammed. Back tomorrow.
