Yesterday in Philly

The day Philly made a reservation with Michelin

Philly finally gets (actual) Michelin Stars

They said it would never happen, but last night the Michelin Guide dropped its first-ever stars for Philly. Three restaurants got stars: outshining a lot of the national food scene doubters. If you want a seat at these places, good luck—reservations are about to get impossible. Oh, and four Philly spots also made OpenTable's list of the 100 best in the country, for good measure.

If you want to eat something new and not impossible to book, check out Huda Burger in Rittenhouse: Oklahoma-style patties, Japanese milk buns, and a menu that doesn’t hate vegetarians.

Travel & Transit: Heads up for weekend plans

Marathon weekend is coming. Expect major road closures around Center City and the Parkway starting Friday—plus classic SEPTA detours, so check your commute before heading out. On the airline side, Allegiant Air is adding three new nonstop routes from PHL, which is a win if you’re allergic to layovers.

In union news: SEPTA and its largest union are heading back to the table, trying (again) to avoid a strike. No new deal yet, but worth watching if you value not having to walk three miles to work.

Sports: Injuries, odds, and mailbags

The Sixers are getting creative—with or without Joel Embiid, who’s still doubtful for Wednesday. Last night’s game saw the team muster another comeback (again without Paul George), but don’t look for a star-studded lineup this week. If you like overthinking strategy, check the latest Sixers mailbag.

The Eagles are still riding high in power rankings, but the real gossip: Will Lane Johnson make it back before playoffs? Bookies like Philly over Dallas next week (odds preview), but one bad O-line day changes everything.

Elsewhere in the city

  • Fake Philly Water Department payment sites are popping up—double check the URL before you pay that bill.
  • The Mütter Museum wants to take over a neighboring Center City church. Creepy? Maybe. Interesting? Definitely.
  • And in big news for public health nerds: Philly is launching its first dedicated anti-human trafficking campaign.

That's your catch-up. If you want to look like you read every article, now you do. See you out there.
