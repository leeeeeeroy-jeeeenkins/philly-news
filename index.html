Yesterday in Philly

Subject: Phillies Ready to Clinch, SEPTA Fares Up, and Rent-Stress is Real

Good morning, Philly. Here’s what actually mattered yesterday:

⚾ Phillies: Magic Number Down to ONE

The Phillies pulled off a come-from-behind win that sets their NL East magic number at 1. Barring catastrophe, they should clinch the division soon. For playoff obsessives: here’s who they might play and all the bracket scenarios for October. It’s about to get rowdy in South Philly.

🚇 SEPTA Squeeze: Fares Up, Some Service Back

Heads up if you ride regional rail or buses: fare hikes kick in this Sunday. We’re talking a 50-cent bump (to $2.75) for single rides and monthly passes going up too. The silver lining: more evening and weekend trains and buses are returning to pre-pandemic levels. Still, budget accordingly.

🏠 Renters Feel the Pinch

If you’re struggling to keep up with rent, you’re not alone (seriously). A new report puts several PA cities, including Philly, among the worst in the nation for quality of life—mainly thanks to sky-high housing costs and not enough affordable options. Par for the course, but it’s still worth paying attention as city officials argue over zoning and future development.

🟢 Sporting Side Notes

  • Eagles fans: In case you’re still processing the Super Bowl, take a stroll down memory lane with past Eagles-Chiefs battles. And yes, draft obsessives, 2026 college prospects are being talked about already.
  • The Sixers are doing their bargain-hunting thing again—Trendon Watford’s the latest veteran-minimum guy with potential upside.

🚨 Quick Hits

  • Longtime SEPTA employee arrested on serious charges—company says it’s cooperating with police.
  • Yesterday saw two kids shot in separate incidents in Northeast Philly (everyone expected to recover). Meanwhile, an 11-year-old missing in the city was found safe in Upper Darby after a search.
  • If you braved I-95 yesterday in Delaware County: that massive traffic backup was from a multi-vehicle crash and an ongoing investigation.

🍝, 🏃, and Figs?

  • For the food crowd: Montco’s Fig Festival drew local fruit obsessives this weekend.
  • Meanwhile, hundreds ran up the Art Museum steps for Legacy of Hope, raising money for cancer patients.

That’s it. If you’re heading out: bring that SEPTA card and an extra $0.50.
