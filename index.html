Housing Push, Sports Shuffles, and a Windy Mess

Good morning. Let’s run through what happened in Philly yesterday. Weather chaos, homelessness plans, sports drama, and, yes, more reasons to side-eye the grid.

Big Moves on Street Homelessness

Mayor Cherelle Parker wants the end of street homelessness in Philly to be more than just talk. She signed an executive order yesterday aiming to add 1,000 new shelter beds and get people off the street, plus a “Philadelphia Street Outreach Council.” (Details on the plan—and pushback—here.) In Montco, a Lansdale businessman pledged $1 million to help open a new shelter there. Read how it’ll work.

Rough Weather and Power Woes

Friday was a mess. The morning brought heavy rain, then temperatures plummeted, winds hit 50+ mph, and lots of trees came down (one right onto an SUV in Roxborough—everyone’s OK). In Jersey, the Cherry Hill Mall lost power for half an hour, and a Sunoco gas station roof collapsed under the wind. Grid warnings: the region continues to face increased risk of outages, thanks to demand crunch.

  • Sixers: Joel Embiid is out (illness + knee) vs. the Knicks. Tyrese Maxey returns to the lineup. Breakdown and hopes here.
  • Flyers: Forward Tyson Foerster needs surgery (so much for those rookie goals). Also, longtime radio announcer Tim Saunders was suspended after an explicit hot-mic slip. Here’s what happened.
  • Eagles: This weekend’s game: offense and defense both look better than Washington’s, and people are already picking Philly. Tale of the tape. Staff picks + odds.
  • Phillies: A couple of bullpen swaps—Kyle Backhus joins from the D-backs, Matt Strahm goes to Kansas City for Jonathan Bowlan.
