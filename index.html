Opening Day Wins, Legal Shake-Ups, and Pennypack Park Gets a Cleanup

Hey Philly, here’s what you missed yesterday:

⚾ Phillies: Strong Start, Off-Field Drama

  • Opening Day hype lived up to the noise: The Phillies took down the Rangers 5-3. Bohm and Schwarber both went deep, and Cristopher Sánchez put in six shutout innings with 10 Ks. Enjoy it—big vibes for the start of the season. Fans showed out as always.
  • Off the field: Alec Bohm is apparently suing his parents over allegedly misused funds. No, this won’t help his batting average.

🚧 City & Region: Legal and Political Vibes

  • Big headline out of Harrisburg: PA’s Supreme Court ruled automatic life-without-parole sentences for second-degree murder are unconstitutional. Opens the door for resentencing; expect ripple effects.
  • City Council is tabling changes to the resign-to-run law. If you’re one of the five people who obsess over Philly’s tangled politics, it’s back to “ugh, never mind” for now.
  • After a lot of trash talk (literally), the city finally started a Pennypack Park cleanup to clear out illegal dumping. About time—maybe Northeast Philly will stop smelling like...whatever that was.

🚌 Getting Around: Buses, Road Closures & More

  • The Philly PHLASH is adding stops ahead of the tourist storms expected in 2026 (think: World Cup, America’s 250th). Also, heads up: proposed new bill could let bars stay open until 4am for those events.
  • Sunday is the Love Run, so Center City streets and SEPTA are getting the usual detours and closures—plan your weekend accordingly.

🍔 Eating/Drinking & Fun Stuff

  • Looking for an actually perfect burger? Go to Huda—the word is out, but it’s still a must-hit if you care about the craft.
  • Something Vintage Rentals opened their floral-soaked Poppy Showroom if you’re planning a wedding or just want a new Instagram background.
  • Bunch of Philly restaurants are teaming up for a major event to support the immigrant community. Good excuse to eat well and do good.

🎶 Events & Other Headlines

  • Weezer and Jack Harlow both announced Philly shows. Mark your calendars now if you care—these will go fast.
  • Sad one for the animal crowd: the Zoo's beloved red panda Khumbie died at 11. RIP to a true local celeb.

Stay warm, stay loud. See you out there.
