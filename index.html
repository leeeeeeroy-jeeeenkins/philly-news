Getting Around Just Got Pricier (And Yes, Shane Gillis Is Playing the Linc)

Transit & Tolls

If you're hitting the road (or river) for Thanksgiving, get ready to cough up a little extra: Tolls are going up next year for eight Delaware River bridges. Meanwhile, a quick PSA for SEPTA riders: Schedules are changing for the holiday—so double-check before heading out. And trash pickup in most neighborhoods isn’t happening until after Thanksgiving. Don’t put yours out too early.

Big Names, Big Shows

In a move that’ll surprise everyone who thought Kevin Hart was our comedy king, Philly’s own Shane Gillis just sold out the Linc for a one-night-only show this spring. Take that, stadium size jokes. And congrats to Philly rapper Lil Uzi Vert, who signed with Jay-Z’s Roc Nation and dropped new music this week.

City Life: Closures, Delays, and Some Good News

  • Over 80% of Philly public libraries will finally open Saturdays again by January. Welcome news for anyone desperate for weekend Wi-Fi or storytime.
  • The William Way LGBT Community Center is closing its 13th Street space on Dec. 18, but programming (from health services to queer history) will keep happening at pop-ups across the city.
  • And if you want to see the Air Show exhibit at the Franklin Institute, do it soon—it’s closing in January after decades on display.

Sports Check

  • The Eagles are banged up heading into the Bears matchup; power rankings still look good, though, and Malcolm Jenkins gets his Hall of Fame nod Friday.
  • The Sixers lost Trendon Watford for at least two weeks, are missing Embiid, got bounced from the NBA Cup, and just got blown out by the Magic. It’s getting rough out there.
  • Phillies and Kyle Schwarber aren’t close on a deal yet, so don’t order your custom jersey.

Quick Hits

  • If you spot a giant crane near Columbus Boulevard next week, it’s PennDOT installing a massive pedestrian bridge (all in one night, supposedly).
  • Philly Mag launched a new daily news podcast if your eyes are tired.
  • Feeling festive? Magic Gardens is doing after-dark hours this winter.

That’s what you missed. Deep breaths, eat something good, and good luck with the travel.
