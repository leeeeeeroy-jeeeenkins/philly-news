SEPTA Fare Drama, Phillies Cling On, and Navy Ships Take Over the Delaware

Welcome to Friday. If you were too busy dodging rain or cursing at your Regional Rail app, here’s what went down in Philly yesterday.

SEPTA: Lawsuits, Fares, and Train Woes

Just when you thought SEPTA’s week couldn’t get any messier – George Bochetto filed a class-action lawsuit arguing that SEPTA’s September fare hike was illegal. (See also: more lawsuit details.) If you ride Regional Rail, yesterday probably felt like Groundhog Day: an outage suspended all lines in the morning (again), but service mostly resumed by late afternoon. SEPTA’s older Silverliner IV trains are still under inspection after cracks were discovered, desperately trying to get enough cars back in rotation. Also, a person was struck by a train on the Trenton Line, so Amtrak and some SEPTA service were disrupted even more. In short: If you get where you’re going today, it’s a win.

Phillies and Eagles: Stayin’ Alive (Sorta)

The Phillies aren’t dead yet. Kyle Schwarber smashed two homers (game recap | Fox29) and Ranger Suárez showed up, keeping the NLDS alive for at least one more day. But the margin is thin, and Game 4 is do or die (preview). As for the Eagles: injuries are stacking up (inactives report), and the Giants have some new faces in the backfield (know your enemy). Betting folks, here’s your odds and staff picks.

Navy Parade, Philly Events, and Local Moves

If you saw huge ships and military flyovers on the Delaware River, that’s the Navy’s 250th anniversary bash kicking off (how to catch the next celebration). On the ground: Philly’s Free Library is adding Saturday hours at seven more branches—that’s a win for anyone whose schedule is, well, chaotic. The 2026 Flower Show will focus on American gardening, so mark your calendars if that’s your thing.

Food & Neighborhood Bites

A few tasty highlights: Everybody Eats Cafe in Delco is getting a lot of love for going above and beyond as a Black-owned local spot. Delco’s classic foods got their own shoutout (yes, there’s more than cheesesteaks out there). Meanwhile, Philly Coffee Festival returns soon, so brace for more lines at La Colombe. Oh, and this October is Dine Latino Restaurant Week—supporting actual local eats, not chains.

Quick Hits

  • Bundle up: Frost advisory overnight; nor’easter coming this weekend (details here).
  • Proposed city rebate could help low-income renters if it passes—could be a big deal for lots of Philly tenants.
  • Local crisis center cutting rape counseling services amid budget delays—not good.
  • Flyers will honor Bernie Parent this season with a memorial jersey patch.

Stay dry out there. And maybe pack a backup SEPTA plan.
