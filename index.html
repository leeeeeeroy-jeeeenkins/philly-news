Restaurant Hype, Arts on Deck, and a Surprise Curfew

Yo — here’s what you missed in Philly yesterday.

Food & Restaurant Moves

Philly got a major culinary shoutout. Phila Lorn of Mawn made Food & Wine’s Best New Chefs of 2025, and both Mawn and Meetinghouse landed on the New York Times’ list of best US restaurants. If you want more, a top Texas sushi chef is teasing a collab-heavy project in Philly — details TBD, but expect a lot of buzz.

But Center City Restaurant Week is canceled for fall. Blame “finite resources.” So, no prix fixe stampede this season. Read up here if you need to mourn.

City Life & Policy

Kensington’s new overnight curfew means businesses there now have to close from 11 p.m to 6 a.m. Some residents and organizations are not thrilled and urge City Council to rethink.

The MLK Drive Bridge is set to finally reopen after serious repairs. Good news if you’re one of the hundred people who use it — or drive West Philly/Center City. Details here.

Tourism season is about to get an upgrade, with Visit Philly launching a $9 million campaign aimed at 2026 (America250, World Cup, all that jazz).

Arts & Events

The Fall Arts Fest returns to the Kimmel Center this Friday (9/13) with free performances and big ticket deals. Check out the lineup if you want to feel cultural on the cheap.

Opera more your speed? The Academy of Vocal Arts just rolled out their fall season — mix of comedy, high-stakes singing, and drama.

Philly Sports Report

  • Eagles: D-lineman Jalen Carter avoided suspension for last Sunday’s spit-then-shove moment (just a $57k fine). Also, rookie RB Will Shipley broke a rib vs. Dallas, so they traded for Tank Bigsby from Jacksonville. Still 2nd in NFC power rankings.
  • Phillies: Aaron Nola pitched a shutout to open the Mets series—huge for playoff seeding. Trea Turner’s hammy is on track for a postseason return.

Quick Hits

  • A suspect surrendered after vandalizing the Weitzman National Museum of American Jewish History (details).
  • Some West Philly residents can get free internet and laptops via a city pilot (check eligibility).
  • People are still arguing about if Philly police are undercounting homicides (story here) — stay tuned.

Stay tuned, stay caffeinated — and tip your servers, seriously.
