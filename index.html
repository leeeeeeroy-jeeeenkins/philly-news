Market Street Shuts Down, SEPTA Faces Bus Cuts, and Eagles Rookies Keep Balling

Yesterday in Philly – All the city’s mess, wins, and new road closures you actually need to know.

🚧 A Big Chunk of Market Street Is Closing — For Months

If Old City is on your route, brace yourself: westbound Market Street will be closed from 3rd to 4th for months thanks to roadwork, adding more headaches just as tourist season ramps up. Here’s another look at what to expect and the detours involved.

🚌 SEPTA's Deadline Drama: 32 Bus Routes At Risk

State lawmakers are still bickering about mass transit funding, and if Harrisburg doesn’t pull together by August 24, 32 city bus routes could vanish. Some Dems are pushing hard, but it’s a “wait and sweat” situation. If you rely on buses: keep your eyes on this.

🏈 Eagles Rookies Look Legit — Camp Stays Spicy

The Birds released their unofficial 2025 depth chart, and first-year CB Quinyon Mitchell is making everyone talk after another pick at camp (practice notes here). Cooper DeJean is flashing, too, though Vic Fangio says he’s not full-time at safety — yet. Details here if you need every roster battle. And yes, they even signed a long snapper. Football is Back Season is in full swing.

🌆 Quick Hits: What Else Happened

  • Serial sexual assaults: Police say the same man is wanted for at least five assaults in Center City and South Philly — extra vigilance is urged, more details forthcoming.
  • Schuylkill Yards Park opens in University City, featuring outdoor art and green space for some sweet new lunch spots.
  • New Jersey lands $2 billion "forever chemicals" settlement from DuPont and others — not Philly, but anyone drinking Jersey water should take note.
  • Spraying for West Nile virus starts this week in Germantown parks. If you see mosquito trucks, that's why.
  • A one-menu steakhouse, Medium Rare, is coming to Fishtown. Get ready for steak, salad, fries, repeat.
  • Catch up on Cristopher Sánchez throwing heat for another Phillies series win.
  • If you like oysters (or an excuse to go out in Delco), Clam Tavern declared it’s National Oyster Week. Go get weird with mollusks.

🍅 Extra Stuff to Know

  • Philly-area population is shifting: New census numbers say Hispanic and Asian communities are driving the only real growth in Philly, PA, and NJ.
  • Rittenhouse got another TV cameo — Reacher is filming around the square again if you're hunting for celeb sightings.

See you out there. Watch those detours — and, for now, keep swatting the mosquitoes.
