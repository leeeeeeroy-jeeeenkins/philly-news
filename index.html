Yesterday in Philly

Airport Upgrades, Big Losses, and a Philly Sports Kind of Day

What's Good, Philly?

Here’s what you actually missed yesterday: a pileup of city stories about everything from travel headaches to Christmas light drama, city politics stirring up old fights, a huge law enforcement tragedy out west, and, as always, the Birds. Let’s get into it.

City Life & Infrastructure

  • Philly International is about to get a $500 million makeover as the city readies for the 2026 Semiquincentennial and the World Cup crowds (PhillyVoice). Expect new terminals, better baggage handling, and – we’ll believe it when we see it – faster security.
  • Philadelphia rolled out a new logo and slogan for the airport too, because in true Philly style, we like to argue about fonts before we fix the Wi-Fi.
  • The Wanamaker Christmas Light Show is trying to raise $250k or risk some major cutbacks. Holiday magic: still not free.
  • Need something hopeful? Philabundance's annual food drive kicked off, aiming to get real food to real families before the cold sets in.

Justice, Crime & City Politics

  • There’s a lawsuit against the city after another deadly incident at Philly jails. The family of Amanda Cahill (who died inside) is pushing for real accountability.
  • Political drama: Larry Krasner called MAGA "fascist" at a town hall. Local conservative activists are mad; the national showdown over policing and democracy feels closer than ever.
  • Meanwhile, there's new talk about the National Guard in Philly if Trump wins — with locals asking how much say they’d actually have. File under: don’t ignore this.
  • A West Philly teen and a local man were both convicted for plotting terrorist attacks – one targeting the LGBTQ+ Pride parade. It’s heavy, but worth knowing.
  • Plus: A grim one—a body was found decomposing in the trunk of a stolen car in North Philly. Not a storyline anybody wanted.

Sports & Philly Energy

  • Eagles news: The latest injury reports are rolling in for Eagles-Rams (Goedert and Jalen Carter are dealing with issues). The preview includes what to watch, from AJ Brown's touches to a possible "Tush Push" sighting (yes, the rest of the league is still trying to ban it).
  • Peek at the 2025 depth chart if you like thinking way ahead or panicking early.
  • The Philly Cycling Classic is back for the first time in 10 years. More roads closed, but hey, it’s tradition.
  • And extra kudos to the Phillies—NL East champs! If you missed it, where have you been?

And Also

  • Looking ahead to '26? Philly needs YOU to give a TED Talk about democracy during the 250th anniversary year.
  • You can now time travel to Colonial Williamsburg—if you’re into that sort of nostalgia.
  • If you’re into big Philly cakes (who isn’t?), this list delivers.

Get through the week, Philly. The airport is finally getting serious for the big 2026 moment… maybe. Keep your eyes on City Hall and always double-check your trunk.
