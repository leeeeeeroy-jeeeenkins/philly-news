Year-End Shakeups and Storm Warnings: Philly in Review

Welcome to Yesterday in Philly. Before you unplug for the long weekend, here's what's up: everything from landmark closures to bad weather on the way and the city’s latest grudge match with Big Pharma. Let’s get into it.

Weather, Transit & What’s Messing with Your Plans

  • Heads up: A wintry mix (snow, sleet, ice, rain—basically all the gross stuff) is rolling into Philly Friday night into Saturday. Here's the forecast. There’s a Winter Storm Watch for much of the region. Hope you like slush.
  • New Year’s Eve planners: SEPTA is expanding late-night service Sunday. Check the schedule before you head out—and leave the car at home.
  • Doing last-minute shopping? Center City, Cherry Hill Mall, and Christiana are all open for business (and crowds).

Closings, Lawsuits & City Moves

  • Macy’s is out of the Wanamaker Building, closing out a huge retail chapter downtown. Read the nostalgia, but also: what’s Center City without that department store?
  • Philly is suing major drug makers and pharmacy benefit managers like Eli Lilly and CVS, blaming them for jacking up insulin prices. They want payback for those struggling with diabetes or wild pharmacy tabs.
  • Pennsylvania & New Jersey joined a lawsuit fighting a Trump-era ban on gender-affirming care for minors. More here if you’re following local legal fights.

Sports: Hits, Misses, and Auctions

  • Eagles: Injury-watch is real for the week 17 game vs. the Bills—Lane Johnson and Nakobe Dean both in question. Breakdown here. At least someone’s winning: a 2018 Super Bowl ring just sold for $124K. Peep the buyer if you want to feel poor.
  • Sixers: Survived a scare with Embiid’s injury, but depth problems are showing. The team signed MarJon Beauchamp on a two-way to bandage up the roster. Recap and analysis.

2025: The Highlights and Farewells

  • Philly still had its moments in 2025—yes, even this year. Check the year’s best feel-good stories if you need a mood lift.
  • If you’re eating out for the holidays, some restaurants aren’t taking a break: For them, Christmas is the Super Bowl.
  • 2025’s most defining restaurant reviews, just in case you forgot where to eat next year.

That’s it. Stay warm, stay safe, watch out for ice, and—per usual—don’t get stuck on the El after midnight.
