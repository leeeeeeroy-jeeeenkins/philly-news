Yesterday in Philly

Subject: Philly Workers Hold the Line, SEPTA Parking No Longer Free

Good morning. Here’s what went down in the city yesterday—labor drama, some burning trash, double takes for Philly sports, and a heads-up so you don’t get hit with surprise parking fees today.

Strike Watch

The DC33 city workers strike hit day six. No talks Sunday, so the trash will keep piling up for now—a couple of sites even caught fire from dumped garbage (firefighters were busy), and the union is setting up a strike fund and asking for food and water donations.

If you’re annoyed by overflowing cans, you’re not alone. No end in sight until the city and union get back to the table.

Heads Up: SEPTA Parking Fees Back

Parking at SEPTA lots? The holiday is over—paid spots return today (Monday). Expect to shell out the usual, or risk a ticket.

Sports: All-Star Shocks (and Not the Good Kind)

If you thought the Phillies’ killer first half meant a roster takeover at the All-Star Game, think again. Only Zack Wheeler and Kyle Schwarber made it, which has left plenty of people scratching their heads (more here). Still, Wheeler nearly pitched a perfect game against the Reds, and the Phillies secured another series win—ho-hum, just another day for the best record in baseball (recap, highlights).

The Sixers’ new rookie VJ Edgecombe dropped 28 points and a double-double in Summer League action—yes, it’s only July, but hope springs eternal.

Crash Course

Center City had a busy stretch of collisions involving police: one sheriff’s deputy and another driver were both hospitalized, and three more people were hurt in an unmarked police car crash—minor injuries reported.

Worth Noting

  • Heat wave on deck means keep an eye out for air quality alerts. Bad air days often land with the summer stickiness.
  • South Philly’s Real 2 Real Lounge was shuttered after a recent mass shooting.
  • UPenn scientists turned a toxic fungus linked to 'King Tut’s curse' into a cancer-fighting tool. Not your average science beat.
  • The Philadelphia Latino Arts & Film Festival wrapped up with a big closing party Sunday.

Stay tuned—if strike talks pick up, or something blows up (literally or figuratively), we’ll let you know.
