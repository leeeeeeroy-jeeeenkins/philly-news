⚡ Philly News: Starbucks Closures, Phillies Playoffs, and SEPTA's Usual Drama

Yesterday in Philly:

Big Stuff First

  • Six Starbucks locations closing — If you rely on your corner Starbucks for caffeine or Wi-Fi, brace yourself: six Philly stores are shutting down this weekend because of “changing patterns and neighborhood needs.” No official list from Starbucks yet, but count on long lines at literally every other café for a minute. (WHYY)
  • Phillies clinch NL’s No. 2 seed — Playoff tickets? Already a thing again. The Phils landed the No. 2 seed but ended their home stretch with a dud, getting blanked by the Twins. Their offense went ghost, leaving some questions hanging as the postseason kicks off this week. On the upside: intrasquad tickets expanded for those who want a taste of playoff vibes before the real thing. (More coverage)
  • Weather: More rain on deck tonight — Bring an umbrella. Showers returning this evening and overnight. Possible street flooding if we get any of that classic Philly drainage magic.

City Life & Local Moves

  • Quentin Grimes' agent torches Sixers' front office — In NBA world, restricted free agent drama is alive and well. Grimes’ agent basically called out the Sixers for stalling all summer. Read the tea here. (Not exactly what you want to hear heading into the next roster shuffle.)
  • Resy’s Top 100: Philly’s got taste — Little Fish BYOB scooped a spot on Resy’s Top 100 Restaurants list, keeping Philly firmly on the national food map. Make a res now or prepare to wait until spring.

Neighborhood Headlines

  • West Philly hit-and-run: Police have ID’d the 77-year-old grandmother killed walking back from ShopRite. Suspect still at large. Neighbors say speeding is constant in the area. (More here)
  • Carjacking & hit-and-run in Spring Garden: Video’s out of a crash following a carjacking. If you spot something off in your block, say something; it’s been a minute since we saw so many bold moves in broad daylight.

Quick Hits

  • Eagles Draft Gaze: Already scouting for 2026? Never too early for Birds fans. Some possible next-gen targets for your way-too-early mock drafts.
  • SEPTA Watch: Nothing caught on fire, and trains ran (mostly) on time. We’ll call it a win.
  • Events: Light up the city next month at the Lights Lantern Fest. It’s like a party and your Instagram grid had a baby.

That’s your day. Go birds, tip your barista (while you can), and, as always, watch those puddles.
