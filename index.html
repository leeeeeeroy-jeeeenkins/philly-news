Yesterday in Philly

Storms, Streets & Bullpen Drama

Good morning. Here’s what mattered in Philly yesterday while you were dodging puddles, tracking baseball trades, and maybe wondering if SEPTA's ever going to get it together.

Storms and Streets

  • Weather got wild: Heavy storms brought flash floods, downed trees, and a dramatic cool-down (NBC10, Fox29). NJ even went into a state of emergency. Expect some lingering mess on your block.
  • Kelly Drive closing at night in August: Just a heads up for anyone who actually drives: overnight shutdowns for resurfacing start next week (PhillyVoice).

SEPTA’s Budget Cliff Looms

  • SEPTA still in crisis mode: Lawmakers and riders are pounding the drum: unless Harrisburg does something, we’re looking at major service cuts (6ABC, NBC10). Nothing solved yet.
  • Special prosecutor could stick around: Pa. AG Dave Sunday says keep the current SEPTA prosecutor on to tackle crime and quality-of-life stuff (NBC10).

City and State Politics

  • Ban the phones? Pennsylvania lawmakers are talking about a statewide ban on cell phones in schools (Philly Mag). Could be a big shift for students and teachers—if it happens.
  • School funding in limbo: Billions in aid to Pa. schools are held up thanks to the budget impasse in Harrisburg (NBC10).

Sports & Food (Because It’s Philly)

  • Phillies’ big moves: GM Dave Dombrowski pulled off a deadline day bullpen overhaul—including flamethrower Jhoan Duran and outfielder Harrison Bader (PhillyVoice). The pen could finally be elite down the stretch (more).
  • Royal Tavern’s hot dog contest: PhillyMag recapped the culinary carnage and crowned some wiener winners (Philly Mag).
  • Stephen Starr’s next move: His new Italian spot, Borromini, opens in Rittenhouse in August (PhillyVoice).

Other Stuff Worth Your Time

  • West Philly Rec Center shooting: Five people, including kids, were injured at Christy Rec Center. Police released surveillance footage of suspects (PhillyVoice, PHL17) and the community’s now demanding more from city leaders (6ABC).
  • Mural Arts’ latest: Beautiful new mural in Bella Vista honors Underground Railroad hero William Still (PhillyVoice).
  • Need help with your PECO bill? The utility is offering $500 grants for folks who qualify (Fox29).

Stay dry. See you tomorrow.
