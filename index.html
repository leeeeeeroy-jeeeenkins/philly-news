Heat, Harper and Philly’s All-Time Podcasts

Tuesday, July 29

Weather & Heat Warnings

First up: It’s brutal outside. A Code Orange Air Quality Alert is in effect for today. It’s one of those days where the air feels like soup and every sidewalk is melting. Cooling centers, pools, and spray grounds are open—check the link for locations if your AC is struggling. Oh, and pro tip: transit and paving crews are also fending off the heat, so pack extra patience for any delays.

City Life & Headlines

  • Dog care outrage: A Warminster kennel (RC Dog Care) has temporarily closed after two dogs died—families are looking for answers and there’s now an investigation. If you’re boarding pets, keep an eye on this situation.
  • Open primaries push: Independent voters are pushing for an open primary system in PA. If the petition and court fights work, voters outside the parties could pick who runs in November. Major shakeup if it happens, so stay tuned.
  • SEPTA & Roadwork: Lane closures are coming to South Philly (resurfacing), and while there aren’t any new apocalyptic SEPTA rumors, this heat is known to slow down transit.

Philly Sports, Extra Spicy Edition

  • Bryce Harper v. The Man: Phillies' Bryce Harper is reportedly not thrilled about the MLB floating a salary cap—he told Commissioner Manfred to "get the f--- out" of the clubhouse in a now-viral exchange. Labor talks might get…interesting.
  • Eagles camp: Rookie corner Quinyon Mitchell is turning heads at training camp—yes, it’s July, but it’s already feeling like his jersey is going to fly off shelves.
  • Sixers talk: Some roster churn (Ricky Council IV waived) and no, Marcus Morris won’t be back (he was arrested on fraud charges in Florida).

What’s Actually Fun This Week

  • Best of Philly food drop: Philly Mag rounded up the top 10 must-try dishes right now. Use this as your dinner inspiration (or bragging rights if you’ve already eaten them).
  • TIME names two Philly podcasts among the 100 best of all time. Yes, Fresh Air made it, and we have even more audio to humblebrag about.
  • Taco Bell but taller: A two-story location opens next week on Chestnut Street. Not the boozy Cantina you maybe hoped for (more fast than fancy), but still—late-night bean burritos, now above street level.
  • Oddities & Curiosities Expo: Want taxidermy, skulls, and other weird stuff? This expo’s headed to town this week for those who like their shopping a little macabre.
  • More to do: Events roundup here—make plans, or just get ideas for escaping the AC doldrums.

That’s it for today. Hydrate, avoid starting Twitter beef with Rob Manfred, and maybe try one of those new dishes tonight.
