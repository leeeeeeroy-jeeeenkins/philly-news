City Council, Charlie Kirk, and Playoff Math

Hey Philly, here’s what you actually missed yesterday:

City Hall & Neighborhood Moves

City Council is sticking by the business curfew that started in Kensington and is now rolling out to Germantown and Fairhill. The plan: 24-hour spots (except pharmacies and gas) will have to shut down from midnight to 6 a.m. Council insists it’s about curbing crime, but plenty of folks aren’t thrilled about losing their late-night bodegas to policy instead of competition.

On the classroom front, the Board of Ed approved a new teachers’ union contract—details are still slim, but it’s a move toward more stability ahead of the year.

And for those in Port Richmond: the Richmond Library finally reopened after a long $2.5M reno. Grab your library card and go see some new carpet.

Big Headlines

The aftermath of Charlie Kirk’s assassination is still a top story. The governor ordered flags at half-staff, and the FBI has released photos of a person of interest, offering a $100K reward. Meanwhile, political drama’s brewing in Harrisburg, with state lawmakers talking security upgrades for politicians after a scary string of incidents.

Out in Jersey, another tragic headline: a road rage shooting near Stockton University led to a shelter-in-place, but the suspect is now in custody and the campus is back to normal.

The saga of Bob Menendez isn’t over, but his wife’s likely out of the picture for a while: she was sentenced to 4.5 years for her role in the bribery scheme.

Sports Math & Local Legends

Phillies playoff number crunching is officially on. The next two weeks will decide their path, opponents, and if you’re allowed to daydream about Red October.

The Eagles are watching the Chiefs’ injury list—there’s hope for a banged-up secondary. And for the truly committed, there’s even a Week 2 rooting guide if you want to keep other NFC teams at bay.

If you somehow forgot about it, Mitchell & Ness has restocked the Princess Diana Eagles jacket. Uptown chic meets tailgate energy.

Odds & Ends

  • New fancy furniture showroom opened in Bella Vista for anyone in the market for a conversation piece—or a chair that costs more than your rent.
  • Maze Days are coming to Dilworth Park. Hay maze, cider, general wholesome October stuff.
  • Planning your fall weekends? The Carnivorous Plant Show will be at Haverford College Oct. 11, because Philly people love a weird little plant moment.

That’s it. Back tomorrow with another round.
