Yesterday in Philly

Subject: How’s Your Commute? SEPTA’s on Fire (Literally)

Philly in 5 Minutes: Injuries, Delays, and a Home Collapse

SEPTA drama, a Phillies nightmare, and a rough day for some Brewerytown neighbors. Let’s get into it:

TRANSPORTATION

SEPTA trouble (again): After a pair of small fires on regional rail trains, SEPTA is pulling some train cars out of service temporarily. Don’t panic—but do expect more delays and crowded commutes, especially if you’re on the older Silverliner V cars. If you rely on SEPTA daily, check alerts in the AM and maybe add an extra 10-15 minutes to your travel time. Meanwhile, union heads are warning about looming layoffs and deeper cuts in 2025. Not like things were running all that smoothly to begin with.

PHILLIES: BAD NEWS BEARS

Zack Wheeler is suddenly out with a blood clot near his shoulder (read more). No timetable for a return—it’s a big blow for the rotation. The team is "processing the jarring news" (NBC10). On top of that, closer Jhoan Duran took a hard grounder to the foot and left the game in a cart. X-rays were negative, but geez. Just in time for key games down the stretch.

EAGLES: PRESEASON HYPE (AND DOUBT)

The Birds lost to the Browns in preseason, but there were some moments: rookie Andrew Mukuba snagged a pick-six and linebacker Jihaad Campbell made a statement with a sack (his take). On the downside, the offensive line depth is looking shaky. Quarterbacks struggled and the pass rush was hot and cold. Training camp is never boring, at least. More stock up/down here.

CITY LIFE + WEATHER

Brewerytown building collapse: A home that was already flagged as dangerous collapsed yesterday in Brewerytown. Neighbors were evacuated; no injuries, but if you notice a janky-looking house on your block, maybe give L&I a call.

Weather: We’re heading into a hot, muggy weekend—highs in the 90s, humid as hell. Sunscreen, water, you know the drill.

QUICK HITS

  • PA voter ID proposal: May not do much for election security, experts say (PhillyVoice).
  • Funky ticks: A new invasive tick is in the region—watch out for bites and weird symptoms (NBC10).
  • Manayunk’s Dog Day of Summer is back—if you need some pure vibes (6ABC).

That’s it. Drink water, check your train alerts, and say a prayer for Wheeler’s shoulder.
