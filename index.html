Missed the headlines? Here’s what actually mattered in the city yesterday—transit drama, shutdown headaches, sports moves, and a touch of Taylor-mania. Let’s get you caught up.

🚊 SEPTA Shrugs Off NTSB Fire Warning

If you ride Regional Rail, heads up: The feds want SEPTA to pull over half its trains (those familiar Silverliner IVs) due to electrical fire risks—there’ve been at least 5 fires lately. SEPTA says nope, not happening yet, because, well, there’s no backup plan. So if your commute smells a little toasty, that’s probably why.

🚨 Government Shutdown = Weird Philly Closures

Day one of the shutdown brought the usual confusion: Liberty Bell is closed, but most national parks are technically “open,” just don’t expect restrooms or rangers. TSA lines at the airport might get longer, and a bunch of government offices are running skeleton crews. If you rely on SNAP benefits, Pennsylvania has some protection for now, but don’t get too comfy—state agencies are still sweating delayed payments.
