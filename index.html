Yesterday in Philly

This City Never Bores: 911 Woes, Union Showdowns, & Trolley Trouble

Weather check: Still muggy, with storms threatening all weekend. You know the drill—hydrate, and keep an umbrella nearby. More here.

Big Systems, Big Headaches

  • PA 911 Meltdown: If you tried to call 911 yesterday and got a weird error or nothing at all, you weren’t alone—Pennsylvania’s 911 system went down statewide for a chunk of Friday. It’s back now, but, yeah, feels good to live in a city where even emergency calls are on shaky ground. Details here and here. Officials are still trying to figure out why it failed (and how not to let it happen again).
  • Trolley Tunnel Shut-Down: SEPTA’s annual "Trolley Tunnel Blitz" kicks off next week—tunnels close for a month starting July 12. If you ride the trolley downtown, start planning on walking, bus detours, or just giving up and working from home. All the reroute info here.

City Workers Union Drama

  • DC 33 Contract Not a Done Deal: The city’s largest municipal workers union starts voting today on a new contract. Last week’s handshake between union leaders and the mayor doesn’t mean the 9,500 city workers are satisfied yet—some members are ready to strike if the deal isn't sweetened. Trash collection, rec centers, and city services are on notice. What to expect. Background here.

Public Safety, Philly-Style

  • Car Break-Ins = Stolen Guns: Philly (and pretty much everywhere else) is seeing a jump in gun thefts from parked cars. Guns left in vehicles are now a top source of crime guns, new report says. Seriously, if you drive with a gun, stop leaving it in your damn glovebox. Here’s the data.
  • Deed Fraud Sentencing: A Philly man got 6 years in federal prison for stealing houses through wire fraud—literally forging paperwork to swipe people’s homes. Reminder: keep an eye on your deed. How he did it.

Weekend & Future Plans

  • The Roots at Dilworth: Countdown to the 2026 MLB All-Star Game starts Thursday with a free open-air Roots concert at Dilworth Park. Yes, it’s early, but free Roots is free Roots. Concert details here.
  • Live Aid Revisited: 40 years ago this week, Philly hosted one of the wildest crowds in history at the Live Aid concert—90,000 in-person, nearly half the world’s population watching on TV. Nostalgic numbers here.
  • And Yes, Greased Pole Season: Penn’s Landing, Saturday: Watch brave (or bonkers) people run across a greased pole over the Delaware. Expect splashes and a lot of crowd energy. Event info.

If you need an excuse to get out: It’s Fry-Day, and some spots are offering free or discounted fries. Deal rundown.

That’s your quick catch-up. See you out there—if you can get a trolley.
