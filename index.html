Major hat tricks, superfans, and Center City changes

Let’s talk penny auctions, $60 World Cup tickets, the Phillies’ next moves, and why you might see a Rally House where your Rite Aid used to be.

City Life & Business

The last Philly pennies are big money: Turns out, people really love pennies. Nearly 700 of the last-ever coins pressed at the Philly and Denver Mints just pulled in over $16 million at auction—seriously. So maybe it’s time to empty those coin jars?

Center City gets more team gear: Sports retail chain Rally House is eyeing its first Center City location, filling the vacant Rite Aid at 2000 Chestnut. If you ride for the Birds (or any Philly squad), you’ll soon have another spot to rep your colors. Details here.

Sports—All Eyes on the Birds & Phils

Eagles primed to clinch: The odds-makers love us again: Eagles favored to lock up the NFC East against Washington this weekend. Plus, is the defense really looking Super Bowl-worthy again? Catch the analysis here.

Phillies shuffling their squad: Big interviews dropped with Dave Dombrowski, who says "catcher is the main focus" (yes, Realmuto is still recovering). Outfield-wise: expect Justin Crawford to get a serious shot, per this update.

2026 Watch

World Cup drama turns (slightly) fan-friendly: After backlash over sky-high prices, FIFA says real 'superfans' can get $60 World Cup tickets. Superfans = people who’ve been verified by FIFA as the real deal—good luck getting through that process.

Transit & Safety

Peace patrol on SEPTA: Anti-violence group Philly Truce is teaming up with SEPTA to put "peace patrols" on trains—aiming for fewer problems (and more eyes/security) during your commute. The info here.
