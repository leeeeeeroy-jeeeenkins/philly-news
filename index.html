Open Streets and Closed Icons: Philly’s Week in Motion

Welcome to Yesterday in Philly. Here’s what happened while you were out dodging puddles and SEPTA woes. (Or maybe just living your life. Good for you.)

City Life + Infrastructure

Center City is getting some breathing room: Open Streets returns to Rittenhouse this fall, shutting down Walnut from 8th to 22nd on six Sundays in September and October. Expect plenty of joggers, bikes, strollers, and folks looking for photo ops. If you’ve got plans near Rittenhouse on those dates, plan ahead—or just walk, for once.

SEPTA can’t catch a break: The feds have kicked off an inquiry into how SEPTA is storing its retired electric buses (story), because if something can be a mess, Philly transit will find a way.

Closings, Openings, and Philly Flavor

End of an era: Giordano’s in the Italian Market is officially calling it quits. After generations, the owner is saying goodbye, citing “the right time to retire.” Stop by for a last stash of produce and to say thanks for all the memories (and baskets of peppers).

Looking for something new? Amada in Old City just reopened with a slick new design after a month-long renovation. If Spanish tapas are your thing, Jose Garces wants you back.

Best of Philly Soirée eats were revealed—here’s where (and what) to expect to snack on if you scored tickets: Food preview here. No word on how many bites is too many.

In sports bars news, Watch Party PHL just raised nearly $90,000 to help open what might be Philly’s first proper women's sports bar. Keep an eye out for the opening date.

Transit & Getting Around

Flights to Europe just got a little easier: American Airlines is adding nonstop service from PHL to Budapest and Prague (details). If you need an excuse for a fall getaway, this is it.

Running over the Ben Franklin Bridge returns November 2. Register if you want bragging rights (and a few thousand steps to justify all that cheesesteak).

Public Safety & Important Local News

Grays Ferry mass shooting: An arrest was made in Sunday’s tragic shooting (coverage). Police say more suspects could be coming. Elsewhere, Philly police faced a rash of dangerous traffic stops; two officers were injured in separate incidents (NBC10).

State troopers shot outside Philly: In Susquehanna County, two state troopers were recovering—and a gunman and one other person are dead after a violent Thursday (Fox29).

ICE detention center under fire: After a detainee died at the Moshannon Valley ICE facility, activists are again calling for its closure (read more).

Quick Hits

  • Legally, it’s Columbus Day (again): A PA court says Philly can’t swap in Indigenous Peoples’ Day by executive order (details).
  • South Philly’s Riverview Plaza movie theater will reopen in 2026 with a new IMAX screen. That’s one big reason to leave the couch.
  • Schwarber for MVP? The Phillies slugger is actually in the conversation (breakdown).

That’s yesterday. See you tomorrow for more of what matters in Philly—minus the fluff and cable news drama.
