SEPTA Cuts Hit—and Philly Scrambles to Cope

Good morning. Here’s what actually mattered in Philly yesterday:

Transit Chaos as SEPTA Cuts Land

SEPTA service cuts kicked in right as kids returned to school. Naturally, that meant late buses, packed cars, missed classes, and a bunch of frazzled parents (again). City leaders are urging Harrisburg to cough up some cash and fix this mess, but for now? If you use SEPTA at rush hour, build in extra time—or just bring patience and snacks. Mayor Parker and City Council are not letting up on the statehouse about the budget, but no promises things improve soon.

Schools: New Deal, Old Problems

The Philadelphia teachers union and the district hit a tentative contract agreement right before classes started—so, no strike. Still, the superintendent is talking staffing shortages, soft bans on cell phones, and a renewed focus on school safety. Oh, and some students managed to bring guns and drugs to class on day one. Just in case you thought things might be boring.

City Hall & Culture: Big Moves

After more than 40 years, Jane Golden is stepping down from Mural Arts. She basically painted half the city (and made sure the rest was painted too). Maybe you’ll spot her mural somewhere today and feel deep, wistful feelings.

Neighborhood Watch

  • A massive 5-alarm warehouse fire in Tacony sent smoke sky-high. No word yet on the cause, but avoid the area if you can.
  • A rowhome in North Philly partially collapsed, trapping an elderly woman and her son inside—they got out okay.
  • Spray-painted graffiti hit the Museum of American Jewish History.

Openings, Eats, (and a Netflix Theme Park?)

Netflix is launching its first-ever "Netflix House"—picture a fan theme park + restaurant + merch bonanza—at KOP Mall, opening Nov. 12. Could be cool, could be chaos.

On the actual restaurant front, Malgudi Cafe is getting buzz for spicy South Indian food that doesn’t skimp on heat. For when you want to sweat for reasons besides SEPTA delays.

Sports: Eagles Cut, Phillies Shine

  • Eagles chopped down to 53 players; see the full tracker and projections here. Also: backup QB drama, O-line trades, and the usual Howie Roseman tinkering.
  • Former GM Jim Murray died at 87—a big loss for Eagles history and Ronald McDonald House. More here.
  • Meanwhile, Phillies starter Ranger Suárez is your NL Player of the Week.

Random Stuff

  • Keep your eyes up tonight—NASA rockets may be visible over Philly just seconds after launch from Virginia.
  • ‘Pink salt trick’ for weight loss? Total nonsense, say actual dietitians.
  • American Idol virtual auditions coming up Sept. 4 if you need a side hustle or a viral moment.

Go easy—it’s only Tuesday. See you tomorrow.
