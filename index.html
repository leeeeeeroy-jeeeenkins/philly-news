Winter Hits, Philly Sits: Big Snow, Bigger Disruptions

Yesterday wasn’t subtle: it dumped, it froze, and it’s not over yet. If you spent Sunday indoors, here’s the fallout you’ll notice heading into the week.

Weather (a.k.a. Everything Right Now)

Philly just got walloped by its biggest snowstorm in five years, with most of the city hitting 6 to 10 inches, and surrounding 'burbs digging out even more. Here’s a breakdown of snow totals by neighborhood. And with cold temps sticking around, expect those slushy sidewalks and iced-over windshields to be a whole vibe for the next few days.

Yes, there’s a snow emergency still in effect (details). Don’t forget: shovel your sidewalk or risk $300 fines. Keep an eye on slippery streets — PennDOT reduced speed limits Sunday; check before driving this morning.

Power’s been spotty for some (outage resources here), and if you need to report an issue, there’s help. If you’re looking for school or work updates: here’s the running list of closures and delays, and who’s already called off for Monday.

Transit? Not great. SEPTA suspended all bus and Regional Rail service Sunday. As of this morning, expect delays during recovery and NJ Transit is still getting back up to speed. Plan for extra time (and cold platforms).

City & Legal Updates

Between shoveling, some headlines snuck through: the State Supreme Court suspended a Philly judge for partisan Facebook posts (for real, judges, you have to do better on social), and the court also blocked Chester’s attempt to sell its water authority without board approval. Score one for local control.

Sports Corner

The Sixers are sitting both Embiid and Paul George tonight against the Hornets, which means if you've got tickets, maybe bring snacks. Tiny bright spot: Charles Bassey's back on a 10-day contract.

The Eagles are still hunting for an offensive coordinator. New rumors every day—don’t lose sleep yet.

And, if you’re already plotting your next Phillies heartbreak: rumors are swirling about Nick Castellanos hitting the trade block.

The Fun Side

Not everything is a snow slog: got kids (or nieces, or neighbor’s kids you want to impress)? Bluey’s making a visit to King of Prussia soon.

And if you’re an art fan, you’ve got time to check out Arleen Olshan’s exhibit at iMPeRFeCT Gallery (on view through Feb. 21).

Stay warm, shovel your sidewalk, and watch SEPTA’s alerts. We’ll see what tomorrow unburies.
