🌧️ Nor’easter Incoming, Airport Chaos, Eagles Blues

Yesterday in Philly | Sunday, October 12

Weather: Brace for a Classic Nor’easter

If you’ve got plans Sunday or Monday, better check that sky—Philly is in the crosshairs for heavy rain and wind. Flooding is possible, so don’t play hero with those road puddles. The AccuWeather folks say this one could linger into Monday. By midday Saturday, the FAA halted flights at PHL for a bit due to low visibility. If you’re flying, double-check your status or, honestly, just reschedule.

Eagles: Searching for Vibes (& Victories)

Two straight Ls for the Birds and suddenly it’s not all brotherly love in Philly sports talk. Between messy game plans and injuries, Nick Sirianni and Jalen Hurts have some diagnosing to do. If you’re trying to look ahead, scouts are already peeking at 2026 prospects – but that feels a little too soon, even for us.

Meanwhile, Hurts made a surprise stop at the Nike ‘World of Flight’ store opening in Center City, proving he’s still a fan favorite—even if the offense is lagging.

SEPTA & City Stuff

  • Strike Cost: The city finally tallied up how much the DC33 strike cost us this summer. Spoiler: it wasn’t cheap (think millions, not thousands).
  • Airport Eyesore: Crews found hundreds of dumped tires near Northeast Philly Airport. City’s investigating, but if you spot an illegal dump, report it. It adds up.

Crime & Neighborhood Headlines

  • Awful scene near Pennypack Park: two men killed execution-style, hands zip-tied. Cops are searching for leads.
  • Shootings and violence kept police busy, but unless it’s messing with your block or commute, we’re moving on for this round.

Sixers & Sports Updates

  • Sixers preseason: Tyrese Maxey’s in midseason form already after a free-and-easy scoring night, and Kennedy Chandler is finally getting real run.
  • Phillies: Now officially in offseason mode with big questions for Dombrowski (read the postmortem here).

Odds & Ends

  • Penn’s new urban wildlife project is putting camera traps up all over the city. Apparently, you have more raccoon neighbors than you think.
  • Lincoln High’s football suspension after that brawl has parents and players heated; advocates are pressing the district to reconsider.

That’s the quick & dirty. Stay dry, be patient if you’re near the airport, and keep an eye out for wild (literal) city life.
