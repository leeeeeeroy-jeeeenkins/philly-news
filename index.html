Yesterday in Philly: Strike Fizzles, Big Moves at Wanamaker, and Pool Drama

City Stuff: Trash, Pools, and The Aftermath

The DC 33 city workers' strike is technically over after a tentative contract deal—but don’t expect instant clean streets. Union membership still has to vote, and even after that, city officials say it’ll take a minute for regular trash pickup, recycling, and other city services to return to normal. Pools, though? They’re hustling to open all of them for the rest of summer, which is more welcome than ever after this week’s swampy weather. (Details)

Wanamaker’s New Era?
The Wanamaker Building is trying to stop its slow fade into irrelevance: new plans would convert it into over 600 apartments, add a rooftop pool, and bring some retail back to the lower floors. No timeline yet, but if the Macy’s organ recitals get replaced by pool parties, you heard it here first.

Legal Weed Closer in PA?
A new marijuana legalization bill just hit the PA Senate, pitched as 'smart, fair, and realistic.' Don’t fire up quite yet, but move this closer to actual law than we’ve seen in a bit. Across the river, Delaware’s full rec sales start next month.

World Cafe Live Workers Unionize
Front-of-house staff at World Cafe Live, the beloved venue, voted to unionize, citing unfair labor practices. Management says they look forward to working with the union. Get ready for possible ripple effects across other local venues.

Federal and State Headlines

  • Pennsylvania scored $28 million in opioid settlements from eight manufacturers—more funds for treatment and prevention on the way.
  • Jefferson Health landed a $28 million donation from Sidney and Caroline Kimmel for ER upgrades and research.

Tough Stories

  • Philadelphia woman files a sexual assault lawsuit against a former Obama/DNC operative, with serious allegations drawing national attention.
  • The man behind the 2019 shootout that wounded six Philly officers (Maurice Hill) was sentenced to at least 120 years.
  • Trash dumping is still a mess in neighborhoods hit worst by the strike. (Neighbors fed up)

Quick Hits

  • Live Aid nostalgia: WXPN will rebroadcast the legendary concert this weekend if you need a blast from the past. (Michael Jackson was a notable absence, FYI.)
  • Wokworks is bringing a AI-powered wok robot to Bryn Mawr. We live in the future.
  • Mayfair Diner just hit the market. Northeast Philly folks, get your retro booths while you can.
  • Pool’s open—but watch your hand near fireworks. A North Philly girl was seriously hurt inside her home playing with them.

That’s it for now. Watch out for someone pitching a tent in the Wanamaker lobby, and look for those trash trucks—they’ll get there eventually.
