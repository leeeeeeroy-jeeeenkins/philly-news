SEPTA Cuts, Christmas Comes Early, & City Workers Cash In

Welcome to Yesterday in Philly, where we skip the fluff and hand you what you actually need to know. Whether you spent Friday dodging storms, dreaming of air conditioning, or just trying to keep up with the city’s relentless chaos—here’s what happened.

🚨 SEPTA Service Cuts Just Got Real

If you rely on SEPTA, brace yourself: notices are up across the system warning of major service cuts set to hit next month. (Details here too.) Riders and city officials are bracing for what’s being called “catastrophic impacts,” especially for daily commuters. If SEPTA's funding hole doesn’t get filled at the state level soon, expect longer waits, crowded trains, and real pain for anyone who doesn’t drive.

🎄 Wanamaker Light Show & Dickens Village ARE COMING BACK

Christmas in July? Pretty much: Macy’s closing won’t stop two Philly holiday traditions. The Wanamaker Building’s light show and Dickens Village will return this holiday season, thanks to new management stepping in and some quick work by city leaders. Get your nostalgia (and kids) ready.

💰 City Workers’ New Deal

District Council 47—the biggest union for Philly’s white-collar city employees—just ratified a new three-year contract. It means higher pay, a bit more job security, and…not much chance you’ll find City Hall suddenly moving any faster, let’s be honest. Still, a win for the folks keeping Philly’s bureaucratic gears turning.

🌩️ Heat, Storms, and Commute Chaos

Friday brought some of the worst heat and storms of the summer, with heavy rain knocking out power and tying up traffic across the region. Recap and impact here. Check before you drive today—aftermath may linger.
