Yesterday in Philly: Football Shuffles, SEPTA Surprises, and a New Era in Frankford

City Moves & Hometown Headlines

Frankford High School is officially reopening for the school year after a messy, expensive two-year shutdown for asbestos repairs (that $30 million budget wasn’t just for show). That’s a big deal for families counting on neighborhood schools to actually open on time. Some Philly students are even getting a bonus this month: kids get in free at the Museum of the American Revolution over Labor Day Weekend.

On the weird/only-in-Philly front, Fishtown somehow now has a J.D. Vance mural. Because of course it does.

Transit: SEPTA’s at a Crossroads (Again)

SEPTA riders, listen up: protestors rallied outside State Senator Joe Picozzi’s office demanding lawmakers save SEPTA from budget cuts and fare hikes. SEPTA’s already sent “ambassadors” into stations to prep people for possible service reductions—never a good sign for your commute.

Politics: Shapiro Gets a Challenger

State Treasurer Stacy Garrity is officially running for governor, setting up a 2026 showdown with Josh Shapiro. Don’t expect the fight for Harrisburg to stay friendly for long.

Sports Desk: All Eyes on Practice & the Pennant

Football: Eagles’ training camp brought a cornerback shakeup as the team tries to shore up its defense. Rookie Cooper DeJean is now getting outside cornerback reps and Jahan Dotson (still weird in green) is apparently catching everything. The preseason’s heating up—even if the QBs aren’t.

Baseball: The Phillies are holding the NL East lead, but it’s overshadowed by Zack Wheeler’s surgery for a blood clot. There’s still no timeline for his return—and that rotation is about to get a real test. They’re shuffling the bullpen (adios Max Lazar, hello Nolan Hoffman: recap here) while hoping for good news before October.

Openings & Eats

Hungry? AMA just opened, and folks are saying it’s a new high for Mexican food in Philly. Worth a reservation before everyone else catches on.

Quick Hits

  • Temple’s new Women and Families Hospital is open and aiming to lower Philly’s ugly maternal health disparities.
  • Evening lane closures on I-95 are back (and not letting up soon).
  • Beach plans? Hurricane Erin means dangerous surf all week—don’t even think about swimming in Wildwood right now.
