Yesterday in Philly

Labor Day traffic, SEPTA whiplash, and some Philly flexes

Hey. Here’s what you actually need to know from yesterday across Philly before you zone out for the long weekend (or get stuck on the Ben Franklin Bridge).

🚍 SEPTA service cuts? On pause.

After days of drama, a judge slapped a temporary injunction on SEPTA’s latest round of service cuts. Translation: all those routes you thought you’d lose for back-to-school—hold tight, they’re sticking around, at least for now. The ruling comes as Philly also committed extra cash to restore bus lines around school commutes, so if you or your kids rely on SEPTA, double check your route before Tuesday, but things aren't as dire as they looked last week.

🏫 Teachers union ratifies new contract

Philly public schools will open Tuesday without big labor drama: The teachers’ union just approved a new three-year deal with raises and some new health perks. That should mean fewer gnarly disruptions in schools this year (fingers crossed).

🚧 Holiday travel: The great Jersey Shore exodus begins

If you didn’t leave already, plan for traffic. Early rush jammed the Ben Franklin Bridge as Jersey Shore-bound folks tried to get out of dodge. PennDOT and NJDOT are begging you to watch for kids and drive like a human as school restarts next week. Want weekend ideas that don’t involve hours on the Expressway? Check this roundup of Labor Day events and weather.

🍂 Weather: Seriously pleasant for August

What a weird summer. After those scary-hot June days, August is one of the coolest in years. Expect dry, mild weather through the holiday—unheard of, but enjoy it.

🎨, 🍔, ⚾, and more to do & know

  • The Rittenhouse Square Fine Art Show returns Sept. 19–21. Mark your calendar.
  • Wonder’s third food hall opens in University City next week if you crave something new by Penn.
  • Abbott Elementary and Phillies world collided: Quinta Brunson filmed at the game where Kyle Schwarber went deep four times. Hero plane crash rescuer threw the first pitch.
  • Pumpkin Spice is back. Here’s where to get your fix already because apparently that’s how time works.
  • A 60-foot-tall sculpture just lit up on the Delaware in Northern Liberties.

TL;DR: Students won’t lose their buses, teachers got a deal, traffic’s nasty, weather’s glorious, and there’s a whole lot happening if you’re in town. Don’t say nobody warned you.
