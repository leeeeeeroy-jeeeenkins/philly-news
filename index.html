Subject: Construction Hell, SEPTA Squeeze, Eagles Hype

Yesterday in Philly

Vine Street is about to be a mess, SEPTA cuts are feeling too real, and the Eagles have Philly buzzing. Here’s what you missed around town:

Transit & Getting Around

Commute: prepare for chaos. Major construction is set to start on the Vine Street Corridor in Center City. Expect closures, lingering traffic, and detours in the coming weeks as PennDOT tries to 'improve safety.' If you drive or bike through the area, maybe pad that ETA.

Meanwhile, SEPTA is sounding the alarm on pending service cuts unless Harrisburg comes through with funding. Staffers will be at stations to help riders when (not if) the schedule gets brutal. Some Montgomery County lawmakers are trying to get cash moving, but for now, brace yourself.

One transit bright spot: Amtrak is rolling out new, faster Acela trains with more seats, USB chargers, and those fancy glass doors if you need to escape the city on a semi-bougie ride.

Eagles Preseason: Let the Speculation Begin

The Birds beat the Bengals 34-27 in the preseason opener, and somehow everyone is already writing their Week 1 roster projections. Standouts like Johnny Wilson and Darius Cooper are suddenly part of the wideout conversation (link), the QB room is as hot as ever (link), and the linemen absolutely flattened the Bengals' starters (link). It’s preseason mania, but it feels pretty good so far. Even Bud Light is in on the action with a new Eagles-themed can.

Local Eats & Openings

The much-loved Amada in Old City reopened after a top-to-bottom facelift (link). Get your tapas fix with a new look. Across the river, NJ’s Tacoria is opening up its Mexican street food spot in Cherry Hill on Saturday (link). Still hungry? Here’s an updated cheat sheet of Philly’s best new restaurants this month.

Stuff You’ll Be Talking About

  • A bomb threat shut down the infamous Franconi’s Pizza in Wildwood — no more late-night slices (for now).
  • The Bucks County alligator was finally caught and is off to a new (warmer) home.
  • Penn quietly closed its law school’s equal opportunity office and hit pause on a key scholarship — incoming law students take note.
  • PECO is warning (again) about scam calls offering to 'review' your bill. Don’t give out your info.
