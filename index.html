SEPTA Scrambles, Eagles Go Retro, Bus Chaos Eases

Good morning, Philly. If you blinked yesterday, here’s what actually happened in this city that refuses to take a breather.

Transit Drama (and Some Relief)

After days of outcry (and a lawsuit from riders), the city is stepping in to restore some SEPTA bus routes next week—specifically the ones students use most. Mayor Parker is pushing city funds to make it happen while SEPTA figures out its budget mess. If you’ve got kids or commute via bus, check for updates—and, if you’re a senior, Uber’s teamed up with nonprofits to offer free rides for now. SEPTA still faces a lawsuit from fed-up riders, and any long-term solution is stalled thanks to Harrisburg’s usual gridlock.

Schools: Teachers & Safety

Some rare good news: The Philly teachers union voted YES on a new contract, so no strikes as back-to-school looms. Elsewhere, a school safety officer is recovering at home after surviving being shot 11 times while responding to a road rage incident. Philly resilience at its finest.

Eagles Buzz—Uniforms, Moves & Dallas Fire Sale

Let’s talk Birds: The Eagles will be rocking Kelly Green throwbacks for three divisional games this year (Cowboys, Giants, Commanders). Swag prediction: high. Major NFL shocker: the Cowboys just traded Micah Parsons to the Packers, which…well, good luck with that, Dallas. The Eagles shuffled the roster this week, picked up receiver Britain Covey (link), brought back safety Marcus Epps (link), and remain favorites over Dallas for opening night (line here).

Health, Safety, & Random Good Stuff

  • City is installing new speed cameras on Broad Street (going live mid-September), so maybe tap the brakes.
  • Penn State developed a breath test for diabetes—no needles, rapid results.
  • Free overdose-reversal meds are now available outside 61 Philly firehouses as city activists protest persisting approaches to the opioid crisis.
  • Bomb threats and active shooter hoaxes at Villanova and other schools tied to a known swatting group. FBI is on it.

Bits & Bites

  • Flyers’ Garnet Hathaway collabs with Dogfish Head on Engine 19 IPA—try it, proceeds go to EMTs.
  • Mike Jerrick gets a late-night talk show on FOX29. Broadcast chaos incoming.
  • New Amtrak Acela trains are now rolling through 30th Street. Faster, shinier, only slightly less delayed.

That’s your Philly catch-up. Enjoy the chaotic energy—Labor Day traffic and weather tips here—and see you out there.
