Holiday Crowds, Wintry Mix, and Why Nobody Can Move

Yesterday in Philly – December 23, 2025

Travel & Transit: Good Luck Out There

Holiday travel is back in full force at Philly International, so expect terminals and parking lots to be even more of a circus. Officials say 5% more flyers than last year; maybe charge up your headphones and double-check your gate before leaving home. The overnight forecast has a wintry mix of snow and rain rolling in, just in case you needed another reason to stress about getting to your family gathering (or the grocery store). On the roads, a major eastbound crash on the Pa. Turnpike near Bensalem snarled things up Monday and was a reminder to plan for delays if you’re heading out.

City Life: Stuck & Struggling

No big surprises for anyone trying to buy a rowhome—Philly’s housing market pains are expected to keep going through 2026. Prices are up, inventory is down, and everyone pretty much feels trapped in their rentals (or stuck holding out on a sale). Not a fun outlook, but it helps to know your pain is very, very common.

New Year’s in Philly: More Bang, More Closures

The city is expanding its New Year's Eve fireworks. You’ll have more options (and hopefully less crushing crowds) to watch the magic go down. But with more shows comes road closures in Center City and along the river, so check your usual route before heading anywhere Sunday night. If you’re celebrating with little ones, the Please Touch Museum’s annual event will do a LEGO ball drop before bedtime.

Health & Wellness

Flu season is turning up in the Philly region. Cases are rising in both PA and NJ, so maybe get that shot if you haven’t, and stay home if you’re feeling especially gross. Also: If winter has your muscles feeling locked up, there are four local stretch centers that might help you loosen up.

Sports Quick Hits

  • The Eagles’ playoff path is basically locked into the NFC’s 3-seed at this point. Curious who you should root to face? Here’s the latest scenario roundup.
  • Joel Embiid is likely back for Sixers-Nets tonight. Don’t get your hopes up for a Paul George cameo—he’s set to return for the Clippers, not us (yet).
  • Phillies added bullpen depth, signing reliever Zach Pop through 2026.

Quick Hits

  • If you’re looking for something weird and festive: Peddler’s Village is running Fire & Frost Fun—think ice sculptures, fire pits, lights, and maybe less chaos than your typical city party.
  • Baby African penguins were born at Adventure Aquarium. They’re critically endangered and very tiny, so nice to have some good news.
  • Sad news for South Jersey: McMillan’s Bakery is officially closed. No more legendary cream donuts.

That’s the stuff you actually needed. See you tomorrow.
