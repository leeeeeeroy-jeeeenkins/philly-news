Yesterday in Philly: More Shakeups, Some Wins, and Streets You'll Want to Avoid

Elections & City Hall

It was Election Day and, as expected, Larry Krasner easily won re-election as Philly’s DA. Across the river, Mikie Sherrill is the new NJ governor. Some polling places in NJ had to close briefly due to bomb threats (totally unfounded, but real enough to slow things down), and Chester County’s elections had hiccups with third-party voters forced onto provisional ballots (details). Meanwhile, Democrats maintain control of the PA Supreme Court.

Institutions in Flux

The Philadelphia Museum of Art fired CEO Sasha Suda just a month into a big (and apparently controversial) rebranding push. That’s a quick exit, even by Philly standards. More background here if you want the gossip.

Also, in education chaos, Morrisville School District in Bucks County says it might shut down in January if Harrisburg doesn’t pass a budget. So, yes, schools hanging by a thread over in the burbs.

Transit (Good Luck, Everyone)

Heads up: Two TSA checkpoints at PHL Airport will close for construction starting today, so expect longer security lines. PATCO riders: credit/debit card payment is down system-wide, so bring cash. On a positive note, Open Streets returns to Center City on Dec. 7 (think: Rittenhouse area pedestrian party, car detours incoming). And yes, red bus lanes are real — and might actually help cut through traffic if drivers stop ignoring them.

Meanwhile, Federal officials blamed SEPTA management, not funding, for the agency’s ongoing headaches. So maybe less budget talk, more leadership shakeups next?

Money, Eats, and a Little Cocktail History

Main Line billionaire Jeff Yass is reportedly funding Trump's new ballroom (yep), and scammers are bombarding Philly restaurants with fake one-star reviews so double-check your Yelp hate-reads. Good eating news: Texas sushi hot spot Uchi is coming to Philly, and if you missed it, Hop Sing Laundromat changed the city’s cocktail scene for good. Bit of local food nerd history for your next bar convo.

Quick Hits

  • SNAP benefits pause: City has pointers if you need food assistance.
  • Hotel staff at the Center City Hampton Inn ratified a new union contract – but 7 other hotels are still fighting on expired deals.
  • Route 202 northbound finally reopened in Upper Merion after sinkhole drama.

Sports & Fun

The Eagles didn’t do anything wild at the trade deadline, but GM Howie says A.J. Brown isn’t going anywhere. Sixers are back soon — but keep an eye on possible action around Jared McCain. If you can’t handle election news, try checking out Longwood’s over-the-top holiday display or just take a walk during tonight’s supermoon (how to spot it).
