Transit Turbulence, Bridge Drama, and Bivalves

Hey Philly. Here's what you missed yesterday—let's get into it:

🚌 SEPTA Still on the Edge

So, Harrisburg finally (again) approved a new mass transit funding bill, throwing SEPTA a potential lifeline—but it's not a done deal yet. This doesn't mean the looming doomsday service cut scenario—where your bus/El/Regional Rail rides get axed—is actually off the table. SEPTA’s warning the deadlines are, no joke, right before school starts. So, if you or your kids depend on transit to get to class/work/life, keep your eyes peeled for next steps (and maybe dust off your old bike just in case).

🚧 Market Street Bridge Construction—Again

Get ready for even more Center City traffic fun: the Market Street Bridge just started “emergency repairs” that'll close lanes for weeks. Detours, backups, classic Philly stress. SEPTA buses will likely re-route; car and bike folks, consider your alternate routes now.

🏡 City Hall Moves & Data Fights

  • The feds just asked for Pennsylvania's voter rolls (including personal data) to check for issues. State leaders are pushing back, concerned about privacy. If you’re registered, your info could be in play.
  • Delco is moving to require helmets for e-scooters. Not law yet, but count on hearing about it (and seeing more helmet hair in Media).

🍽️ Restaurant Shuffle

  • Tesiny, a glam new oyster bar, just opened on East Passyunk. Think caviar bumps, shellfish towers, and serious cocktail game. Worth it if you’re flexing on a Tuesday.
  • Kismet Bagels is taking over the old Pizza Jawn in Manayunk. Expect chewy things in your near future.

🏈 Injury Watch

If you're following the Eagles: Pro Bowl guard Landon Dickerson tore his meniscus at the Linc open practice; he's heading for minor surgery. Should be back for the start of the season, but the O-line is suddenly a bit shakier. No need to panic … yet.

🎳 Quick Hits

  • Volunteer signups are open for the 2026 FIFA World Cup matches in Philly. If you want to play host to planet Earth, here’s your shot.
  • Market Street not your scene? There’s a rundown of cool stuff to do this week inside and out.
  • Comedian Nate Bargatze adopted a dog from the PSPCA and named her Philly during his Wells Fargo show, because of course he did.

That’s the big stuff. Protect your commute, check your voter reg, and maybe treat yourself to a bagel. More tomorrow.
