Schuylkill Trail Sinks, SNAP Crunch Looms, and Greek Food Ghost Towns

City Life & Survival

  • Schuylkill River Trail: Closed (Again) — Heads up if you bike or run along the river: a 10-foot-long, 8-foot-deep sinkhole shut down the trail between the Columbia Bridge and East Falls. No timeline on repairs, so reroute your commute or workout until further notice.
  • Food Bank Squeeze Incoming — Philly food pantries are already jammed and now a pause in federal SNAP benefits could mean lines out the door. If you rely on food assistance, brace for potential delays — advocates warn a “massive strain” is coming fast.
  • LIHEAP Funding Delayed — Expect help with heating bills to be delayed at least a month thanks to congressional gridlock. Pennsylvania officials blame the government shutdown, so prepare for higher costs as winter sets in.

Neighborhood Notes

  • Greek Restaurants: A Dying Breed? — If it feels harder to find spanakopita lately, you’re not imagining things. Local Greek dining is vanishing in Philly, with longtime favorites closing and few newcomers opening. Yes, you’ll have to work harder for a good gyro.
  • Jersey Kebab Finds New Home — After their ICE ordeal, the owners of Jersey Kebab are moving the shop to Collingswood. Mark your map if you’re a fan.
  • Wawa Recall & Reward Shakeup — PSA: Some Wawa milk and lemonade products are being pulled from shelves (check your fridge). And big changes are coming to Wawa Rewards — time to read the fine print if you’re a sizzli points hound.

Politics & City Hall

  • Kada Scott Homicide: Charges Filed — The murder of 23-year-old Kada Scott is officially ruled a homicide; Keon King is charged. D.A. Krasner is catching heat, with his opponent calling him a "co-conspirator" (ouch). The property where Scott’s body was found is getting a policy review.

Transportation Disasters

  • I-95: Night Closures Return — If you drive in Northeast Philly, brace for more nighttime lane closures on I-95 through November. Give yourself extra minutes — or just avoid if you can after dark.

Fresh in Philly

  • Netflix House Arrives — A new Netflix House experience launches Nov. 12 at King of Prussia Mall (think merch, food, and themed mini-golf). Mark your calendar if you need to entertain visiting relatives.
