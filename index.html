Yesterday in Philly: Comebacks, Goodbyes, and Playoff Hopes

Eagles Make History, Again

If you somehow missed it, the Eagles pulled off their biggest comeback ever at the Linc, roaring back from 19 points down to beat the Rams 33-26. Jordan Davis blocked two field goals in the fourth, and Jalen Hurts put on a show when it mattered. Philly is 3-0—and if you want the wild details, here’s a rundown of the game’s biggest moments. The Phils fell to the Diamondbacks this weekend. Not great, but the focus is now all on playoff prep—and key arms like Ranger Suárez are getting ready to go. It’s scoreboard-watching time and, yes, we’re still keeping an eye on those "nagging Mets" for seeding. Series summary here, and weekend rooting guide here.

Flyers Legend Bernie Parent Dies at 80

Tough loss for Philly: Bernie Parent, the Hall of Fame goalie who led the Flyers to their only two Stanley Cups, passed away at 80. If you don’t know your Flyers history, Parent was the backbone of those Broad Street Bullies championship squads. Obits and memories from WHYY, 6ABC, and FOX29.
