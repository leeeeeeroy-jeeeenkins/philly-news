Yesterday in Philly

Eagles Wrap the East, Power’s Out, Hit-and-Run Tragedies

Eagles do it again: If you only have bandwidth for one headline over breakfast, let it be this: Birds clinched the NFC East (again) after beating the Commanders in D.C. With Jalen Hurts looking steady (despite a sore finger), Philly locked in a home playoff game. Yes, Lane Johnson was sidelined, but the Eagles have been rolling the division for decades (they really have owned the NFC East this century). Enjoy it—we’re living in the golden age for this team.
Quick injury watch: No major new issues, but the offensive line is duct-taped.

Sixers keep fighting: While Joel Embiid is still fighting off injury, Tyrese Maxey carried the load in NYC for a gritty win over the Knicks at MSG. Notable: rookie VJ Edgecombe made his Madison Square Garden debut and didn’t flinch (more here).

Commuter/Neighborhood Watch:

  • SEPTA: A woman was killed by a train at Melrose Park Station—trains were delayed/canceled on the Lansdale-Doylestown line. Double-check your Monday commute if you use Regional Rail.
  • Trash schedule: If you’re in North Philly, bi-weekly trash pickup starts in January—look out for changes to your normal routine.
  • Power outages: Thousands lost power across the region last night as gnarly winds rolled through. If your lights are out, you’re not alone.

Streets: Too Deadly

  • 54-year-old cyclist killed in West Philly hit-and-run, and a woman killed in North Philly while crossing the street. It’s grim, and both drivers are still out there. This is becoming a sick routine: two fatal hit-and-runs overnight, details here and here.
  • One dead, several hurt in Upper Darby/Drexel Hill apartment fire late Saturday night. Take care of each other.

Other Stuff Locals Care About:

  • Delco Pooper update: The notorious ‘Delco Pooper’ gets community service and anger management. Yes, that’s real life in 2025.
  • Looking for a Christmas meal you don’t have to cook? A bunch of Philly spots are open on Christmas Eve & Day.
  • If you bought a Powerball in PA: somebody scored a $1 million ticket. Jackpot’s now at $1.5 billion.

That’s the city as of Sunday morning. Take care out there. See you Monday.
