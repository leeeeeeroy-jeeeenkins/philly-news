Snow, Icy Streets, and a Rough Weekend for Philly Sports

Good morning, Philly. If you spent yesterday peering out the window at the mess or cursing at SEPTA alerts, you weren’t alone. Here’s what actually mattered across the city while you were shoveling (or hiding):

Weather Mess: Accidents, Delays, and Snow Totals

Let’s start with the obvious: snow blanketed the region, with some spots racking up over 5 inches and even more ice on top. The roads weren’t friendly. Emergency crews responded to deadly crashes in Port Richmond and South Jersey. If you’re in the ‘burbs or Jersey: warnings stay in place, with New Jersey under a state of emergency and snow emergencies declared across Pa. communities.

Transit’s a headache too: SEPTA’s Trenton Line was suspended after a fatal accident, just as travelers packed stations trying to dodge flight cancellations and delays (yep, the airport is still a mess).

Sports: Slippery for Fans, Too

It wasn’t much better on the hardwood: Sixers fell to the Bulls, and now Joel Embiid is out for tonight’s Thunder game (double injury status—fun). In football, Eagles rookie Tyler Steen picked up a fine for last week’s brawl (he’s not the only one), and some folks are already looking at potential picks for the 2026 NFL Draft (stay grounded, y’all).

If you want a big-picture look at who’s actually running Philly sports, this top 10 list is worth a scroll.

Living Here: From Rent to Goats

Winter means higher bills: Here are some tips to keep your power bill from spiking. In less common news: pet goats in Franconia might be evicted (yes, really—maybe don’t tell your landlord about this precedent).

Also, if you rely on SNAP benefits, new rules could shake things up—worth a check-in. In Bucks County, the NTSB updated their investigation into the Bristol nursing home explosion, with more to come soon.

Elsewhere

If you’re desperate for a reason to leave the house: the Bowie & Prince dance party is coming to Underground Arts, and the Philly Fishing Show kicks off in Oaks if you want to dream about warmer days.

That’s the city—more icy than nice right now, but we’ve seen worse. Stay warm out there.
