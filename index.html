2nd Street Fest Packs the Block, Delco Hospitals MIA, and Saquon Is That Guy

Good morning, Philly. Here’s what went down yesterday:

City Life (and Things That Make Us Go Hmmm):

First up: 2nd Street Festival brought thousands to Northern Liberties for beer, food, and some throwback block party energy. If you were looking for parking anywhere nearby: sorry for your loss.

In way less festive news: Crozer Health, the folks who run those two shuttered Delco hospitals, told local officials they might just... abandon the properties (yeah, seriously). Town leaders aren’t thrilled about the idea of two giant empty buildings just sitting there, attracting trouble. TLDR: No fix in sight, and neighbors are pissed.

Commute & Weather Watch:

Not going to sugarcoat it: It’s about to get real hot again to start the week, with classic Philly summer stickiness in full effect. Hydrate or complain loudly—your call.

Sports: No Off-Season in This Town

Eagles: Training camp’s rolling along, and Saquon Barkley is already looking like the real deal. (Yes, it’s early. Yes, people are still excited.) Meanwhile, keep your eyes on Moro Ojomo—the "awkward" D-lineman is making noise. If you’re worried about youth on the roster, don’t: Eagles are stacked with under-25 talent right now. (Take that, haters.)

Phillies: The big red machine took the series from Detroit behind Cristopher Sanchez and the bullpen. The dream stays alive.

Quick Hits

  • Crews knocked down a row home fire in Frankford (no injuries reported).
  • Bensalem saw a police-involved shooting after a road rage chase—investigators are sorting through what happened.
  • If you missed voting deadlines for next year’s PA primaries, here’s your catch-up guide.
  • Shoutout to the Gaudreau Family 5K folks, who raised over $500K for an accessible playground in Jersey.

That’s the stuff Philly was talking about yesterday. Get out there (or crank the AC and stay cool). We'll do it all again tomorrow.
