SEPTA Cuts, College Drama, Michelin Buzz: What Philly Talked About Yesterday

Hey, Philly. Here’s what you missed yesterday, whether you were stuck in traffic (maybe thanks to SEPTA) or just trying to get through the week. Let’s get into it.

💥 SEPTA Cuts: Lawsuits, Confusion, and Business Panic

SEPTA’s service cuts kicked in, making a mess of commutes, frustrating drivers, and creating plenty of headaches for workers and businesses. Lawsuits are already flying: riders are challenging the cuts for being unfair to low-income communities (WHYY), and another group outright claims the agency’s whole “financial crisis” is overhyped (NBC10). While lawmakers in Harrisburg are still hashing out SEPTA funding (NBC10), businesses (6ABC) and bus drivers (FOX29) say the whole thing makes everything less safe.

🎓 Philly Colleges: One Up, One Down

Drexel took a beating on Niche’s new “Best Colleges” list, falling way down the ranks (Philly Mag). But on the bright side, Gen Z is turning Philly’s Free Library system into something way more political and activist-minded than the old “shhh” days. Basically: don’t sleep on your local branch.

🍽 Michelin Guide: Philly’s Big Dining Moment Is (Almost) Here

The Michelin Guide will finally reveal which Philly restaurants make the cut at an awards ceremony downtown this fall (PhillyVoice). Our city’s critics are making bold predictions—if you want to get a res at any favorites, do it before November.

🏥 Health Roundup

  • A Bryn Mawr OB-GYN is launching a menopause center this fall (Be Well Philly) to actually help people through all the not-so-fun changes.
  • A Philly biotech company is in hot water for claiming their products killed COVID (feds say: nope).
  • Penn State researchers have developed a breath test for diabetes. Could be big for fast diagnoses.

🏈 Eagles & Sports Things

  • The 2025 Eagles depth chart came out. Hype! But heads-up: talent is deep at the top, but thin behind the starters (PhillyVoice).
  • The full cut-down tracker is here (PhillyVoice), and the Birds just picked up lineman Willie Lampkin (PhillyVoice).

🎬 Quick Hits

  • Mike Jerrick is getting a late-night show on FOX29 (PhillyVoice).
  • ‘Deli Boys,’ the Hulu sitcom set in South Philly, got picked up for season 2 (PhillyVoice).
  • Dinos After Dark is back at the Academy of Natural Sciences Sept 26 (PhillyVoice).
  • Woodmere Art Museum is suing the Trump admin for killing a $750K grant (PhillyVoice).

That’s yesterday in Philly. If SEPTA didn’t mess up your plans, something else probably did.
Stay tuned and stay scrappy.
