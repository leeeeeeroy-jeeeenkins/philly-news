SEPTA Squeeze, Teacher Strike Threats, and Labubu Riddles

Hey Philly, yesterday brought big news for anyone who rides SEPTA, has a kid in school, or just likes their gelato cold and their collectibles real. Here’s what you missed—and what might mess with your next commute.

Transit Headaches Incoming

If your mornings depend on SEPTA: fair warning, the transit authority just published new schedules with service cuts up to 20% systemwide. Blame the budget deadlock in Harrisburg—the agency set an August 14 deadline for the state to cough up more funding before these cuts become real (and yes, things like Regional Rail are absolutely on the chopping block). If you want all the political back-and-forth, watch lawmakers debate the SEPTA budget mess here. SEPTA’s basically calling it a death spiral unless new money is found—so, no pressure anyone in state government. Here’s a breakdown of the new cuts.

Schools & Labor Watch

With less than a month to the first bell, the Philadelphia teachers union is prepping for a possible strike unless a deal gets done soon. So far, no agreement with the district and talk is getting tense. Start thinking about backup plans if you’ve got school-age kids—it’s not just saber-rattling.

Crime Roundup

Some relief: police arrested a man suspected of sexually assaulting at least six women in Center City and South Philly, after a pattern of attacks over the past few weeks. Details on the ongoing investigation here.

Culture, Eats & Small Joys

  • Labubu Mania: If you’re not sure if your quirky little Labubu toy is real or "lafufu,” Philly shops are here to help you spot the fakes. Apparently, the collectible craze is at all-time high (and so are the knock-offs).
  • Gelato Alert: New pop-up Cocco’s Gelato is taking over the local food scene. Expect lines, but apparently it’s worth it.
  • Zoo News: The Philadelphia Zoo just introduced a new Amur tiger and leopard, with hopes for some tiger cubs in the future.

Odds & Ends

  • If you love football: Devan Kaney is the new sideline reporter for Eagles games. Meanwhile, training camp is heating up—see who’s rising, who’s falling.
  • Rare whiskey lottery is open now, if that’s your thing.
  • Phillies couldn’t sweep the O’s yesterday—one rough inning did them in.

That’s it. Stay cool—heat (and air quality alerts) continue today, so adjust your plans/elderly neighbors accordingly.
