Yesterday in Philly

Subject: Friday Eagles Slide, Wanamaker Lights Up Again

Eagles on Thin Ice & Holiday Traditions Rally

The big stories: the Birds are going through it, Wanamaker's Light Show is officially back, and Philly shops like it’s 2019. Here’s your Friday rundown:

Eagles: Not the Weekend We Wanted

Let’s get the pain out of the way early: Eagles lost to the Bears on Black Friday, marking back-to-back losses for the second time this season. The run defense just straight-up forgot to show up (game analysis here, and more here). With Dallas suddenly breathing down our necks, that playoff clinch is feeling a lot less comfy (clinching math explained here). Long story short: defense, do something!

Holiday Glow & Classic Philly

Good news: the Wanamaker Christmas Light Show is fully back in Center City for 2025, thanks to a push from IBEW Local 98 and big community vibes (coverage here), so drag your people into Macy’s and let ‘em feel the nostalgia. Meanwhile, the Holiday Horses and Bazaar event at Northwestern Stables in Fairmount Park kicked off, if your holiday needs more horse than LED.

The Shopping Mood

Philly showed up for Black Friday—King of Prussia, Cherry Hill, the whole region. Malls were busy, people actually seemed happy to be out, and despite economic weirdness, folks are looking for ways to stretch every dollar (here’s how). Gas prices are down (below $3 a gallon) so that probably helps. If you want to give gifts that do more, Philly Mag dropped a Do-Good Gift Guide.

Transit & Safety Notes

PHL airport had a brief ground stop Friday due to a security issue, but things are back to normal. Tragically, several fatal crashes and shootings happened across the city (Strawberry Mansion fire, pedestrian traffic fatalities), but citywide impacts are limited.

Quick Hits

  • Want some nostalgia? Philly’s lost concert halls remembered.
  • Head spas are apparently the new thing in Philly (explainer here).
  • Specialized urgent care for cancer is now easier to access in the city (details).

Breathe. It’s only Friday.
