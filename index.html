Yo, SEPTA's Acting Up and Water Just Got Pricier

Here’s what went down in Philly yesterday — the headlines, the headaches, and the food news you’ll actually care about.

Transit Troubles (Again)

If you’re heading to the Eagles home opener (good news: ticket prices are dropping), brace yourself for transit chaos. SEPTA is still dealing with those service cuts, so the team is basically begging fans to plan ahead — or risk being late (or stuck on the wrong side of the Schuylkill). To add insult, some bus riders got overcharged Monday, but SEPTA promises refunds. If you think you’re due, keep your ears open for details.

Water Rate Hikes Are Here

Those new water rates just kicked in, and yeah, you’re going to feel it. The city says the average customer can expect to shell out almost $100 more per year. If your landlord passes it through, heads up.

Cyclist Killed by Speeding Driver

West Philly lost a longtime cyclist advocate — John "Jay" Jastrzembski was killed in a hit-and-run while trying to cross in Fairmount Park. If you bike, you already know these streets are rough. Police have recovered a vehicle but are still looking for the driver (more here).

Food, Fun, and Touchdowns

  • Fleur’s, a new fancy French spot, just opened for reservations in Fitler Square. If you have money left after your water bill.
  • SEPTA giving out refunds for that bus fare screw-up feels like a very Philly apology.
  • Eagles stock is up in the power rankings, and Saquon Barkley — yes, now in green — landed the top spot on the NFL’s Top 100 list. He also got his own Wawa hoagie. (You know you wanna try it just once.)

Quick Hits

  • Phillies bats and bullpen powered them to the top of the division last month.
  • Port Richmond barbershop needs help after a break-in if you want to kick a few bucks their way.
  • Haunted Philly tours, pop-ups, and a new look for the Xfinity arena: see what else is happening around town this week.

That’s the gist. Don’t ignore those bills, watch the crosswalks, and hope SEPTA gets it together before Thursday. Catch you tomorrow.
