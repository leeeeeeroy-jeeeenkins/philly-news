Yesterday in Philly: Murals, Meltdowns, and SEPTA Actually Improving?

City Beautification Blitz
With the 250th anniversary and a World Cup around the corner, Philly is spending $11.5 million to clean up highways, paint murals, and blast graffiti before the tourists roll in. The Parker administration says this “gateway” project is about “uplifting communities,” but honestly, most people just want to see less trash and tags near the bridges. Fingers crossed the landscaping lasts longer than a Wawa hoagie on a park bench.

SEPTA: Something Good for Once
Surprise! Regional Rail morning express service is coming back Monday after ages of pandemic cuts. Need the Trolley Tunnel? That’s back too (reopens Monday) after a months-long closure. If you commute, rejoice—at least until the next delay.

Art Museum Drama: Airing Dirty Laundry
Remember all that palace intrigue at the top of the Philadelphia Museum of Art? Turns out it’s messier than we thought, with new details about internal meltdowns, staff reorganizations, and an institution struggling to stay relevant. Worth a read if you’ve ever wondered why it takes 4 months to hang a new painting.
