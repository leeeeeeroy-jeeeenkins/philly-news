Yesterday in Philly

Subject: Cold Snap, Cheaper Cafés, Major City Moves

Weather

Bundle up—light snow and real winter cold are finally rolling into Philly this weekend. Salty boots and frozen sidewalks are back on the menu. Plan accordingly, and maybe consider a hot chocolate crawl (here’s one).

City Life & Getting Around

  • More Sidewalk Cafés: If you like your dinner under the city lights, good news—City Council just made it way easier for Philly restaurants to put tables outside. Expect more al fresco dining in 2025.
  • Greyhound’s Back Downtown: Bus travelers, rejoice: Council says the Greyhound terminal is coming back to Center City. No more shivering on random Market Street benches.
  • I-95 Lanes Closing: Overnight lane closures on I-95 are coming next week, because of course they are. If that’s your route, check the detours.
  • SEPTA Strike Averted: SEPTA reached a tentative contract deal with a big union, so your buses and trains are safe for now.

City Hall & Real Estate

  • Housing Fight: Parker’s big housing plan got a first-round approval—but not exactly how the mayor wanted it. Lot of debate on funding and timelines; more drama to come.
  • Delco Tax Hike: Delaware County is hiking property taxes by 19% in 2026. If you own, brace yourself. If you rent, prepare for the fallout.
  • Empty School Buildings: Philly schools are negotiating to hand over vacant buildings to the city. Fingers crossed for future community use, not more abandoned eyesores.

Food & Fun

  • New Taproom Alert: Fishtown’s got a new hang: Forest & Main’s taproom (yes, with bread pizzas AND funnel cake).
  • The PAFA holiday pop-up is happening—a rare chance to score real art without breaking the bank.
  • Otherworld Philly is glowing for the holidays (go if you want trippy lights and wild selfies).

Sports

  • Eagles: Week 15 injury report looks okay—key guys expected to play. Pass offense is improving (finally, some proof).
  • Sixers: Embiid and Maxey probably playing Friday against the Pacers. No excuses.
  • World Cup Tix: If you’re thinking of seeing World Cup games at the Linc—four tickets to every Philly match could run you over $5,000. Oof. Time to start that GoFundMe group chat.

One More Thing

PS—If you want some advice from a Philly doc who keeps it real, Dr. Mike is now taking your questions.
