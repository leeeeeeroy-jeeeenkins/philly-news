Yesterday in Philly

Subject: Birth Centers, Rocky Drama, and Renters Flooding Center City

Good morning. Yesterday was mostly about big changes: some wins, some losses, and a reminder that if you blink, Philly’s already moved on. Here’s the main stuff you actually care about.

City Life

  • More Philly birth centers are closing. Another one bites the dust with Lifecycle giving notice. That means fewer options for expecting parents who want births outside hospitals — and more questions about access and affordability. If you or anyone you know is pregnant, pay attention: your choices are shrinking fast.
  • Who’s moving into Center City’s new apartments? Transplants, mostly from DC, NYC, or Boston. Local renters are getting priced out, but if you overhear someone raving about ‘Bagelsmith’ for the first time — now you know why.
  • North Philly gets a trash pickup upgrade. The city’s rolling out twice-a-week pickups for parts of North Philly, finally. If it’s your block, you’ll see less trash mountain build-up (we can dream, right?).
  • Wanamaker Building: future up in the air. New renderings are out for what could become of the iconic Wanamaker — but for now, it’s limbo and a lot of big ideas. Worth a peek if you’re into Center City rumors.
  • Delco approved a 19% property tax hike. Oof. Not Philly proper, but if you live in Delaware County, check your next bill — it’s going up.

Old Philly, New Drama

  • Rocky statue stays put (for now). Stallone says the original Rocky statue isn’t leaving, even though someone floated moving it back to the top of the Art Museum steps (proposal here). For now, your iconic selfie spot is safe.
  • Windows smashed at federal courthouse. Some late-night vandalism at the federal courthouse in Market East. There’s an investigation, but expect some visible repairs if you walk by.

Getting Around

  • Heads up if you use the I-95 ramp from Columbus Blvd: it’s closing Thursday for the capping project. Expect headaches if that’s your route.

Food & Local Wins

  • Chef Omar Tate of Honeysuckle is now a Pew Fellow. If you’ve been to Honeysuckle Provisions, you already know. Everybody else: check them out — and congrats to one of Philly’s most innovative voices in food.

Sports Check-in

  • Schwarber stays, Realmuto next? Schwarber’s sticking with the Phillies (not that anyone doubted it). Now the front office is making J.T. Realmuto the top priority.
  • Eagles: Still sliding, but favored. Yes, they’re in a freefall, but somehow Vegas likes them vs. the Raiders (odds here).

That’s yesterday, Philly. Stay warm, watch for trash trucks, and maybe buy that friend in Delco a drink.
