Yesterday in Philly

Subject: Birds Fly High, Rent Rules Set & SEPTA Swag Drops

Eagles Clinch the NFC East, But It Was Weird

Another drama-packed Sunday for the Eagles: despite self-inflicted wounds, they sealed the NFC East title for the first time in two decades. Turnovers? Yes. Special teams, uh, challenges? Definitely (Jake Elliott is… not having his best stretch). But Jalen Hurts keeps making history, and the locker room is already talking about who should rest or play the next two weeks (debate here). If you want the down-and-dirty details (snap counts, game balls, awards), check the analyses and the recap.

Sixers Find a Spark (and a Win)

Over on the court, the Sixers managed a gritty win despite missing key players. Nick Nurse rolled out a brand new lineup that nobody saw coming—and it actually worked. Maxey delivered again, and the bench stepped up. File this one under "ugly but effective."

City Council’s Big 2025 Housing Push

If you rent, own, or even just live here, pay attention: City Council passed a bunch of new housing bills aiming to tweak rules for renters, homeowners, and developers in the new year. No, it won’t fix everything, but changes include shifts to rental protections and some plans meant to slow up rent spikes. Get a quick rundown here.

What Else Happened?

  • Tragic loss: The cyclist killed in last week’s hit-and-run was officially identified as longtime local DJ Robert "DJ Rock Steady" Sellers (NBC10).
  • West Philly shooting: Mother and baby were shot just days before Christmas. Police are investigating (details).
  • SEPTA merch drop: If you have strong feelings about public transit logos, SEPTA opened a pop-up shop at HQ for all your regional rail fashion needs (WHYY).
  • Random good news: The War On Drugs raised cash for Philly public schools with a big guest-filled holiday gig (more).
  • Puppies found abandoned at South Philly Target. There’s a silver lining: they’re all getting vet care (NBC10).

Looking Ahead

Holiday forecasts say don’t get your hopes up for a white Christmas (6ABC). And if you’re flying, heads up: airline food workers are striking, which could make for even more airport chaos (info).

That's it for Philly. Take care of each other and keep an eye out—it's always something out here.
