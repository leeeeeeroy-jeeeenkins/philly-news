Yesterday in Philly: Cold Snaps, MLK Service, and Eagles Shakeup

Weather & Schools

Winter’s really here: arctic winds are cutting through the city, with single-digit wind chills expected all week. Following the storm that left up to four inches of snow over the weekend (how much snow did your block get?), icy roads have led to delayed openings for schools on Tuesday. Check before you bundle the kids up.

MLK Day: Philly Turns Out

The city turned out for MLK Day with service projects all over, from cleaning up Fairmount Park’s invasive species (details) to new student murals at Kensington Health Sciences Academy (see them). Protesters marched in Center City demanding an end to ICE raids and detention deaths (recap). Over in Germantown, residents are organizing against displacement and leaning on “people power” to save their neighborhood (read more).

Eagles: Now What?

With the season over, the Eagles are looking for a new offensive coordinator—and quickly. They’re reportedly interviewing former Bucs OC Josh Grizzard (report) and have requested a chat with Miami’s Bobby Slowik (update). The whole direction of the offense is on the table, so expect more maneuvering in the days ahead (thoughts here).

Sixers: Maxey’s Big Moment & Trade Talk

Tyrese Maxey will start in the All-Star Game—for the first time ever (get the story). Meanwhile, Jared McCain is back from the G League, but his stint was rough—it might be time for a trade to bolster the guard depth (analysis).

Neighborhood News & Openings

  • Queen Village is getting a new lesbian bar this spring: Val's, if you’ve been missing those kinds of spaces in Philly.
  • Rittenhouse’s Pink Elephant pop-up is back for Valentine’s, serving up espresso martinis and themed snacks (peek here).
  • For the one-percenters, Four Seasons unveiled a new penthouse suite that’ll run you $25,000 a night (yes, you read that right).

Quick Hits

  • Drexel scientists found a new use for mosquito needles. Something about 3D printing and less pain (it’s weird, but cool).
