SEPTA Deal, Trolley Tunnel Trouble, and Haverford Bomb Scare

Transit & Getting Around

The city’s biggest transit headache might finally ease up: SEPTA’s largest workers union voted to ratify a new contract, putting any imminent strike talk to bed. But you’re not out of the woods—the Center City trolley tunnel is still closed with no reopening date, thanks to major wire damage. If you ride the trolleys, factor in delays and detours (and maybe look into remote work). Meanwhile, a driver fleeing state police crashed into a SEPTA bus on Market Street yesterday, sending the bus driver to the hospital and jamming up another busy afternoon.

Schools & Safety

Haverford High School got evacuated and dismissed early due to a bomb threat, but after a good sweep, police said everything was clear and classes resume as normal. Rough vibe, but nothing found.

On transit safety: the new community-led Peace Patrols on SEPTA are starting up to try to keep things calm on the platforms this winter. We’ll see if it helps.

Housing & Money

If you’re in Bucks County or paying attention to taxes, the commissioners unanimously approved an 8% property tax increase. Not what anyone wanted to hear at year-end, but that’s how it’s shaking out. Plus, a new report says homelessness is on the rise in Philly—even as holiday donations ramp up.

Sports

Football-wise: the Eagles’ week 16 injury report is out—nothing game-breaking, but Lane Johnson is still questionable. For playoffs, here’s the math on how the Birds can clinch the NFC East and grab the 2-seed. For Flyers fans, bad break: Tyson Foerster is having surgery and could miss the rest of the regular season.

  • Kensington nonprofit Klean Kensington is betting that public gardens might be the city’s answer to fighting litter. Yes, seriously.
