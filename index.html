Yesterday in Philly: Parks on the Back Burner, SEPTA Price Hikes & Hoagie Chaos

Touching on what's getting funded (or not), where to eat, why the bus will be late, and what everyone's actually talking about.

City Hall Watch

  • Philly parks are broke: The city’s parks have been underfunded for years, and it’s showing—from cracked courts to broken playgrounds, and a $600 million maintenance backlog that isn’t shrinking. This matters for real: fewer summer camps, fewer trees, and a whole lot more excuses why the rec center’s closed. Now, after COVID’s wake-up call, advocates are pushing (again) for the city to step up.
  • School shake-ups? Building conditions are so uneven that the School District is studying every campus to see what should close and when. Get ready for some tough neighborhood fights as that all sorts out.
  • Legislative Overload: City Council is kicking off the fall session, with bills on deck covering utility costs, affordable housing, and—yes—efforts to crack down on those sketchy shops selling kratom and delta-8. More: PhillyVoice.

Transit, Money & Messes

  • SEPTA’s changing again: Full service is back starting Sunday, but so are higher fares. Officials dropped another fare hike just as things are finally running closer to normal. Look up your new monthly cost before you get blindsided Monday morning.
  • Trash crackdown: A new task force is actually hitting illegal dumpers with fines. The hope: less open-air garbage mountain, more livable city.
  • Philly not #1 (for being broke): For the first time in decades, Philly isn’t the “poorest big city in the US”—but don’t order champagne yet: our poverty rate is still sky-high, just not the highest.

Eagles, Phillies & What to Watch

  • Birds/Chiefs rematch: It’s Week 2 and the Eagles are heading into Arrowhead for a Super Bowl do-over. Injury watch is on (see who’s questionable). Get your takes, picks, and how-to-watch details here.
  • Phils magic numbers: Phillies swept the Mets (again), putting the postseason within reach. Magic number talk is officially on.

Eats, Drinks, & Philly Moments

  • Bon Appétit loves Provenance: Provenance just made Bon Appétit’s list of 20 best new restaurants in the country. If you haven’t been, expect reservations to get harder than ever.
  • Hoagie madness: There was a Hoagie Throwdown, it got wild, and someone tried to make a "sweet chili turkey" happen. (Don’t worry—Italian still reigns supreme.)
  • Philly Fashion Week turns 20, with six days of runway shows, panels, and a big gala. Yes, you have an excuse to bust out those questionable statement pieces. Details.

That’s the gist. More weekend events here. Enjoy the chaos.
