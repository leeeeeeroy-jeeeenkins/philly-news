Flight Cuts, SNAP Struggles & Wildcat Drama

Quick hits on flights, food aid, and the latest Philly bar flex.

Transit & Travel: Watch Your PHL Flights

The FAA is slashing flights at Philadelphia International Airport starting today, aiming to reduce pressure on air traffic control. If you’ve got travel plans coming up (or family descending for Thanksgiving), check your flights: PHL is one of 40 major U.S. airports facing cuts and delays (full details here). Airlines are offering some refunds or free changes if you’re impacted, but flexibility is the name of the game.

SEPTA’s also back in panic mode — not "fixed," according to a general manager. There’s inspection drama and ongoing budget stress. Don’t expect train miracles just yet. Oh, and heads up: the Rocky Run and Veterans Parade will have Center City traffic extra snarled this weekend.

Squeezing SNAP: Food Aid in Flux

Food banks are slammed with long lines and shortages as the ongoing SNAP "crisis" deepens. Philly City Council made noise about it yesterday, but the pain on the ground is real. There’s a court order forcing the feds to restore funding for November, but the situation stays shaky. Pitch in if you can, and double-check your benefits if you rely on SNAP.
