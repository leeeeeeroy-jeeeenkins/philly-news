Yesterday in Philly

Holding Pattern: PATCO Delays, Kada Scott Case, a Giant Zoo Ferris Wheel

‣ Transit/City Life

PATCO riders, you know the drill: Trains ground to a halt for hours yesterday morning thanks to a power outage (details). If you were stuck or late, at least it wasn’t you. Service is back but—per usual—the city’s transit headaches are ever-present. (Also: another take.)

In other recurring drama, Termini Brothers Bakery got hit by vandals overnight. Video footage caught the spray-painters in action at the legendary South Philly shop (story), and police are looking for suspects (update).

The city’s only rape crisis center got a last-minute financial lifeline from the DA’s office after layoffs threatened its survival. It’s not a total fix, but it keeps vital services going for now (read more).

Rent, Insurance & Home: Pennsylvania has approved some eyebrow-raising health insurance premium hikes for 2026, with increases up to 38% (WHYY). If you’re looking for guidance, here’s how to navigate the maze (PhillyVoice).

Civic Stuff: The Will Power program hit a big milestone, helping 1,000 homeowners get their paperwork and property titles squared away—something to note if you’re a Philly homeowner or know someone fighting through the city’s estate headaches.

People & Crime

Kada Scott update: Investigators arrested Keon King in connection with the disappearance of the 23-year-old East Mt. Airy woman whose case has gotten citywide attention (PhillyVoice report, Fox29 background, WHYY update). King has a troubling arrest history (Philly Mag deep dive). Police say the search for Kada continues.

Northeast Philly: An off-duty firefighter was found dead in Holmesburg. A family member is in custody (details).

Fun & New Stuff

Forest & Main—the Ambler beer destination—soft-opens in Fishtown this week. Expect a solid local tap list and a much-needed cozy corner for this stretch of Front Street (Foobooz).

The Philadelphia Zoo is about to get way flashier—they’re building a 110-foot Ferris wheel as part of their 2026 upgrades (PhillyVoice). Reason 232 to drag your friends (or nieces/nephews) out to the zoo next year.

TV & Sports Crossovers: Abbott Elementary did a shoutout to Kyle Schwarber’s monster four-homer playoff game, with Citizens Bank Park and Phillies cameos all over last night’s ep (recap). Maybe the most joyful Phillies-related media you’ll see all week.

That’s the big stuff. Stay warm, watch for more PATCO delays—and maybe ride a Ferris wheel to cope. See you tomorrow.
