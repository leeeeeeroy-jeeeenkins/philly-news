Yesterday in Philly

City paychecks, broken bars, and a big ol’ splash

It’s official: Philly’s biggest city worker union ended its strike, your recycling pickup is still playing catch-up, and the city’s heat is breaking things (including another beloved bar). Catch up on what actually mattered yesterday:

🗳️ City Labor Drama: Settled

  • DC33— the union behind your sanitation and other city workers—approved their new deal with Philadelphia after last week's strike saga. No major changes from the tentative agreement, but it means no more delays… except for your recycling, which is still a backlog mess. Patience.
  • Elsewhere in city services: If you rely on mobile outreach in Kensington, heads up—new restrictions are about to hit, and critics are sounding alarms about how this could leave more people out in the heat.

🍔 Delco Kindness, Flooded Bars, and Neighborhood Eats

  • In Delaware County, locals rallied to save the Clam Tavern, a barbecue spot run by a blind woman, after it faced serious hardship. Faith in humanity: restored (for today).
  • Sonny's Cocktail Joint—finally back open after a 2022 fire—got flooded over the weekend. No word yet on how long it'll be closed this round.
  • Queen Village launched its first Restaurant Week. Try somewhere new and support the locals—find more here.

🚌 SEPTA, Frosh, & City Scenes

  • Temple is probably about to welcome its largest freshman class ever. Not unrelated: SEPTA is still planning to cut more bus routes, which has students scrambling to figure out how they’ll actually get to campus in the fall.
  • If you're Downtown, Dilworth Park’s fountain is getting Fantasia-themed for a hot minute. Consult your childhood for 1930s Mickey cosplay ideas.
  • Bored or melting? What to do in Philly this week.

⚾ Sports Desk

  • Phillies fans: David Robertson wants a ring, and the team is still hunting for bullpen help before the trade deadline. Also: bullpen confidence is a moving target.
  • The Eagles got their entire 2025 draft class signed and locked before training camp kicks in. 25 things to watch at camp if you’re feeling way too ready.

Other quick bits

  • Pennsylvania broke a $6.4 billion gambling record—so, everyone’s losing but the state budget.
  • Morgan Parrish joins Fox 29’s morning show. More Philly in your morning coffee.
  • If you hate the heat (who doesn't?): natural ways to chill and the summer AC survival guide.

Tl;dr: Labor peace, SEPTA headaches, neighborhood wins, and another bar casualty courtesy of Philly water. See you out there.
