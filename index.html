Yesterday in Philly

Unions Flex, Streets Flood & Philly’s Full of It

July 15, 2025

📝 The Worker Showdown

City workers are getting serious: District Council 47 (think lots of desk jobs at City Hall, PHA, and the PPA) voted to authorize a strike if they don’t get a deal they can live with. Over at District Council 33 (trash, streets, rec), the union is voting on a contract and trash pickup has finally resumed—so maybe the piles on your block might shrink soon. Stay tuned: depending on how this shakes out, expect delays for public services and potentially another strike headache later this summer.

🌧️ Wet Streets, Water Woes

Some neighborhoods in North Philly were awash after a water main break flooded a few blocks and cut water for at least 50 homes (FOX29). Crews say things are patched up for now, but the water drama isn’t over: heavy storms rolled in Monday night, with flooding and wind damage across the region. If you park in a low spot, maybe move your car next time.

🚨 Philly Being Philly

  • Some city lawmaker wants to bring back front-and-back license plates in PA. Yes, again. Cops say it’ll help with tolls and hit-and-runs, but honestly, it just means one more thing for you to get ticketed over.
  • Meanwhile, Delco’s got a new ‘Pooper’ on the loose (if you know, you know). It’s the summer, let’s all try to keep it together.

🏀 & ⚾ Sports Quick Hits

  • Paul George went in for a knee scope. Sixers say it’s minor but, real talk: pray for those knees. Also, Summer League notes: new big man Adem Bona just wants to help (and yell).
  • Phillies wrapped their MLB draft, and folks are hyped about Arkansas pitcher Gage Wood. Might be a while before you actually see him at CBP, but hope springs eternal.
  • And Eagles training camp is looming. Will the linebackers hold up? Sirianni still mad at national media for doubting Hurts (and he’s swearing about it).

🍩, 🎨, and More Stuff Worth Knowing

  • A Phish-inspired doughnut exists at Federal Donuts (yep, it’s purple).
  • New murals in Fishtown have dropped if you need some Instagram material.
  • Registration for Dîner en Blanc (this year’s theme: ‘Great Gatsby’) opens tomorrow, if you’re into eclectic outdoor picnics in all white.
  • Gritty 5K also just opened for signups—they’re capping it at 5,500 runners this year, so if you need an October goal (and love chaos), now’s the time.

More: Philly’s first queer women’s sports bar (it’s called Marsha’s) is coming soon, and a Philly poet just dropped a graphic novel imagining universal health care in the future. Because, why not dream?

That’s it for now. Umbrellas up, trash out, and keep an eye on those knees (and the streets).
