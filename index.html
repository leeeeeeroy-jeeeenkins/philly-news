Big Closures, Bigger Roadwork, and a Few Wins

Catch up on everything from restaurant heartbreaks to long-term traffic headaches. There's some sports spice, SEPTA drama (as always), and maybe even a baby sloth to make it better.

🚧 Stuff That'll Mess With Your Commute

Brace Yourself, City Ave Regulars: The dreaded rumors are true — a major section of City Avenue goes down to one lane in both directions for nearly two years starting this week ($22M bridge repair). Seriously, adjust your Google Maps now — this is going to be brutal.

NJ Transit Upgrades: If you're one of the warriors schlepping over from Jersey, shiny new NJ Transit buses are hitting the road, with more coming. At least you might get a more comfortable ride while City Ave’s a total mess.

🏙️ City Life & Closures

End of an Era for Cantina La Martina: After four years, the beloved Cantina La Martina is shutting its doors. Fishtown loses another gathering spot. Go while you can, say goodbye with a margarita.

Mütter Museum Plans Weird, Cool Halloween: Party with some medieval “dancing plague” vibes at this year’s Mütter Halloween bash. They know how to keep it macabre (and educational).

Airplane Lounge Situation: If you had fancy lounge plans at PHL’s United Airlines spot, change ‘em — the lounge is closed for health code issues. Yes, the airport food court is your fate now.

Event Organizer In Trouble: Riot Nerd, the crew behind Philly’s alt and pop-culture theme parties, says they might have to shut down from money trouble.

Shorter Hours: The Academy of Natural Sciences is cutting back its hours thanks to low ticket numbers. Visit before it’s even harder to fit into your week.

🏡 Housing & Local Politics

PHA Drama (Again): Several Philly Housing Authority rowhomes are literally falling apart — peeling paint, water damage, all of it. The city’s on it (sort of), but don’t hold your breath.

Govt. Shutdown Watch: With the federal shutdown looming, plenty of Philly residents, city workers, and local businesses are bracing to feel the effects.

Philly Police Are Trying Out New Uniforms: Starting Wednesday, officers will be test-driving uniforms in new colors. (No, you don’t need to memorize what to look for, they’re still cops.)

🏟️ Sports Quick Hits

  • Eagles keep tweaking: New long snapper, injured reserve shuffles; surprisingly spicy takes on whether winning is hiding bigger issues (more analysis here).
  • Phillies—good news, bad news: They’ve got a first-round playoff bye, but is that a blessing or a curse? (PhillyVoice has thoughts.)
  • Sixers preseason buzz: Maxey’s embracing his leadership moment, Embiid’s just “checking boxes”.
  • If you need good news: Elmwood Park Zoo has a new baby sloth.

🎉 Quick Hits & Events

  • LGBTQ+ pride gets a new fest: OURfest launches in time for National Coming Out Day.
  • Philly transit advocates dare you to ditch your car for “Week Without Driving.”

— That’s it for now. Stay dry, stay patient on City Ave, and tip your bartenders at Cantina La Martina while you still can.
