Transit woes, tacos, & toilets: Philly’s essentials in crisis

Yesterday in Philly

Let’s start with the big one: SEPTA is on the edge, and that could take Amtrak down with it. Officials warn that the looming SEPTA cuts and fare hikes aren’t just a commuter headache—they could wreck Amtrak service up and down the Northeast Corridor. Riders staged a sit-in at state Senator Picozzi’s Northeast Philly office (WHYY recap), while Chester County lawmakers are pitching plans to protect Philly-area tax revenue—and SEPTA itself (story here). Gov. Shapiro spoke out against the fare hikes (watch), and with both local and regional transportation at risk, it’s not an abstract problem.

Neighborhood Stuff

  • The Philly Phlush keeps rolling: our city’s public bathroom renaissance continues with Clark Park getting its own restroom this fall. Come for the jazz, stay for the indoor plumbing.
  • Nicetown-Tioga residents just got 400 new trees and a dozen fresh gardens (see how), proving that Philly’s green thumb is still out there between all the concrete.

Events & Openings

  • Philly Bike Ride is back this fall (details)—plan for a car-free stretch down JFK soon. And if you’re running the 2025 Philly 10K, road closures are up.
  • Suburbs taco fans rejoice: popular NJ chain Tacoria is finally moving into PA, starting with the burbs. Queso for all.

Weird Weather & Health Warnings

  • Hurricane Erin is churning out dangerous currents—swimming bans are now in place across Jersey and Delaware beaches. Don’t test the lifeguards.
  • Malaria in Jersey? State officials are investigating the first potential local case in decades. Fun summer.
  • Also, if you shop at Walmart: the FDA says don’t eat the shrimp—no, seriously, radioactive shrimp recall. Pennsylvania is on the list.

City Hall & Institutions

  • Mütter Museum will keep displaying its human remains, but with new rules on consent and ethics (more here).
  • Swarthmore College is in the hot seat—transgender runner Evie Parts has filed a federal lawsuit after being removed from the track team.

Sports Quick Hits

  • Eagles update: John Metchie III debuted and looked right at home among the other Alabama guys (see him here), but training camp’s offense is still sloppy and some new injuries are cropping up (breakdown).
  • Tyson Foerster is projected to start for the Flyers despite a rough offseason (update).
  • José Alvarado is officially off suspension and back with the Phillies.

That’s your day. Watch out for transit drama, don’t eat the shrimp, and make room for more public bathrooms in your life.
