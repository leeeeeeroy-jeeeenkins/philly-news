Waymo, Windshield Barnacles, and More Field Trips

Good morning. Here’s what Philly’s been up to while you were working, parenting, or dodging potholes.

Transit: Robotaxis Eyeing Your Block

Waymo—yes, Google’s self-driving car spinoff—just started running fully autonomous vehicles in Philly. They’re not taking passengers yet, but PennDOT’s giving the green light for more testing. Pittsburgh’s already seeing these on the road, so get ready for the awkward moment you realize no one’s in the driver’s seat. (More details here.)

SEPTA: Contract Talks (Again) + Silverliner Slog

SEPTA’s returning to the table today with its largest union. Nobody’s shouting “strike” yet, but service slowdowns or walkouts are lurking if they can’t agree on pay and benefits. Also, those infamous Silverliner cars still aren't fully fixed—no big surprise for regular riders.

Education & Community: Quinta Brunson’s Big Philly Energy

If you love Abbott Elementary, good news: Quinta Brunson just launched a fund to cover field trips for 117,000 Philly students. The goal is to make sure every kid gets to see museums and historic sites, not just the schools with parents who have time and cash. Do we still need to say Philly public schools aren’t funded equally? Brunson’s on it either way. (More info.)

Elsewhere, the William Way LGBT Center locked down funding to stay at its longtime home on Spruce—renovations incoming.

City Life: New Cruise Terminal—Seriously

Philly’s going full-on nautical again for the first time in 20 years: Construction is underway on a new port terminal at Philly Shipyard for Norwegian Cruise Line ships starting April. Tacky Hawaiian shirts incoming. (Even more details.)

In more parking drama: Residents in Northern Liberties are making noise about “windshield barnacles”—those giant sticky squares used instead of boots. They say it’s predatory and maybe not even legal. If you drive, keep your eyes peeled (and pay those tickets).

Openings, Eats & Culture

Emmett’s—the new Philly outpost from the NYC pizza spot—just landed on Esquire’s Best New Restaurants list. If you need an excuse to wait two hours for a pie, there you go. Also: Ellen Yin, the hardest-working woman in the city's restaurant biz, got the “day in the life” treatment.

Quick Hits

  • Spotify Wrapped dropped: The top-streamed Philly artist is not Taylor Swift this year (find out who took the throne).
  • NBA fined the Sixers (again) for Embiid injury report shenanigans. $100K this time.
  • Shredded cheese recalled for possible metal fragments. Maybe stick to blocks for now.
  • New JetBlue direct flights to Puerto Rico taking off from PHL soon. Book now if you need sun.

That’s it. Keep your umbrella handy and see you tomorrow, Philly.
