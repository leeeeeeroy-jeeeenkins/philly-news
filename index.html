Yesterday In Philly: The I-95 Mess, Eagles Frustration & Good Eats

Welcome to the Tuesday edition of Yesterday in Philly—your at-a-glance catchup for what the city was actually buzzing about.

City & Commuters

  • Another day, another I-95 headache. There were two crashes yesterday—first near Penn's Landing and then another southbound in New Castle—that jammed up traffic. Plus, if you drive through 26th and Girard, there’s still a downed pole and debris in the road more than two days after a crash. Avoid it if you can.
  • Worried about gas leaks? Good news: Philadelphia Gas Works is offering free methane detectors to residents who want to up their home safety game.
  • The Academy of Natural Sciences is cutting its hours; starting soon, you can only visit three days a week (Friday–Sunday). Blame budget woes.

Food—Two New Must-Try Spots

  • Japanese with a clubby twist: Chef Jesse Ito (Royal Sushi & Izakaya) just opened Dancerobot in Rittenhouse. Think bar snacks, wagyu sliders, and late-night hours—already getting buzz.
  • Filipino comfort food makes waves: Baby’s Kusina & Market is earning raves for homestyle Filipino dishes with a personal touch in East Passyunk.
  • Beer news: Fishtown’s Evil Genius Beer Co. throws its first Oktoberfest this week with stein-hoisting and special pours.

Eagles: Technically 4–0, But...

  • The Birds are undefeated, but no one seems thrilled—not even Nick Sirianni. Players are openly frustrated, and everyone agrees they should look a lot better than they have. Saquon Barkley’s still hunting for that breakout game (should he be getting fewer carries?). If you want the full stats/awards, they’re all here.
  • The Sixers are back for media day, and Joel Embiid is setting the tone (no drama yet; check back soon).

Quick Hits

  • YouTube TV warning: NBC channels (including NBC Sports Philly) could disappear Tuesday if Comcast and Google don’t settle their beef. Cord-cutters: keep an eye out.
  • Starbucks closures: After six Philly shops closed suddenly, workers rallied to demand answers and highlight union issues.
  • Masonic oddities: Did you know there’s an Abraham Lincoln face mold hiding inside the Masonic Temple? This place keeps getting weirder.
  • Crozer-Chester & Springfield hospitals are headed to auction. Bad sign for local healthcare—especially in Delco (details here).
  • True crime fans: A new ABC docuseries re-examines the mysterious death of Philly teacher Ellen Greenberg—don’t go down that rabbit hole unless you have hours to spare.

That’s the gist. For more, here’s what to do around the city this week. See you tomorrow.
