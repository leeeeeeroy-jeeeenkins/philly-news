Snow Incoming, City Moves, and the End of the Robot Wawa

Hey Philly,

If you haven’t checked your weather app yet: grab your groceries and salt now, because this weekend’s looking icy. Meanwhile, the city’s talking new housing, busted power lines, and some real movement in sports and food. Here’s what stood out yesterday.

Weather: Snow’s Coming, Get Ready

  • A major snowstorm and an arctic blast are set to hit Philly this weekend. Warming centers are open around the city if you need them (details here).
  • Folks are already raiding stores for supplies and ice melt is scarce. Remember the storm survival basics: charge your stuff, avoid panic, don’t drive unless you have to (checklist here).
  • Last night’s cold already sparked power line fires in Pottstown, leaving folks without heat. Fingers crossed PECO holds up when this storm really hits.

City Moves: Housing Factories and the End of Robot Wawa

  • Mayor Parker has a plan for the Logan Triangle—yes, the spot famous for sinking homes. She wants to flip it into a hub for modular home construction factories. It’d be a big swing for local housing—if the decades-long struggles with that lot finally end.
  • The all-digital, cashierless Robot Wawa experiment is shutting down. Turns out Philly was just not into a hoagie experience with zero humans. Back to real hoagies made by real people.

Sports: Eagles’ Search, Sixers’ Juggle

  • The Eagles interviewed Chiefs OC Matt Nagy for their own coordinator opening. Rumors are swirling, but a few top names are already off the board.
  • Meanwhile, the search has raised bigger questions: Is Philly an undesirable spot for OCs? (Spoiler: it’s the fans. It’s always the fans.)
  • Sixers are still short-handed and tired, losing to the Suns on night two of a back-to-back (full takeaways). Embiid might play tomorrow; Paul George is questionable (update).

What Else

  • If you’re thinking about a housing move: A body was found in that West Philly senior housing complex slated for a $50M renovation—no more info yet.
  • On a lighter note—here’s a refresher of the city’s best sneaker shops if you need an excuse to avoid the snow and refresh your rotation.
  • And yes, Philly is a point of concern for security with America’s 250th birthday coming up. Local institutions are prepping for eyes on us in 2026.

Stay safe, check on your neighbors, and maybe bring your hoagie money to a real Wawa this week.
