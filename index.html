SEPTA Hikes, Wanamaker Woes, and Philly Still Can’t Get a Decent Appraisal

Happy Friday, Philly. Let’s get into it:

Commute Corner

SEPTA rolled out its fare increase and is busy installing new gates to crack down on fare evasion (link). Small reminder: you’ll pay a little more for your ride this morning. If you noticed delays yesterday, that was probably the Regional Rail being halted for fire department activity downtown, but trains are moving again. Marple Township, meanwhile, just started enforcing a ban on e-scooters. Congrats if you’re a parent shuttling teens.

City Hall, Messy as Ever

City Council is officially beefing with Trump—they passed a resolution condemning his plans to change the slavery exhibit at Independence Park (link). Meanwhile, Mayor Parker did the annual State of Philadelphia thing (link), touting economic growth and public safety. (We’ll see...)

Home and Money Stress

Home appraisals in Philly still show racial bias, according to a new analysis (link). So, as usual, if you’re Black in this city, selling your house might mean a lower number just because. On the rent side: Pennsylvania and New Jersey officially landed in the Top 10 for student debt. No wonder you still have five roommates.

Wanamaker Christmas show may get cutbacks unless organizers hit their fundraising goal (link). If you love the lights, maybe toss them a few bucks, or brace for a dimmer holiday.

Crime + Courts

High-profile cases, all local: Four Philly prison correctional officers were charged with covering up an alleged inmate assault (link). And a Philly lawyer’s teen son was convicted on terrorism charges for trying to make bombs for a group linked to Syria (link | link).

Openings + Fun Stuff

Italian food lovers, rejoice: Eataly is finally opening October 2 in King of Prussia Mall (link). If you’d rather hang in town, check out fall festivals and happenings around the city this weekend via WHYY’s list.

Also of note if you’re a fan of local spectacle: Ed Sheeran announced a 2026 Philly tour stop at the Linc (link), and the Sixers unveiled a throwback court to the Allen Iverson era. Expect AI nostalgia this season.

Alright, that’s it. Make it a decent Friday, Philly.
