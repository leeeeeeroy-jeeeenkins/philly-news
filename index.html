SEPTA Shakeups, Sixers News, and a Philly Summer Preview

It's Friday, so expect big transit changes, restaurant wins, and some drama about the 250th birthday bash.

Transit: Get Ready for New Bus Routes

Heads up: SEPTA is rolling out major bus service changes starting in August—the first part of their Complete Bus Revolution plan. About 60 routes will look different, so if you rely on buses, it’s time to check if your daily ride is about to change. If your route’s on the chopping block or being shifted, don't sleep on the schedules—there will be public info sessions, but, shocker, not everyone’s thrilled.

Sports: Sixers & Phillies Make Moves

  • Sixers: After weeks of rumors, the Sixers confirmed they’re hiring Mike Gansey (ex-Cavs GM) to run basketball operations. Jameer Nelson was in the mix, but it’s Gansey who gets to steer the ship.
  • Phillies: The hot stove is heating up: Philly is watching if two-time Cy Young winner Tarik Skubal hits the trade market (report). Watch for rumors to fly as the deadline approaches.

Money & Housing: PECO Bills, Hotel Tax Controversy

  • PECO's about to charge you more: Expect your electric bill to go up by about $5/month starting June 1 (details), so maybe don't crank the AC just yet.
  • Tourism tax drama: Mayor Parker says hospitality leaders are backing her hotel tax hike, but three major tourism groups are calling this bluff. The debate could impact Philly’s draw for big events coming up.

Food, Drink & Outings

  • Kalaya’s a national star: South Philly’s Kalaya lands at No. 11 on North America’s 50 Best Restaurants. Plan ahead if you want a table.
  • Beer Week: Yes, it's still happening (barely) after losing its longtime organizer. Bar owners say it’s “like a holiday” anyway (more).
  • World Cup fever: Philly is hosting in 2026, but for now pick a backup squad (how-to) and scout the best bars to watch.

Looking Ahead: History, Festivals, and Big Questions

  • SEPTA’s not the only thing changing: Philadelphia's 250th birthday is sparking talk about how we celebrate—and what should go in a time capsule (ideas here), plus a look back at Philly’s messy Bicentennial and Centennial party past.
  • Don’t just do the Liberty Bell: Want to skip the tourist mob? Check out these underrated Revolutionary War sites around Philly instead.
  • Events this weekend: Philadelphia Cycling Classic returns in August (preview), Atlantic City’s Soar & Shore Airshow is back, and Philly Beer Week is officially underway. Boredom not included.

Stay cool, keep an eye out for bus detours, and don’t forget to tip your bartender—summer is here (sorta).
