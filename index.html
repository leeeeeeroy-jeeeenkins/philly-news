Yesterday in Philly: Need-to-Know Edition

Hope you like winter—because the snow’s sticking around and making itself at home. Streets and sidewalks are still a mess, business is crawling back, and yeah… your plans are probably still being shuffled.

Weather Aftermath: Philly Still Digging Out

  • Snow emergency? Lifted Tuesday (details), but cleanup isn’t close to done, especially as temps drop and everything freezes up again (streets department update). If your block feels forgotten, credit goes to the ongoing scramble—and possibly, our city’s complicated relationship with plows.
  • Road closures are still in effect in parts of Center City for overnight snow removal (see what’s closed). Businesses are reopening slowly—some still shuttered after the weekend storm (check on your spot).
  • Regional Rail: Back to normal service today, so SEPTA commuters, try to act surprised (read more).
  • Schools: Most Philly public and archdiocesan schools are remote again today (Wednesday) (virtual day info). If you’ve got kids, that probably means ‘more snacks, less work.’
  • Heads up: Another coastal storm could hit this weekend (uncertain track).

Eat, Drink, Repeat (Try, Anyway)

  • Center City Restaurant Week is extended through Feb 4 because of snow delays (details and list). Bonus days if you missed your rez or if your favorite spot was closed.
    Also, check out everyone serving at 2026 Wine & Dine—big night for food nerds.
  • Amazon Fresh in Northern Liberties is done (never really lived): Amazon is closing all Fresh stores by February (impact, alternatives).

Council Watch: ICE Out Bills Unveiled

  • City Councilmembers rolled out ‘ICE Out’ legislation aiming to limit what ICE can do in Philly—think: less cooperation, more transparency, possible ban on agents wearing masks while operating here (read more). Worth following, especially with local/national pushback after this month’s ICE-connected shootings.

Quick Hits

  • Sixers: Embiid is questionable vs. Bucks tonight, arguably the biggest ‘will he/won’t he’ of winter.
  • Eagles: Still no OC—front office interviewed Packers’ QB coach Sean Mannion and seem intent on keeping us guessing.
  • Bucks County triple homicide: Suspect is in custody (ongoing investigation, all major details covered).
  • Art museum news: The Philadelphia Art Museum is doing Pay-What-You-Wish Friday nights again for the ‘Dreamworld’ show—cheap date, art lovers rejoice.

Stay warm, stay patient, and keep dodging those curbside slush lakes. See you out there.
