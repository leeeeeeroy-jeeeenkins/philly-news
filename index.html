Yesterday in Philly: Air Quality, SEPTA Cuts, and a Pizza Scandal

Hey Philly, here’s what actually mattered yesterday:

Hazy Skies & Health Warnings

So about that weird smell and haze: Code Orange air quality alert was up across the city because of—you guessed it—more Canadian wildfire smoke. If you’ve got asthma or small kids, take it easy outdoors.

SEPTA Service Cuts Incoming

Heads up: Big changes coming for SEPTA lines have students and teachers sweating late buses and packed trains this fall. Riders are pushing back, but for now, brace for trims on some routes.

Nazi Imagery at Wildwood Pizza?

This one’s blowing up on local feeds: A top Wildwood boardwalk pizzeria got outed for Nazi memorabilia in its decor. The owner says it’s all about "history," but most folks aren’t having it. Wild stuff, especially peak summer Jersey Shore season.

Center City, South Philly: Repeat Sex Assault Suspect Sought

Police say one man is likely behind five recent sexual assaults across Center City and South Philly. The city’s on alert—check the descriptions and keep an eye out for updates.

Commuter Headaches

  • I-76 westbound was briefly closed near Mifflin St. for police activity. All clear now, but fun for the Tuesday crowd.
  • If your morning coffee feels more expensive, it’s not just you—climate change and tariffs have coffee prices up, again. Local tip: Yaupon tea might be your wallet-friendly backup.

Quick Hits

  • Borromini, Stephen Starr’s new Italian spot in Rittenhouse, is (finally) opening end of the month. Reservations will go fast.
  • The SS United States lost its first smokestack as prep begins to turn the historic ship into the world’s largest artificial reef.
  • The Phillies bats woke up in a big way, with Schwarber back in the MVP convo just in time for the playoff stretch.
  • Red-light cameras are coming to more Bucks County intersections (yep).

All for now. If you missed National Night Out, there were cookouts everywhere and the city’s still standing.
