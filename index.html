Yesterday in Philly

Market East Skepticism, SNAP Benefits on Edge, and Hotel Workers Hit the Picket Line

Market East Is Back (Or Not?):
Mayor Parker says she’s ready for input on Market East’s next big plan, but folks who lived through the Sixers arena drama are side-eyeing the whole thing. Many longtime residents worry “public input” means the same as last time, i.e., handed a done deal. Whether Comcast, the Sixers, or City Hall get their way, plenty of neighbors want an actual say. Here’s another look at what’s (maybe) next for Market East — no shovels just yet.

SNAP Uncertainty (Again):
If you rely on SNAP (food stamps), don’t make any big grocery runs. The Supreme Court’s emergency order has put full payments in limbo for PA and NJ. The back-and-forth could impact thousands locally, and advocates are scrambling. Gov. Shapiro says things may normalize but, honestly, the situation is anything but clear. It’s not just families feeling it: Even their pets are getting caught in the mess.

Center City Wyndham Strike:
If you have friends or family staying in the city or work in hospitality, heads up: Wyndham Hotel workers in Center City walked out yesterday, demanding better pay and workloads. It’s not a one-day thing either. Travelers might notice changes, and it’s another sign of Philly’s workers turning up the pressure for better deals.

Eagles & Phillies Updates:
For Birds fans watching that Week 10 injury report like it’s the weather, the skinny: A.J. Brown and a few other starters are questionable, and Cam Jurgens is officially out against the Packers. Don’t expect miracles, but the team still has options.
Phillies off-season watch: Outfield depth is a big burning question, and fans are keeping an eye on possible free-agent pickups. Stay tuned (or, if you’re already annoyed, just don’t look).

Other Quick Hits:
- The Rocky Run brought out thousands — streets were sweaty but happy.
- No new Christmas album from the Philly Specials, but you CAN buy a Jason Kelce plush (for charity, so go on).
- Recipe Philly: new crowdsourced eats and a docuseries on the way.
