Good luck getting across town: Trolley woes, road closures, and FAA cuts.

Happy Saturday, Philly. If you’ve got somewhere to be this weekend—and you want to get there on time—read this first.

Transit Troubles, All Around

If you ride SEPTA trolleys: Center City tunnel is closed until at least Monday for overhead wire repairs. Here's how to get around (hint: shuttle buses, and pack patience).

PHL flights? You'll want to double-check. The FAA is slashing flights at Philadelphia International—delays, cancellations, and not a lot of straight answers from airlines. Travelers are nervous and could see headaches through the weekend and beyond.

Driving? Multiple street closures for the Rocky Run, Veterans Parade, and Marine Corps birthday. Check the map before you move.

SNAP Benefits Drama (With Some Good News)

After a complete mess of delays thanks to legal fights, SNAP benefits are finally hitting cards for Pennsylvanians. Governor Shapiro says everyone should have the money "by midnight," and food banks are scrambling to help people who got caught in the lurch. If you rely on food assistance or know someone who does, spread the word: payments are resuming, but it's still a stressful time. Here’s what Shapiro says.

Business, Shutdowns, and Layoffs

Tower Health is laying off 350 employees and closing the ICU and cancer center at Pottstown Hospital. More pain for suburban healthcare.

Meanwhile, on the upside, Philly’s own Maya Nazareth landed $300K on Shark Tank for her women’s fightwear brand. Score one for local entrepreneurs.

Sports: Watch for the Injuries

The Eagles are prepping for the Packers, but the week’s injury report is a laundry list—AJ Brown is questionable, lots of banged-up backs on both sides. What to watch for Monday night.

Sixers and Flyers: Joel Embiid and Jared McCain are available for the Raptors game tonight; Paul George is still out. Flyers’ Travis Konecny is finally heating up. Flyers update here.

Quick Hits

  • Market East makeover: After the Sixers’ arena fizzled, the city is asking for community input on what’s next for that stretch.
  • Cirque Dreams Holidaze is back, along with the Art Museum's craft show and a 110-foot Ferris wheel at the Zoo. Plan your weekend.
  • No new Philly Specials Christmas album this year. But you can get a Jason Kelce plushie for charity. Details here.

That's your Philly catch-up. Watch your routes and eat something good this weekend.
