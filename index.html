SEPTA Hikes, Eagles Chaos, and Philly TV Power Moves

Transit headaches, ugly-but-satisfying football, and Delco on your screen this fall.

🚆 SEPTA: Fares Up, Fingers Crossed for Service

Starting September 14, be ready to pay more if you’re still sticking with SEPTA. Fares are rising, and SEPTA says full service will come back—if Harrisburg lets them tap maintenance funds. Local pols are making a final push for transit money before the legislature comes back, but no one’s holding their breath. Read more on restored service hopes.

🦅 Eagles: They Won, But Yikes

The Eagles survived a messy, lightning-delayed opener against Dallas, 24-20. Jalen Hurts did Jalen Hurts things (no turnovers, clutch drives), but the defense nearly lost it, and Jalen Carter got ejected for spitting on Dak Prescott (apology issued). Ben VanSumeren is done for the season (torn patellar tendon), but the Birds are signing EDGE Za’Darius Smith for a needed defensive boost. Need all the tape breakdown, stars, and snap counts? It’s all here and here.

🚲 Cyclists vs. the City, Again

Protests rolled through town as bikers pushed back on Philly’s plans for Spruce & Pine. There’s momentum for safer streets, especially after a tragic Norristown crash. The city’s bike lane priorities aren’t impressing riders (or anyone dealing with Center City gridlock).
