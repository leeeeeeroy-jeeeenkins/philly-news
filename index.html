Philly Slush Pile: Snow, School Closings & Barstool Dining

Yesterday in Philly

Weather: Digging Out, Freezing Up (Again)

The city’s still shoveling out from the latest snow dump. Schools are remote again today and city offices are closed, with icy streets making cleanup a pain. Three people sadly died in Lehigh County while shoveling, a reminder to take it easy out there. Refreezing overnight turned slush into slicks—watch your step and your bumper. Plows are still making the rounds (check the progress here). SEPTA is slowly ramping service back up after major delays (details). Speed limits are back to normal, but travel’s still iffy. Bundle up: real-deal arctic cold is sticking around, and there’s potential for more snow this weekend (don’t shoot the messenger).

Eagles: OC Search in Overdrive

The Eagles are throwing out interview requests left and right for a new offensive coordinator; they’ve reportedly talked to Frank Smith (former Dolphins OC), Texans QBs coach Jerrod Johnson, and others (tracker here). Meanwhile, Lane Johnson’s long-term status is up in the air post-injury (what’s next for Lane), and draft pundits are already arguing about what the Birds should do at pick #23 (mock draft roundup).
