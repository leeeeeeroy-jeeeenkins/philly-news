Goodbye AIDS Walk, Hello Homicide-Free Camden

Yesterday in Philly – September 25, 2025

🏙️ City Moves & Messes

Mayor Parker’s got a new idea for Philly’s crushing rent problem: a tax break for developers building affordable rentals. The city says the shortage is only getting worse (shocker), so expect this debate to heat up fast.

Meanwhile, the city’s going after Bimbo Bakeries and S.C. Johnson—yes, the bread and Ziploc people—suing them for allegedly calling their plastic packaging “recyclable” when it’s apparently not. Philly’s trash situation remains: complicated.

If your commute runs through Manayunk, watch your step—huge sidewalk holes from that water main break are still wrecking Main Street weeks later, and businesses aren’t thrilled.

🌞 Weather Watch

Keep an eye out for Thursday storms—think downpours, gusty winds, maybe even a tornado.

🦅 Sports Check

Tough news for Birds fans: Nolan Smith, Jakorian Bennett, and Darius Cooper are all on IR. But the power rankings still have the Eagles looking strong going into Tampa Bay. Also, if you care about press box food (someone does), here’s your annual snack breakdown.

Sixers fans: The "Iverson Era" black jerseys are officially back this season. The fanbase gets one collective nostalgia trip.

And in baseball, the Phillies broke a bunch of home run records and locked down a first-round bye.

🎉 Neighborhood Stuff

After 38 years and $18M raised, AIDS Walk Philly is officially over. The city’s biggest longtime charity walk bows out, citing less demand and changing times. (Younger folks, take note: you can’t assume this stuff will always be around.)

Vare Rec Center just got a $21 million facelift—including a new turf field. Good news if you’re a Grays Ferry neighbor or have little ones who need to burn off energy somewhere other than your living room.

Manayunk’s Shiverfest and Boo at the Zoo are both back in October for fall/Halloween stuff. Free cider is probably involved.

🧪 Health & Caution

Heads up: the CDC flagged a bacteria strain that’s resistant to nearly all antibiotics, with some local cases reported. Basic advice: wash hands, don’t panic, maybe give the handshake a break.

🗺️ Quick Hits

  • That Hooters drummer you know: still making music, still more Philly than the Liberty Bell.
  • Netflix drops a new series on the 90s Philly mafia war—bring your favorite hoagie for binge-watching.
  • Camden racked up its first homicide-free summer in 50 years. That’s a real milestone.
