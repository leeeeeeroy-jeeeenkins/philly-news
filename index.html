SEPTA Cuts, QB Trades, and a New Union Deal

Good morning, Philly. Here’s the latest from a city running on soft pretzels, sports anxiety, and pure SEPTA frustration.

🚇 SEPTA: The Punishment Has Begun

If your commute felt longer today, it’s not in your head. SEPTA’s major service cuts officially started Sunday, slashing trains, buses, and express options. Riders called it “nightmarish” and a “sense of loss” (WHYY). If you’re a regular on the Sports Express train, hope you got one last ride—fans heading to Phillies games now have to plan ahead (6ABC). Full details on affected lines here.

📝 Philly Teachers Union Reaches Deal

Some rare positive labor news: Philly’s teachers union and the school district announced a tentative contract. No details yet, but it could head off a back-to-school crisis. More on the story from NBC10.

🏈 Eagles: QB Carousel Spinning Again

The Eagles cut their roster to 53 and suddenly started collecting quarterbacks like it’s fantasy football. After finding out backup Tanner McKee has a fractured thumb, Howie Roseman traded for Sam Howell from Minnesota (PhillyVoice). In the process, ex-Eagles legend Carson Wentz landed in Minnesota (PhillyVoice). Full roster updates and depth chart can be found here.

⚾ Phillies: Big Wins + Big News

On-field, the Phillies took the series over the Nats behind both Ranger Suárez and Aaron Nola’s strong outings (PhillyVoice). Off the field, mark your calendar for the 2026 Field of Dreams game versus the Twins in Iowa. Less fun: Zack Wheeler says he’s out for the rest of the season (Fox29).

🚌 Neighborhood Notes

  • After months of closure, Richmond Library in Port Richmond just reopened. Proper air conditioning and all.
  • Northern Liberties is getting another hangout: Lucky Duck, a new waterfront tavern aimed at millennials, is coming soon.
  • NASA’s planning a launch visible from Philly Monday night. Details and sky-watching tips here.

Quick Hits

  • Another fake active shooter call at Villanova (WHYY)
  • Violence continues: Three killed in overnight incidents, citywide (Fox29).

Stay cool, Philly—and check your next bus/train before heading out. See you tomorrow.
