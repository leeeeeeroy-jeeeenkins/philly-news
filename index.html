Yesterday in Philly: What You Missed

City Moves

Broad Street's About To Get Flashy: Speed cameras are now rolling out along Broad Street. For the next month it's warning tickets only, but real fines are coming. Pay attention to signs or prepare to donate extra to the city’s fund.

SEPTA: Less Service, Higher Fares: The 21.5% fare increase is officially biting, and those service cuts from the summer are sticking—for now. If your train or bus routines seem off, that's why.

Lemon Hill Park Glow-Up: Crews have started upgrading Lemon Hill Park to get ready for the massive FIFA World Cup Fan Fest in 2026.

Media & Culture

Kathy Romano’s Comeback: After getting ousted from Preston & Steve, Romano is launching her own morning show on B101. Expect direct competition and maybe some radio drama.

Art On The Parkway: The Alexander Calder Gardens—a new major art space—opened Sunday on the Parkway. Plus, Shepard Fairey is unveiling a new ‘Uplift Justice’ mural in Center City. Philly’s public art game stays strong.

Free at Noon Hits 1,000: WXPN’s long-running Free at Noon concerts celebrate their 1,000th show this week. It’s seen everyone from locals to a then-unknown Adele.

Sports

Eagles Handle Chiefs: The Birds bested Kansas City in an early season Super Bowl rematch (game analysis, position grades). Mukuba’s pick was a highlight (details), but the offense still looks just-OK. And yes, the NFL still can’t handle Hurts' “tush push” move (analysis).

Phillies On The Brink: The Phils’ magic number is down to one. NL East clinch could happen any day.

Sixers Jottings: Embiid is in town, and Quentin Grimes rumors are circling. Stay tuned for what actually develops.

Quick Hits

  • A $1 million lottery ticket was sold at a Sunoco on Cheltenham Ave. Might be time to check your glovebox.
  • Speed camera warning period means actual tickets start mid-October. Slow it down.
  • Wilmington’s Hispanic Day Parade ended in chaos—just a heads-up if you’re traveling nearby.
  • Philly Museum of Art is displaying its “not-quite-Vermeer” next to the real thing. Art nerds: it’s a moment.
