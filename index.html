Yesterday in Philly

City Council, Pierre Robert, and Some Paper Bag Drama

Goodbye, Pierre: Philadelphia lost a true legend—Pierre Robert, the voice of WMMR and a staple on our airwaves for over 40 years. Tributes poured in, including a moving note from Jon Bon Jovi (read it here), and on-air memories from Preston & Steve (listen in). Robert wasn’t alone in the headlines—longtime KYW anchor Harry Donahue also passed.

City Hall & Commute Watch

  • Paper or Plastic? The 10-cent paper bag fee just cleared City Council. It’s meant to cut down on trash and toss-away bags, but Mayor Parker might veto it. So, maybe keep stocking those reusable ones… just in case.
  • Remember the Filbert Street Zoo (aka the old Greyhound terminal)? You’ll be standing in line there again soon. The city and PPA are renovating and reopening it by 2026, in time for the USA’s 250th birthday bash.

The Law & The Streets

  • Posting wild stuff online? Careful: A Philly Mag journalist just won a landmark lawsuit against a Facebook commenter who got (legally) way too personal. Defamation online? Philly courts say, yeah, we see you.

Sports (and Not Just the Eagles!)

  • Sixers/Celtics tips off NBA Cup play tomorrow; Joel Embiid’s good to go, but Paul George remains out. Also, the team locked in Jared McCain’s contract for another year (details).
  • Eagles wanna know who new DB Michael Carter II is and how he’ll help the defense.

Suburban Smoke Shop Crackdown

  • Montco officials and DAs across the region are calling out smoke shops for selling what they say is straight-up marijuana (not legal) labeled as hemp (story here). Expect new rules or at least more news soon.

Quick Hits

  • SNAP Benefits: Federal food assistance cuts loom (here’s how some are helping), and Gopuff is stepping up to fill gaps. Find support or a food drive if you’re affected.
  • Need plans? Philly’s first horror bookstore opens in Manayunk (Thrillerdelphia), National Dog Show is back in Oaks, and Halloween events are popping all over (things to do).
  • Weather: Rainy start, but that should clear for a windy Halloween (forecast here).

That’s it for now. Save this for small talk and impress your neighbor at Wawa. See you tomorrow.
