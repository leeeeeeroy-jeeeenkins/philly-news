SEPTA Deals, Eagles Adjust, Rain Incoming: Just Another Thursday in Philly

December 19, 2025

Transit & Getting Around

SEPTA inked new contracts with three major unions, so no strike drama is looming. That’s good news for anyone who relies on the Transit Lifeline, a.k.a. the Broad Street Line, to pretend they’re still on time for work. Details here.

If you drive, I-76 in Manayunk was a mess for a bit thanks to an overturned dump truck, but the exit's already reopened. And if you’re in the Mummers mood early, just know road closures for the 2026 parade were already announced. File under: Philly priorities.

Weather Watch

Buckle up for heavy rain and strong wind Friday. If you have outdoor plans, you might want to rethink—or bring a kayak.

City Life

We’ve officially crossed over into Krampus territory. More Philly folks seem to be leaning into the “darker Christmas” thing—think mischief, less Hallmark. Curious? Read up on how Philly fell for Krampus.

Philadelphia hospitals are reportedly dealing with a surge in severe withdrawal cases tied to a powerful animal tranquilizer in the street drug supply. Medical folks are sounding the alarm before the holidays; stay sharp and make sure friends and family are safe.

Dining & Local Spots

Splashy new opening alert: Greg Vernick (yep, of Vernick Food & Drink) is launching Emilia, an Italian joint, in Center City. If you’re plotting a date or want pasta to impress your parents, might want to watch those reservations.

On the South Philly institution front, if you finally score a spot at Palizzi Social Club, Philly’s best chefs recommend the meatballs and braciole. Just don’t try to sneak your phone.

And for the artsy crowd: Grammy-nominated producer Will Yip just opened a new recording studio in South Philly, declaring us the capital of indie rock (details). Start that band already.

Sports & Local Drama

Eagles: Week 16 brings an injury shuffle—if you’re tracking Lane Johnson, Jalen Carter and company’s status for the Commanders, here’s a good rundown. Non-Eagles rooting guide and NFL picks here if you need fantasy league help.

Elsewhere: Phillies' relationship with Nick Castellanos is getting weird (story). Hot stove season is officially messy.

Quick Hits

  • The Kingsessing mass shooter was sentenced to at least 37 years in prison.
  • Philly man's alleged Bitcoin scam swindled investors out of nearly $50 million, per the feds.
  • Atlantic City Mayor Marty Small Sr. was found not guilty of child abuse charges.
  • Shopping for the weird and wonderful? Punk Rock Flea Market is back this weekend.
