ICY Side Streets, Coaching Shakeups, and a Krispy Kreme Meat Doughnut

East Coast storms, messy local politics, a few new Eagles rumors, and a serious water main break. If you blinked, here’s what’s worth knowing from Thursday in Philly.

Weather + Streets

Another icy blast rolled in but the real story is what it left behind: Frozen cars and iced-over streets all over Fairmount after a major water main break. That whole block looked like a street-art installation—just, colder and harder to get to your actual car. Also, more snow could brush by the city as the storm shifts east, though it might miss us (here’s what to watch). PSA: clean the snow and ice off your car before driving or risk a hefty fine—and everyone hating you (really).

City Drama

The Presidential House slavery exhibit mess downtown is officially stuck in the courts—the feds removed it, and now Philly’s arguing for its return. A judge ordered a new review but nothing’s coming back yet (more details).

Elsewhere, Philly business owners joined nationwide protests against ICE yesterday—watch for ripple effects if you’re in those communities (read here).

Eagles / Sixers: Who’s In, Who’s Out

The Eagles' offensive coordinator circus continues: Sean Mannion (former Viking) is reportedly your new OC, and former Bucs playcaller Josh Grizzard is taking over as passing-game coordinator. If you’re keeping score at home, that’s a big philosophy shift (read more). Meanwhile, Jalen Hurts is headed to the Pro Bowl after Stafford bailed (small consolation?) (link).

Across the street, Sixers barely took out the Kings last night. Winning ugly counts. Joel Embiid and Paul George (!) are both healthy, at least for the next game (for now).

Quick Hits: Out & About

  • Events this weekend: Murmurs, Philly Auto Show, Lunar New Year, and a literal naked comedy show are all on. Get the rundown.
  • New on the food beat: A Krispy Kreme-inspired MEAT doughnut exists in Fishtown, if you’re feeling brave.
  • Regal abruptly shut down the retro Cross Keys theater in Turnersville—more lost movie nostalgia (details).
  • Puppy Bowl announcement: NINE local shelter dogs made the cut. Go dogs!

Bonus: Philadelphia International Airport now has new tech so you can get back from vacation a lot faster (if you don’t mind facial recognition).
