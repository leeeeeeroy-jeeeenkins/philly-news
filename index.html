Yesterday in Philly

SEPTA Fires, Eagles Camp Opens, and a Push for Safer Streets

Transit: Never a Dull Moment

Yesterday’s big SEPTA story: a regional rail train caught fire at Paoli Station, injuring two people and causing major delays on the Paoli/Thorndale line. Meanwhile, the dreamers behind the Roosevelt Boulevard subway extension aren’t giving up, despite SEPTA’s looming budget cliff. Hope you weren’t planning on a simple commute anytime soon.

City Hall, Justice, and Safety

The city’s still reeling over violence toward unhoused Philadelphians; local leaders promised a crackdown after news broke that a Center City man was charged with assaulting six people as they slept. In other legal news, the man who killed Philly SWAT Sgt. James O’Connor was sentenced to 75 years in prison. Also, a rare case of internet vigilantes: a Center City man got lured and arrested by self-appointed "Predator Poachers".

SEPTA and Safety—Again

A fire on the rails wasn’t SEPTA’s only trouble—Temple Hospital nurses and staff are making noise about safety after last week’s active shooter scare. Across the board, the city’s feeling touchy about public safety in busy places right now.

Sports: Eagles Report & Trade Chatter

The Eagles opened training camp yesterday. New faces are getting buzz (Jihaad Campbell showed up strong; Nakobe Dean starts camp on the PUP list), and nobody on the team wants to talk about the Super Bowl anymore. Over in Sixers world, Jared Butler is heading west to the Suns. On the diamond, rumors are swirling with the Phillies interested in Steven Kwan, and some arguments that they should go all-in at the trade deadline.

Neighborhoods & New Stuff

If you’re looking for good news: The Art Museum is turning its iconic steps into a weekend beer garden (with food trucks). FDR Park needs help naming its new flock of heron-shaped pedal boats. And apparently, Kensington/Fishtown is now Philly’s wine country. (Don’t knock it till you’ve tried it.)

Quick Hits

  • Penn’s Weightman Hall is getting a $25M renovation, finally bringing it into this century.
  • Penn postdocs and research assistants voted to unionize, joining the national surge in academic labor organizing.
  • If you’re a stargazer: Perseid meteor shower viewing tips here—clear skies, fingers crossed.
