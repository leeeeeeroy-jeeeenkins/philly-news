Yesterday in Philly

Big Roster Moves, Mail Ballot Win, and the $100 Cheesesteak

Good morning. If you blinked yesterday, here’s what mattered in Philly:

ELECTIONS & CITY BUSINESS

  • Mail Ballot Relief: Big win for voters—a federal court ruled Pennsylvania can’t throw out mail-in ballots just because the envelope is missing a date or has the wrong one. That could make a difference come November.
  • Budget Drama: Chester County is stuck in the crossfire of the state budget standoff—millions in aid are on pause.
  • Center City Nightlife Shakeup: The city’s threatening to shut down Vinyl, the Midtown Village club from the folks behind Rouge. Trouble with city enforcement, details TBD.

SEPTA & COMMUTES

  • Transit Cuts Start to Bite: Commuters are definitely feeling the pain from SEPTA’s service cuts right now, and local leaders held a rally in Montco demanding more public funding (here's more).
  • I-95 Keeps Acting Up: More headaches for drivers: a multi-vehicle crash slowed southbound traffic near the sports complex, and an unrelated police investigation jammed things up in the Northeast.

SPORTS

  • Eagles Roster Watch: Final cuts are in—the Birds have big-time talent but depth is a concern. 2025 depth chart here. Full 53-man roster analysis here.
  • Phillies Bullpen Scramble: Joe Ross got released, and closer Jordan Romano’s now on the injured list. Plus, 2026 schedule is out—All-Star Game is in Philly.

STUFF TO KNOW (& EAT)

  • Cheesesteak, But Make It Extra: The Bellevue is selling a $100 cheesesteak that comes with a butler and a silver platter. No, this is not a joke.
  • Kimchi Festival Returns: Mark your calendars—the Kimchi Festival hits LOVE Park Sept. 27–28.
  • Best New Chips: Herr’s announced "Long Hots & Sharp Provolone" as the latest flavor contest winner, inspired by the roast pork sandwich. Sounds right for Philly.

QUICK HITS

  • Big love story: Taylor Swift and Travis Kelce are officially engaged. It’s all anyone will be talking about until the home opener.
  • FBI is looking for leads in a string of armored truck robberies across the Philly area. $10,000 reward if you know something.
  • Finally, if you ever wanted to say “I’d like to buy a vowel” on TV, 'Wheel of Fortune' auditions are coming to town for the first time since 2015.

That’s what you missed. Go have a great day—and if you see a $100 cheesesteak, take a photo for the group chat.
