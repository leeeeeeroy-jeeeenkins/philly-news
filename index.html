Union drama, bullpen blasts, and a (literal) beef heist

Good morning. If you missed yesterday, Philly was running on classic city energy — backroom deals, random meat theft, and some full-circle sports moves. Here’s what jumped out:

City Workers Union: Big Vote, Big Stakes

District Council 33, the city's largest municipal workers union, is set to announce the results of their contract ratification vote today. If members say no to the deal, Philly could be staring down a disruption in everything from trash pick-up to street repairs. This isn’t just inside baseball — if you use any city service, pay attention.

Bullpen Déjà Vu & Missing Meat

  • The Phillies can't quit David Robertson: The veteran reliever is reportedly back in Philly for round three (PhillyVoice), hoping to patch up a pen that just watched the team sandwich a monster win between a couple ugly losses (PhillyVoice analysis).
  • Meanwhile on Roosevelt, while a truck driver slept, someone made off with 40 boxes of beef from his rig. No, really. Philly’s got a meat bandit now. Police are looking for leads — hope your cheesesteak spot isn’t next.

Public Spaces & City Changes

  • Kensington’s Peace Park just opened with a new Mural Arts sculpture, made from disassembled firearms, honoring victims of gun violence. If you’re around the area, it’s worth checking out.
  • Mural Arts also installed a new mural remembering gun violence victims citywide (6ABC).
  • Curious (or maybe anxious) about the state of our school buildings? Here’s what you need to know about the School District’s facilities planning process.

Weather & Transit Watch

  • The humidity is finally backing off early this week (6ABC forecast). Go outside. Breathe. Live a little.
  • Heads up if you’ll be in Jersey — there was a major fuel spill at the Wildwood Wawa (800 gallons). If you’re headed to the shore, expect some cleanup delays.

Odds, Ends, and Philly Sports

  • The Eagles are giving away a replica Super Bowl ring. Yes, anyone can enter (you just need luck, not a strong arm).
  • If you saw a ton of spandex on the I-95 corridor, that was Ben to the Shore: thousands riding bikes for charity, including Brandon Graham.

That’s it for Monday. Watch the union vote, keep your brisket close, and check out those new murals if you can. Philly rolls on.
