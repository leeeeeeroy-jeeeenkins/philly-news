Yesterday in Philly: Massive I-76 Closures, Eagles Run Riot, Rent Watch

Mondays don’t mess around—here’s your city catch-up.

🚧 I-76 Traffic Nightmare Incoming

Start prepping now: a major stretch of I-76—five miles worth—is shutting down overnight for the next month, for a $41 million paving/bridge fix-up. Closures are rolling out between University City and Montgomery Drive most weeknights after 9 p.m. This will be a recurring headache, so plot your detours (or just avoid it outright if you can).

🏠 Renters: Yep, Corporate Landlords Own Philly Now

If you’ve lost out on a rowhome to an LLC lately, you’re not alone. A new report confirms corporate investors scooped up a quarter of Philly’s single-family homes sold in the last six years—one of the highest shares in the country. It’s making the rental crunch even crunchier. If you’re a first-time buyer or looking to move, it’s another warning that local families aren’t the only ones competing for keys.

🚨 Around Town

  • Mass shooting at Lincoln University: One person killed, six injured near student housing. Cops believe multiple shooters were involved. Still under investigation. Eyewitness accounts here.
  • Private security ready to strike: Security officers (think office towers, universities, shopping centers) are looking to authorize a city-wide strike soon. That could mean serious coverage gaps if a deal isn’t reached. Get the latest.
  • Sewage in the waterways: A new report picked out Philly and Camden as local sewage overflow epicenters during heavy rain. If you live near Pennypack, Tacony, or Cobbs Creeks, you’ve got some, uh, extra stuff in your water after storms.
  • SNAP benefits expiring: If you or someone you know uses SNAP in PA or NJ, brace for the Nov. 1 reset. Local pantries are bracing; donations and volunteers needed.

🍲 Eat, Drink, Actually Enjoy Philly

  • Griddle & Rice is winning over West Philly breakfast crowds with Indonesian comfort food.
  • End of an era: Pica’s Upper Darby closed its dining room after 70 years. Takeout remains.
  • Yarn bomb at Singing Fountain: Head to East Passyunk to check out the Halloween decorations (all knitted, obviously).

🏈 Birds & Ball

  • The Eagles ran wild on the Giants. Tank Bigsby looked like a cheat code and Dallas Goedert is suddenly having a career year. Third downs are still a problem, but a win is a win. Stat breakdown if you’re into the details.
  • Sixers early-season thoughts: The team’s leaning into three-guard lineups and (so far) it’s working. Quick analysis.
  • Phillies offseason rumor of the day: Walker Buehler—in or out?

That’s the city, for now. Check your I-76 route twice and support your corner store—corporate landlords aren’t the only ones who want to own Philly.
