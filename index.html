High Rents, SEPTA Delays, and Philly Sports Heartburn

Yesterday in Philly — the city that’s supposedly the worst to drive in and now apparently can’t even get Wawa a top spot. But hey, at least we’ve got baseball drama and a new steakhouse.

Transit Troubles & Annoying Closures

SEPTA Regional Rail riders got a dose of chaos thanks to emergency track inspections, which delayed and canceled trains all over the suburbs yesterday morning. Officials say the issues could stretch into more than just one day — so, find a backup plan if you can. If you’re trying to drive instead of SEPTA-ing: Congratulations. Philadelphia just got named the #1 worst city to drive in according to another national study. Shocker.

Meanwhile, Center City’s Vine & 9th intersection will be mostly shut down for months thanks to a major infrastructure project. Expect pain across Spring Garden and around Jefferson.

Housing: 400+ New Apartments Headed for NoLibs

Developers are coming for Northern Liberties with the second phase of Piazza Alta — 431 shiny apartments and a pile more retail means rents might keep doing what they do best (go up). Construction is underway, move-ins expected next year.

Money Woes: Iron Hill Brewery Bankruptcy

Iron Hill Brewery (yes, the suburban staple for burgers & flights) abruptly closed all locations and filed for bankruptcy. What went wrong? A messy expansion, high debts, and trying to compete with every brewpub in a 50-mile radius didn’t help. Sudden closings left a lot of jobs in limbo.

Health & Services: Philly's Only Rape Crisis Center Cuts Services

Philadelphia’s WOAR — the city’s lone rape crisis center — announced layoffs and major service cuts because the state still hasn’t passed a budget (and still isn’t paying providers). Survivors will feel the fallout first.

Sports: Phillies, Eagles & Big Ballpark LEGO Energy

Phillies dropped NLDS Game 2 to the Dodgers, with another late-inning rally that didn’t work. Analysis points to a questionable bunt call and a serious need for the bats to wake up. Meanwhile, Eagles fans are worried about whether the offense (and Hurts especially) is getting fooled by creative defenses.

If you need a sports pick-me-up: Legoland just added a giant “Durantula” LEGO at Citizens Bank Park to celebrate Red October (yes, really).

Quick Hits

  • Tired of Wawa worship? It got dethroned nationally by... Kwik Trip.
  • Philly’s Navy 250th birthday event brings boat parades, tours, and fresh road closures all week.
  • There’s a new steakhouse in Fishtown (Rockwell & Rose), doing dry-aged cuts with major Asian flavors. Bougie but worthy if you’re feeling flush.

That’s the city right now. Stay aware, stay caffeinated, let your friends know about the Vine Street closure.
