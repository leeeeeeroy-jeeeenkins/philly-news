Yesterday in Philly

SEPTA delays, bag fees, and some history on pause

Transit headaches. More fees. Construction shutting down a landmark. Here's what went down in Philly yesterday.

Transit Troubles & Commute Updates

SEPTA riders, brace yourself: signal issues are still making a mess of Regional Rail (“delays” and “cancellations” are the words of the week), on top of the leftover train car inspections that forced SEPTA to lease trains from Maryland. If you depend on Regional Rail, double-check your train before you leave.

Bags & Bucks: Policy Changes Coming

Start saving your tote bags: City Council just pushed through a 10-cent fee for paper bags at almost every Philly shop ( yup, even though the mayor isn’t a fan). Expect it to hit stores soon, so stock up now unless you love paying the nickel-and-dime tax for groceries.

History on Hold

If you have out-of-town visitors with a thing for history, you’ll want to tell them: Independence Hall is about to close for over two months for prep work ahead of America’s 250th in 2026 ( detour to the Liberty Bell, maybe?).

Bonus: Some local punks chucked bricks at the city’s 1702 bell outside Christ Church. Please respect the old stuff.

City & State Moves

Philly City Council recognized abortion rights as human rights in a nonbinding resolution – not law, but it’s a statement.

The full monthly food assistance payment (SNAP) is finally hitting Pennsylvania accounts again after a shutdown-related delay, so check your card if you’re waiting.

Neighborhood Notes & Good-to-Know

  • Speed Cams: Broad Street speed cameras are live; $100 tickets are real and already being sent out.
  • Winter Fun: Dilworth Park’s ice rink and Wintergarden are back if you need a seasonal mood boost (or cheap date idea).
  • Cultural News: The old Manufacturer's National Bank in Old City is turning into the ‘Ministry of Awe’ art space this March; watch this spot if you like immersive art shenanigans.

Quick Hits

  • John Fetterman was hospitalized after a fall related to a heart issue. He’s reportedly okay and recovering. (More from WHYY)
  • Sports: Sixers are banged up; Joel Embiid out, Paul George out for Friday vs. the Pistons. Expect more Tyrese Maxey.

That’s the latest. Hang in there, Philly.
