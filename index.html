Snow, Shutouts, and Philly Doing Its Thing

First things first: it snowed. A lot. Philly picked up over 3 inches (NBC10)—a proper winter sendoff with cold and biting wind. Schools will open late today (NBC10; Fox29), and if you drive, be ready for some icy, questionable intersections. Some power outages hit Sunday too (Fox29). Expect salted sidewalks, frozen-over bike lanes, and the daily chorus of "is SEPTA ever on time?"

Not surprisingly, travel was a mess. Flight delays stacked up at PHL (Fox29), and the city went into hunker-down mode (WHYY). If you see more snowplows and less street parking, that’s why.

Eagles Snap Out of It, Finally

Eagles flattened the Raiders 31-0 (NBC10; PhillyVoice), ending that ugly losing streak. Jalen Hurts bounced back with three TD passes, and Brandon Graham dominated—at a new position, no less (PhillyVoice). Between key turnovers and a defense that finally woke up, this one was never close. If you stuck it out at the stadium through the blizzard tailgate (6ABC), you earned it.

Elsewhere in Philly Sports

The Sixers are still holding their breath: Tyrese Maxey was ruled out for Sunday’s Hawks game (PhillyVoice), and Joel Embiid is iffy as well. The Flyers? They blew a couple close ones this week, but chalk it up to rebuilding (PhillyVoice).

Neighborhoods & City Hall

Cecil B. Moore Library in North Philly is at the center of a battle: local legislator vs. community, with the future of the library on the line (PhillyVoice). Elsewhere, City Council President Kenyatta Johnson laid out what he sees as Council’s job this term: less talk, more actually helping people (WHYY).

Quick Hits

  • Holiday scam season is here—double check before you give out any info (WHYY).
  • Allentown’s holiday light show got torched—literally—by vandals (Fox29).
  • Philly Ballet’s Nutcracker runs through the end of the month if you crave something festive (6ABC).

That’s the big stuff for today. Bundle up, clear your sidewalk, and check those travel alerts—winter came in swinging.
