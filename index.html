City Pay Bumps, Chinatown Money Drama, and All-Star Schwarber

Hey Philly, here’s what you missed yesterday: union raises at City Hall, weird federal funding pivots, local streets that (still) aren’t safe for your bike, and a reminder that Kyle Schwarber only hits home runs. Let’s go.

City Living & Political Moves

  • Raise for City Workers: City Council and Mayor Parker agreed to a contract with District Council 47 (the union for non-school white-collar city workers). It means pay increases and new health care perks. Parker says it keeps us “living within our means”—translation: don’t panic about your taxes (yet).
  • Chinatown Stitch Loses Funding: That big Chinatown project for wider sidewalks and more green space just lost its federal funding. No walkable utopia anytime soon. Future uncertain, with no fix in sight.
  • Traffic Gets No Safer: After two separate bike crashes near the South Street Bridge, neighbors are pushing for a new traffic pattern. Right now, it’s still the same old mess—so look out if you’re pedaling across the Schuylkill.
  • PPA Gets a Snitch App: The PPA rolled out a new app that lets you report illegal parking or street violations in 30 seconds. So if your block’s jammed, here’s your revenge button.

Weather & Getting Around

  • Flood & Heat Doubleheader: We started with major flood risk and are ending the week with dangerous heat. Hydrate—and prep for pop-up storms and possible SEPTA delays.
  • SEPTA Sideswipe, No Injuries: A SEPTA bus hit a police car in Fairmount. Nobody hurt. But, you know, stay alert on your commute anyway.

Health & Safety

  • Virtua and ChristianaCare Eye Merger: Two big health systems (South Jersey/Delaware) are talking a $6 billion merger. Not instant news for city hospital care, but could change insurance and specialist options for a lot of South Jersey/Philly folks.
  • LGBTQ+ Mental Health Support Change: The special LGBTQ+ suicide hotline extension ends Thursday. Local mental health pros are worried, especially for teens. Spread the word if you know someone who uses it.

Wins & Losses

  • Schwarber All-Star MVP: Kyle Schwarber swatted three homers to win MVP at the MLB All-Star Game in a wild new tiebreaker. The Phils’ trade rumblings are heating up. (Prospects are peaking, too.)
  • Fringe Fest: More is More: The 2025 Fringe Festival dropped its lineup—320+ acts, a record. Hope your your September is free.

One to Watch

  • A Philly man used Grindr to carjack people. Take care, especially when you’re meeting new people (and maybe lock your car).

That’s your Philly catch-up. Stay cool, stay smart, and see you tomorrow.
