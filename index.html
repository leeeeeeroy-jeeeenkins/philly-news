Market Street Closed, All-Star Logo Drops, and Philly Festivals Take a Hit

Alright Philly, here's what you missed — or what you'll have to dodge this weekend.

🚧 Street Closures & Festival Cancellations

If you’re trying to drive anywhere near Old City, plan ahead: Market Street is closing to westbound traffic between 2nd and 6th for more than three months due to construction. So, yeah, expect more of that classic Philly gridlock if you’re heading into Center City. Find a new route now—or just surrender and take SEPTA.

In festival news, the Northern Liberties Night Market was officially canceled, thanks to rising costs and new city requirements that have made street festivals nearly impossible to throw. Locals aren’t thrilled, but it’s just another L for Philly’s neighborhood hangs (and for anyone who has strong opinions about birria tacos).

🍕 Food Moves: Angelo’s Heads to Jersey, Jansen's 'Greatest Hits’

Legendary Angelo’s Pizzeria just announced its first South Jersey outpost, taking over the old DiNic’s in Haddon Township. For pizza snobs, it might actually be worth crossing the bridge soon.

Meanwhile, Mt. Airy’s Jansen is going out with a bang before its September closure: they’re switching to a ‘greatest hits’ tasting menu, so if you want to pay your respects (and eat well), now’s your shot.

🏟️ Sports: All-Star Game Heads Up, Phillies & Union

The Phillies dropped the new 2026 MLB All-Star Game logo at City Hall. Looks slick, and it’s a reminder: Philly’s getting the midsummer classic for the nation’s 250th birthday—book those overpriced hotel rooms now.

Union win streak is up to two as they chase the Supporters’ Shield, in case you prefer your sports with a little less heartbreak.

🏠 Housing, College, and Federal Drama

In Harrisburg, a Pa. bill was introduced to require full disclosure of flood history in home sales. After another round of flooding this summer, it couldn’t come soon enough.

Temple and other state schools are raising tuition. If you’re thinking about college or have a kid headed that way—bad news, but sadly, not surprising.

On the federal stage, public broadcasters like NPR, PBS, and WHYY are facing possible budget cuts after a House vote. If you love pledge drives, this is your moment to shine.

📰 Other Stuff Worth Knowing

  • ICE drama in Norristown: Residents confronted agents, calling them “2025 Nazis.” It got heated—watch this one, especially with the election news cycle spinning up.
  • North Philly gets its first History Festival this weekend. Expect panels, concerts, and real talk about the neighborhood’s culture and past.
  • Philly’s honoring Teddy Pendergrass with a street renaming soon. It’s about time.
  • Eastern State Penitentiary has a new educational rebrand—expect more focus on history, less on Halloween scares (but don’t worry, Terror Behind the Walls isn’t going anywhere just yet).

That’s it. Check your routes, savor your pizza, and keep an eye on City Hall. See you out there.
