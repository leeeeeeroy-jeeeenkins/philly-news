Yesterday in Philly: Teachers On Edge, Murals Everywhere, Eagles On the Clock

Back-to-school drama, new art on the walls, and the Birds finding out who actually wants to tackle this year.

🚸 Schools & Teachers: Strike Watch (Again)

Yes, it’s that time of year: Philly teachers could actually strike if the district and union don’t finally agree on a new contract. The current one’s up at the end of August and negotiations are, uh, tense. Meanwhile, some good news: freebies and discounts for teachers and students are stacking up around the city. If you’re prepping your supplies, check the list.

🏈 Eagles: Training Camp Battles & Big Needs

Football’s back, training camp drama is hot, and the Eagles have some real questions. Curious who’s fighting for a job? PhillyVoice has a full battle tracker (yes, RB2 is still a thing). Analysts are already recalibrating the Birds’ biggest needs—all eyes are on the secondary and edge rushers.

Over on the nostalgia front, Eric Allen was finally enshrined in the Hall of Fame, getting real about those Kelly Greens. Well deserved.

⚾ Phillies: Duran’s Debut, Bats Go Quiet

Reliever Jhoan Duran made a whole scene with his blazing debut and lit up the park, but the Phils couldn’t pull off the comeback vs. Detroit. Skubal outdueled Wheeler, and Harper got tossed. Next game’s another shot.

🏙️ City Moves: Murals, Protests & Rec Center Update

Two new murals dropped: South Philly now has a massive Dick Allen tribute and a fresh mural honoring abolitionist William Still and his family. Take a walk and check them out.

Meanwhile, activists rallied at Independence Park to protest attempts to remove the Presidents House slavery exhibit—another round in the fight over how Philly tells its history.

Out West, police arrested a suspect in that mass shooting at the Christy Rec Center (and yes, the center just reopened). The city’s still reeling, but parents and kids are showing up anyway.

🌍 Climate & Water: Jersey Shore, Delaware River, Three Mile Island

Activists say they’ll go all in to fight any federal effort to lift the fracking ban in the Delaware River Basin—that’s your drinking water, folks.

If you’re down the Shore, coastal towns are using recycled oyster shells for climate defense. Bonus: actual oysters fighting sea level rise.

Three Mile Island wants to restart, and locals aren’t thrilled—federal hearings and a lot of pointed questions about safety upgrades.

  • CHOP is keeping those virtual car seat checks for busy parents

That’s the latest. Summer’s not slowing Philly down. More tomorrow—catch you then.
