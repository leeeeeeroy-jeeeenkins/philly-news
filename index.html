SEPTA crisis averted, church fire in West Philly, and why your commute will survive (for now)

Welcome to Yesterday in Philly. Here’s what locals were talking about (and what’ll probably affect you personally):

🏙️ Getting Around: SEPTA Dodges Disaster

Yesterday, SEPTA finally got its wish: Gov. Shapiro OK’d a plan letting them dip into about $394 million reserved for long-term projects so they can keep buses, trolleys, and trains running at current levels for at least the next two years. It’s basically robbing Peter to pay Paul, but at least your bus isn’t about to disappear this fall (6abc). Just don’t expect big upgrades anytime soon.

🔥 Fire Rips Through West Philly Landmark

A big two-alarm fire gutted a vacant church on Market Street yesterday, sending smoke across West Philly. No injuries, but sad to see another old building get trashed. Neighbors are asking about next steps—the city doesn’t have good answers yet.

💸 Philly Chefs in Legal Trouble

Federal prosecutors charged a Philly chef with voting twice in the last presidential election. Meanwhile, the couple behind Catenacci Construction are now facing fraud and theft charges for allegedly taking money and not finishing jobs.

🚦 Commuter Chaos & Closures

  • Major crash shut down I-95 northbound in Bensalem for hours yesterday. Good reminder to check Waze before heading out.
  • SEPTA’s Chestnut Hill East line has finally reopened—sort of. Service is limited, but we’ll take it.

🏈 Sports Snapshots

  • The Phillies won another series against the Marlins, but Trea Turner and Alec Bohm both landed on the IL. They should be back for the postseason.
  • The Eagles’ next matchup is a Super Bowl rematch with the Chiefs. Birds are slight favorites; chaos and arguments about the tush push continue.
  • Tiger Woods was in town to open his Learning Lab at Cobbs Creek Golf Course, drawing a crowd in West Philly.

🧹 Quick Hits

  • West Philly is getting a big new cleanup program—look for crews out on 275 blocks through January.
  • Halloween Nights return to Eastern State Penitentiary on Sept. 19. Get details here if you like haunted stuff.
  • Kensington bar Martha is popping up in South Philly for one night. Would go just to see if the signature cocktails make the trip.
  • Oktoberfest season is coming, and here’s eight ways to celebrate from Kensington to Center City.
