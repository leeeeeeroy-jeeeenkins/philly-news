Yesterday in Philly

Sports, Strikes, Sizzling Fires, and Lanternfly Roadkill

Hey Philly, here's what you missed while you were dodging traffic and looking for a decent hoagie. Let’s get into it.

⚾ Phillies Playoff Watch & Eagles Updates

  • Phillies: October's coming and the weekend rooting guide says there's plenty left to play for—including getting past those Mets. Nick Castellanos kept it honest, basically telling fans: focus on locking down our spot, then the rest gets simpler (NBC10).
  • Eagles: The injury report for the Rams game is in—so far, mostly good news. Also, in case you head to the game, SEPTA is finally boosting service. No shade, but about time.

🔥 Big Fires and Broken Systems

  • Massive fire in Trenton: An 18-year-old student died, three were injured, and 22 now need a place to stay. A row of homes were wiped out—absolutely brutal (6ABC, FOX29).
  • Back in town, Temple Hospital nurses and staff voted to authorize a strike. Patient care impacts will be real if it goes ahead—watch this space.

🚦 City Moves & Neighborhood Shifts

  • Food Truck Clampdown: New curfew, same struggle—food truck owners say they're reeling after the city pushed through new restrictions.
  • Women’s Sports Bar Debuts: Marsha’s, the city’s first women's sports bar, is finally open in Midtown Village. Your group chat just got a new brunch spot.
  • Daytime Crimes: Society Hill neighbors are on edge after a run of daytime break-ins. Philly being Philly.
  • Lanternflies: If you hit the Shore, beware: thousands of dead lanternflies are washing up. They’re gross, but at least they don’t bite.

🍻 Dock Street Turns 40

Dock Street—Philly’s OG craft brewery—just hit its 40th birthday. Started with hippie ambitions, now it’s a city staple. Catch their story if you want a little local food history with your pint.

Quick Hits

  • Rob Van Dam is feeling feelings about ECW's Philly legacy.
  • Republicans are officially backing Stacy Garrity for governor (2026 campaign—it never ends).
  • If you're looking for something actually fun: Upper Darby’s International Fest brought the world to the suburbs this weekend.

That’s the stuff everyone’s gonna talk about at work. Go birds, watch those lanternflies, and pour one out for Dock Street.
