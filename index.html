Philly’s Art Museum Has an Identity, SEPTA Drama Grows: Yesterday In Philly

The museum finally changed its name, SEPTA’s got another lawsuit, and the Eagles/Giants preview is here. Here’s what you missed.

City Moves & Messes

  • It’s ‘Philadelphia Art Museum’ now. If you've called it that since you were a kid, congrats: you’re officially correct. The Philadelphia Museum of Art has rebranded as the Philadelphia Art Museum, dropping the “Museum of” and rolling out a new logo. Is it huge? Not really. Is it what everyone’s called it forever? Yes. You do have to start correcting your mom’s emails now, though (more details).
  • SEPTA: Lawsuits incoming. Local riders aren’t taking those fare hikes lying down—a lawyer just filed a class-action suit arguing the increases (and planned service changes) are illegal. That follows other legal challenges too. Plus, the transit union’s contract is about to expire, and SEPTA is prepping for possible strike drama. Just in time for cold mornings. Nothing is changing yet, but keep your eyes open.
  • Trash for cash? The city rolled out a voucher program to pay folks to dump their junk legally instead of illegally. Could this actually work? TBD, but good luck to the bold souls on cleanup duty.

Philly Life & Openings

  • Fond leaves Philly for Delco. South Philly’s beloved Fond is reopening in Delaware County’s Media, citing Philly’s endless headaches for small biz. Consider this your invite to hop the trolley next time you crave those pork belly buns.
  • Alex’s Pizza heads to the Shore. Got Roxborough roots? Good news: Alex’s Pizza is opening on the Ocean City boardwalk. Yes, swirl pies for all.
  • Philly Bike Ride returns 10/18. Mark your calendar: Center City streets go car-free for thousands of cyclists next Friday, ending with a festival at the (newly renamed!) Art Museum. Expect road closures and lots of folks in spandex.
  • Film Society Center reopens. The Upgraded Philly Film Center is back—new lobby, better snacks, another reason to see movies somewhere besides your couch.

Sports & Things To Know

  • Flyers honor Bernie Parent. Orange and Black will wear a memorial patch this year as a tribute to their beloved goalie—ceremony to come at the home opener.
  • Eagles-Giants week preview. Latest injury updates and what to watch. Saquon’s banged up; Jalen Hurts isn’t, but Philly’s O-line depth could get tested.
  • Sixers: Injuries and new lineups. Both Joel Embiid and Paul George are in "wait and see" mode; Quentin Grimes sounds more interested in a longer-term deal (he’s talking), and local voice Kate Scott will lead the WNBA broadcast team.
  • Phillies keep dreaming. Yes, the Phils are still alive after a clutch Game 3 win vs LA. Hang onto your rally towels.

Temps are cooling, rent isn’t, and the Ben Franklin Parkway is getting another name to screw up your GPS. See you tomorrow.
