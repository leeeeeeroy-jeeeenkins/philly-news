Yesterday in Philly

Winter T-Shirts Are Cancelled: Philly’s Cold Snap Continues

Yo Philly—hope you found your gloves. If you blinked, you might’ve missed a week that felt more January than late December, plus some big moves on the field, in Center City, and under those traffic cones you dodge daily. Here’s what happened yesterday:

Weather: Still Icy Out There

The cold isn’t letting up. Philly’s locked in its coldest December since 2010—no, you didn’t just get softer. Snow, freezing rain, and ice made Friday’s commute a mess. If you’ve got travel plans, check PennDOT's site for vehicle restrictions, and if you’re Jersey-bound, maybe just stay home. NJ is under a State of Emergency with more mess expected this weekend.

Transit & Travel

With all the icy drama, SEPTA didn’t get a break. A person was hit and killed by a train in Delco, so expect lingering delays. And if you’re flying, brace yourself: the airport saw serious backups thanks to the storm. Maybe just bunker in and finish your leftovers.

Sports

The Eagles face a rough Week 17—the injury list is long, but the Bills matchup is a must-win with playoffs on the line. Keep an eye on the Jalen Hurts–Josh Allen showdown; Barkley’s got a lot riding on this too. Also: looking back, the top Philly sports moments of 2025—in case you want to relive the highs before the emotional rollercoaster spikes again.

City Shifts: Center City & South Philly

In a surprise twist, the Sixers gave up on the Center City arena—they’re sticking put in South Philly, at least for now. Also, a Philly classic faded out: Macy's closed up shop in the Wanamaker Building. End of an era. (Sorry, organ fans.)

Holidays & Local Life

Not ready to kick the holiday mood? Plenty of seasonal attractions are still open—and yes, Christmas lights and ice rinks look even better when you can see your breath. Pro tip: don’t just trash your tree; here’s how to recycle it so it helps out city gardens (or even goats) instead.

One More Thing

Kwanzaa started off with celebrations of unity around the city. Check out what’s happening with local events if you’re looking for something meaningful (and warm) to do.

Stay warm, stay safe, stay Philly.
