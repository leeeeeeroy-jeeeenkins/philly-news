Yesterday in Philly

Yet Another Bird Win, Red October Incoming

Sports took over, Parkway was a party, and a couple bridges messed up the drive.

Sports: Philly Just Can’t Stop Winning

Eagles: The Birds are 4-0, but they took the long way. Philly held off the Bucs 31-25 in Tampa, surviving a late scare where the defense almost gave the game away (again). Standouts: a fake tush push for a TD, a special teams score, Jalen Hurts doing Jalen Hurts things—even though the offense still isn’t smooth, and the secondary left you sweating. Still, a win’s a win. For play breakdowns and who’s trending up/down, check the stock watch and pivotal plays from last night.

Phillies: Red October is officially on the calendar. The Phils closed the regular season with a walk-off win over the Twins (details here) and now set sights on the NLDS. Hype is already underway: the Rally for Red October bus tour is coming, so expect a citywide spike in red gear and lawn chair shortages.

City Scene

Parade Season: The Puerto Rican Day Parade put the Parkway on full blast—dancing, music, and flag-waving all over. It’s one of those Philly moments where everyone is out and the weather actually cooperated (unseasonably mild for late September), so all in all: vibes were top tier.

Getting Around: If you were headed over the Tacony-Palmyra Bridge early Sunday: lol, no you weren’t. A crash shut things down for hours. Add in a road rage shooting on the Vine Street Expressway and it wasn’t a great morning for drivers. Take Broad or hope SEPTA isn’t weird, as usual.

  • Over in University City, Dave the Helpful Hound is making rounds at HUP. Not a headline, just a solid dog story.

Stay tuned, stay loose, and wear red this week: it’s about to be wild.
