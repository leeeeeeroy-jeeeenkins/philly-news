Paper Bag Drama, Football Fallout & a New Donut Spot

Yesterday in Philly, October 21

City Hall & Policy

Politics got weird over bags: City Council wants to slap a 10-cent fee on paper bags, and the mayor’s not feeling it. The bill’s stalled again, with plenty of grumbling from councilmembers who want more answers. If you hate bringing reusable bags, stay tuned—this fight isn't over.

Schools & Surprises

A big high school football program is suddenly shut down for the season—Cheltenham High canceled all games after a hazing investigation. There’s an outpouring of frustration from players and parents. This story won’t fade fast.

Justice & Tragedies

In the Kada Scott case, city DA Larry Krasner announced more charges after the South Philly woman’s death (including arson and evidence tampering). Her family says their hearts are shattered and are demanding responses from officials abut bail policies. Krasner openly said bail decisions "could have made all the difference" (read here.)

Transit & Tech Mishaps

A national Amazon Web Services meltdown knocked out all sorts of online services, including Canvas (bad timing for local students). On the physical grid, a crash closed Exit 338 on I-76 in Bala Cynwyd for hours. Fun commute, right?

Food, Arts & Openings

Duck out of the midweek chaos and hit—yes—Honeysuckle, crowned the most important restaurant in the city (an actual must-read review). In sweeter news, Gather Food Hall opens in University City on Halloween, with a brand-new Federal Donuts counter and bar. Bonus: Bread Room is open and cranking out a wild variety of pastries daily. Carbs win again.

Meanwhile, Mural Arts is seeking an artist to depict queer history on a nightclub in the Gayborhood—get in touch if you or someone you know should be in that conversation.

Sports, If You Missed It

The Eagles squeezed out a win against the Vikings, but even they admit it wasn’t pretty (full breakdown and snap counts here). Trade rumors swirling, so watch that space. And rumors are true: Brandon Graham might be coming out of retirement.

On the hardwood, keep an eye on Sixers’ rotation drama as the opener looms.

Quick Hits

  • Peanut allergies in kids are down (thank you, early exposure recommendations—score one for CHOP).
  • SNAP benefits for November are at risk across PA unless the state moves—yep, that's a lot of families watching Harrisburg.
  • An insane amount of events are happening this week, including Halloween history at Pennsbury Manor and—no joke—a casting call for extras in a movie literally called Cheesesteak.

— Catch you tomorrow.
