Yesterday in Philly

Subject: Sinkholes, Songbirds, and a TV Invasion

Transit & City Life

Sinkhole season, apparently. Center City commuters ran into a new sinkhole that shut down part of 15th St. and forced a bunch of SEPTA reroutes. If you rely on the 2, 17, or any bus through Rittenhouse: expect detours until the city patches things up.

Swing bridge, almost ready. If you bike or jog the Schuylkill River Trail: the long-awaited swing bridge in South Philly is close to opening. Pretty soon, you’ll finally have a direct connection between Bartram’s Garden and Grays Ferry Crescent Park (i.e., less asphalt, more river views).

Chubby Chicks is out. Center City’s brunch spot Chubby Chicks is shutting down after ongoing drama with neighbors, harassment allegations, and a drawn-out public spat. If you need a new eggs benny fix, PhillyMag's Best of Philly date night list has options (some even brunchy).

Streets & Safety

Serious heat sticks around. Heat advisories are in effect through at least Wednesday. City pools are crowded, trash pickup is slow, and public works crews are moving gingerly. Conserve water if you can.

Carjackings & follow-homes: Multiple reports yesterday. A woman was carjacked while waiting at a West Philly gas pump, and in New Jersey, a couple was followed from the city, robbed at home. Just keep your head on a swivel coming from night out.

New faces on TV: NBC10 hired Kelsey Kushner to the anchor desk. More news, less cheesesteaks? We’ll see.

Civic Stuff

Five-year plan, approved. The state signed off on Philly’s budget blueprint, meaning more funding certainty for schools, rec, and cleaning crews. Don’t expect tax cuts, but do expect a little less chaos come budget season next year.

SEPTA layoffs dodged (for now): Federal funds are still iffy, but summer learning programs for kids are sticking around thanks to creative city/partner funding. No word yet on Youth Passes for transit, though.

Eat/Drink/See/Do

Date night inspiration: Philly Mag’s Best Of list is out, with 10 picks to actually surprise a date (think candle-making, tiki dens, rooftop cinema, etc.).

Tavern on Camac’s glow-up: America’s oldest gay piano bar just finished a major renovation. New look, same singalongs—worth a stop in Midtown Village.

'Abbott Elementary' goes meta: Quinta Brunson says the new season will actually film a live event in Philly. TV crews inbound soon; expect local cameos and street closure notices.

'Love is Blind' casting here: Netflix is coming for Philly singles. Time to see who’s really here for the right reasons.

Sports, Quick Hits

Phillies are busy, maybe too busy. They’re "annoying" the Twins with trade calls—bullpen arms are a focus. Also reportedly in the mix for Cardinals closer Ryan Helsley. Deadline is coming fast.

RIP Ryne Sandberg. The former Phillies player and Cubs Hall of Famer died at 65. The scene at CBP next home game will be somber.

Eagles training camp: It’s early, the offense is sloppy, but the defense looks tough. Fangio’s challenging Adoree’ Jackson, and battle tracker is heating up. Optimism, but as always in July: measured.

Stay cool if you can. We’ll be back tomorrow with more. –YIP
