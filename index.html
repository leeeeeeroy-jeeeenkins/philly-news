Yesterday in Philly

Subject: Triple L’s For Philly Sports, Nor’easter Incoming

What happened yesterday? Here’s what actually mattered.

The Philly Sports Hangover

It was a brutal night if you care about any local team: the Phillies’ season died in excruciating extra innings to the Dodgers (PhillyVoice instant take), the Eagles laid an egg against the Giants (instant observations, game ball + stars, 10 awards), and the Flyers opened their season with a flat loss (Flyers recap). Need to detox? At least hoops is around the corner: the Sixers’ G-League squad’s getting a new coach, but rookie VJ Edgecombe is now out for the preseason opener.

Weather to Watch

Keep an eye out for wind, rain, and all-around mess this weekend—a nor’easter’s rolling into Philly. Jersey already declared a state of emergency; expect possible coastal flooding if you’re Shore-bound. City folks: maybe don’t risk the umbrella.

City Hall Moves

Mayor Parker signed a landmark executive order on Project Labor Agreements—big news for anyone watching how city contracts get handed out. Also, officials revealed that the recent DC 33 strike set the city back $5.4 million. Labor people, keep taking notes.

  • Festival watch: The Kensington Derby & Arts Fest splashes back Oct. 25 (here’s what to expect).

Quick Hits

Hang in there, Philly. At least the Black urban rodeo is back in town.
