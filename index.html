Broad Street Gets a New Name, SEPTA's Warning, & Fishtown Burgers Incoming

Here’s what happened in Philly yesterday:

Transit Drama — Same Old, But Realer

SEPTA says it might have to slash bus routes the day before school starts this year, unless Harrisburg comes through with more money. So if your September commute or your kid’s back-to-school plans depend on a bus, stay tuned—this fight isn’t over. In slightly less dire news, serious crime on SEPTA is actually down thanks to more cops and better enforcement, but the system is still staring down a potential “transit death spiral” if funding doesn’t materialize.

City & Region — Name Changes and Traffic Woes

The city is finally giving soul legend Teddy Pendergrass his flowers—a stretch of Broad Street will soon bear his name. Elsewhere, after a string of bike crashes, neighbors are calling for a safer traffic pattern at South Street Bridge. (No word yet from Streets.)

Philly Cops & Courts

Former PPD officer Mark Dial, who shot and killed Eddie Irizarry last year, was sentenced to probation and released on parole. Protests and strong reactions from both sides started up almost immediately. Catch up on the sentencing and aftermath here.

Food & Openings

Burger alert: The chef behind Rittenhouse’s Huda sandwich shop is bringing a brand-new burger spot to Fishtown in August. Mark your calendars—Frankford Ave. is about to get even busier.

Sports & Losses

Some bittersweet Eagles news: 2017 Super Bowl champ and special teams favorite Bryan Braman passed away at 38 after a rare cancer . On the lighter side, training camp is coming, and so are debates over who’ll be this year’s offensive MVP. Plus: Five Phillies storylines to keep your second half spicy.

Quick Hits

  • ICE raids hit Norristown: At least a dozen people detained at a local grocery store.
  • Jersey archery range tragedy: One dead, 14 injured by sudden lightning strike—scary reminder about storm safety, even outside Philly.
  • Public broadcasting on the chopping block: Senate passed a bill to slash funding to PBS, NPR, and WHYY. None of these cuts are certain yet, but it's a warning shot.

That’s the big stuff. If you need something lighter: Two toddler tortoises at Philly Zoo just won the city’s heart. And if you’re in a reading rut, maybe try an alien romance novel? Philly has opinions.
