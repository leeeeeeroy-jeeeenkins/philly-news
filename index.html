SEPTA Strike Still Looms, Schuylkill Construction Hits, and World Cup Hype

Here's what Philly was talking about yesterday—so you're not the one asking what you missed at Wawa this morning.

Transit Woes (Maybe Delayed)

If you woke up prepping for SEPTA chaos, hold that thought—for now. Negotiations between SEPTA and the largest workers union are back on as of Monday, which means a strike isn’t happening yet. But unions call it “imminent,” Philadelphia schools are already warning of possible student disruptions, and 51,000 students could be stranded if things go off the rails. Maybe scout backup rides now, just in case.

Traffic & Construction Updates

Just in time for your holiday travels: PennDOT is starting that long-promised, months-long construction project on the Schuylkill Expressway (yes, the actual Schuylkill). Expect new lane patterns and night work between City Ave and Montgomery Drive. Plan for extra time—or find Zen.

West Philly: More Gun Violence

One man was killed and another injured in a double shooting Saturday night near 52nd and Girard in West Philly. No arrests yet, police are investigating. Stay safe out there.

World Cup Count-Up

The 2026 FIFA World Cup line-up dropped, and Philly’s hosting matches with loaded draws: expect to see Brazil, France, and England coming to town—plus a Union player or two expected to take the field. Schedules are still TBA, but the soccer hype is real.

  • A serious overnight fire in Kensington reminded us, again: check those smoke detectors.
  • A woman and two kids were rescued from a CO leak in Upper Darby; they're recovering, but another nudge to grab (and check!) a CO detector too.
  • Temple police threw an early holiday party for North Philly kids.
