SEPTA Funding Drama, Phillies Reboot & Weekend Vibes

Welcome back, bookworms and baseball nerds—here’s what went down yesterday in Philly:

Budget Battles & SEPTA Shenanigans

Governor Shapiro called out a Philly GOP senator over SEPTA’s shaky funding in the latest Pa. budget talks. Meanwhile, GOP state senators dropped their own plan to make SEPTA more accountable, but—predictably—didn’t actually promise more cash. SEPTA’s future (and your commute) is still on the struggle bus.

Phillies Overhaul: Trade Deadline Jitters

If you missed it, the Phillies shook things up at the trade deadline—bringing on Jhoan Duran (pitcher) and Harrison Bader (outfield). Johan Rojas and Daniel Robert got bumped to make room. Analysts ">debated if these big moves were enough: some liked the upgrades, some wanted more splash. Either way, the Phillies and Mets now stare each other down—separated by half a game.

Oh: Jimmy Rollins and Ed Wade landed on the Phillies Wall of Fame, too. Give it up for the legends.

Eagles: Training Camp Heating Up

Young defensive linemen are making moves at Eagles training camp (even as Saquon Barkley keeps getting national attention for everything from running to random Trump appointments). If you love depth charts, track the latest roster battles

Education & Philly’s Money Problem

In case you missed it: Philly colleges are falling behind in college sports’ big-money arms race. Money talks, and right now, local schools are playing catch-up in a world dominated by bigger budgets elsewhere. Long story short: don’t expect major upsets anytime soon.

Staying Afloat

PECO is offering $500 grants to folks behind on electricity. If your A/C is running up the bill, worth checking out.

Local Life: Bookstore Crawl & More

  • Philly Bookstore Crawl is back: Nearly 50 shops offering deals, events, and more this weekend. Grab a map and go browse the stacks.
  • Lansdowne Theater is reopening for the first time since 1987. Feels like a win for Delco and old-school movie lovers.
  • For the good: Over $500,000 raised for a South Jersey playground in memory of the Gaudreau brothers.

Quick Hits

  • Philly’s gun bump stock ban got upheld in court (rare win for local gun laws).
  • Delaware: Recreational weed sales officially started (here’s what you need to know).
  • A new video game set in 'Philabieldia' is coming—yes, there are jokes about Wawa and “Always Sunny.”
  • Unseasonably cool weather is here, and the weekend looks gorgeous.

That’s the gist. Don’t forget to hit a bookstore, try not to get stuck on SEPTA, and go outside—before August turns angry again.
