SEPTA’s Money Problem, Name Changes, & Rent Rage

Yesterday in Philly

SEPTA’s Budget Black Hole Is Now Real Life

Here we go: Lawmakers blew the deadline to fund SEPTA, so cuts and fare hikes are moving forward. If Harrisburg doesn’t cough up real cash soon, we’re looking at major bus and rail service drops (timeline here), plus an extra 275,000 cars on the roads by next year. This is the ‘death spiral’ officials warned about. Expect fewer trains, more crowding, and longer commutes unless something changes.

Krasner Goes After Trump (Again)

Philly DA Larry Krasner had some, let’s say, colorful words for Trump on the idea of sending National Guard troops here in 2025. The message: “Don’t even think about it.” There’s also movement in Harrisburg to block federal takeovers of local police (read the proposal)—clearly, nobody’s in the mood for outside interference.

When Rent Hikes Hit Home

Out in Montco, a proposed 50% rent increase has a 55+ community ready to storm the leasing office. Turns out housing drama doesn’t care about age. Meanwhile, FDR Park activists get another day in court to keep their fight alive over park changes.

Sports & Arena Stuff

  • Sixers fans: the 2025-26 schedule is out. Home opener: Paul George’s real debut at the newly named Xfinity Mobile Arena (yep, that’s Wells Fargo Center’s new title; signage is up).
  • Phillies update: Aaron Nola back Sunday, moving to a six-man rotation. They need the help after another rough series.
  • Eagles’ Myles Garrett demolished practice yesterday (here’s your camp recap). Bonus: Lane Johnson is hyped about Moro Ojomo ("real deal," apparently).

  • Looking for Zahav reservations? Michael Solomonov and Steve Cook talk shop and spill secrets (kind of).
