Yesterday in Philly

West Philly flashpoints, armored truck drama, and a jolt for Eagles fans

The Big Stories

West Philly, Ground Zero for Gentrification Fights: The battle over who gets to shape Baltimore Avenue is in the spotlight. This Philly Mag piece digs into how one beloved West Philly store became a symbol of everything locals love—and fear—about neighborhood change. Not just another ‘change is coming’ story; this one’s about who gets pushed out when “up-and-coming” is someone else’s payday.

Kada Scott Case Fallout: Philly DA Larry Krasner is publicly regretting dropped charges in the Kada Scott disappearance and kidnapping case. Turns out, suspect Keon King had a stack of old charges; the DA’s office says keeping him off the street might have changed everything. More background here (and yes, it’s messy).

Armored Truck Robberies Keep Happening: Two men were arrested in connection with multiple armored truck heists across Philly—one even had the guts to try outside a Wawa yesterday. Security guards fired back, and the would-be robbers didn’t get away with any cash, but still: not exactly comforting for the morning coffee run. Full story.

SEPTA Trouble, Again: Yesterday wasn't great underground. A man was shot and killed after a fight on City Hall's SEPTA platform; the suspect turned himself in, but expect crowding and delays as police investigate. Meanwhile, SEPTA’s facing a federal deadline for older rail car inspections—which, if missed, could actually mess with Regional Rail schedules.

City Life

Restaurant Reservation Drama: City Council wants to keep people from snagging (and ghosting on) Philly’s hottest restaurant tables. Talk of a crackdown on reservation scalping and no-shows is real. If you’re waiting two months just for a dinner spot, you’re not alone.

Home Prices? Still High: Prices for homes keep climbing in the Philadelphia region, making life harder for buyers (and boosting the confidence of your landlord, probably). Nothing new, but it’s making more noise lately.

Rite Aid Shuffle: With Rite Aid’s woes, the chain is moving prescription records to nearby pharmacies. Check ASAP where yours got sent—you don’t want to find out at the counter next week.

The Fun Stuff (and Sports)

Eagles-Vikings Sunday: The Birds have to avoid a three-game slump with a banged-up roster and (wait for it) Carson Wentz starting for Minnesota. Yes, really. Lots more breakdowns and betting odds here.

Sixers Update: Joel Embiid is finally back on the floor for preseason tonight; plus, watch for shakeups in the Sixers ownership group leadership, with NBA exec Bob Myers possibly joining.

  • Weekend plans? Insomnia Cookies is landing at PHL Airport for red-eye sugar hits.
  • Lots of spooky, weird, and kid-friendly fall events this weekend. Also: road closures and parking restrictions for the "No Kings" rally on Saturday (map here).

Alright, that’s the news that’ll actually come up at your next happy hour. See you Monday.
