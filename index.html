Yesterday in Philly

City Council Gets Serious, SEPTA Hits Pause, and a Whole Lotta 'Firsts'

Big Moves at City Hall

After the tragic killing of Kada Scott, City Council says it’s time to reexamine how Philly handles domestic violence. Expect policy hearings and calls for tighter oversight. The medical examiner confirmed Scott was fatally shot, and the community is frustrated about unsafe vacant houses and a system that didn’t protect her (more here).

Elsewhere: Philly might not be done with bag fees. After the plastic ban, City Council is floating a 15-cent fee for paper bags. So, either bring that reusable tote or keep funding the city’s future, one hoagie bag at a time.

SEPTA’s Budget Blues

SEPTA’s trying to keep trains and buses running with a shrinking checkbook. State budget drama drags on, so they’re delaying new bus orders and construction to avoid cutting service—for now. If you care about less-crowded, maybe-not-disgusting buses, you’ll want to watch where this goes.

A Long Year of 'Firsts' for Philly

America turns 250 in 2026, and the city is rolling out 52 weeks of 'Philly firsts'—from the first zoo to the first black-owned bookstore. Full event lineup here. If you’ve got history fatigue, consider it: one event per week, not all at once. Manageable, plus an excuse to get out and maybe learn something.

Quick Hits

  • Anti-Dumping Crackdown: Philly’s finally getting serious about illegal dumping. More surveillance, more fines, new task force. Maybe fewer mattresses and toilets on your block?
  • Local Win: Fairmount’s Alchemize Fightwear just scored $300k on 'Shark Tank'. Philly founders = crushing it.
  • Cats Rescued in Delco: Nearly 70 cats saved from hoarding, owner now facing charges (details).
  • Weather: Bundle up—frost advisory tonight.

Sports Corner

  • Sixers: Tyrese Maxey and VJ Edgecombe went off in a wild opening night win. Edgecombe’s historic debut has fans hyped.
  • Eagles: DeVonta Smith is catching everything (quietly), and Brandon Graham's out of retirement and back to trash-talking. Fans: the Eagles-Giants rivalry nostalgia content is strong this week.

One Last Thing…

Christian McBride (Philly jazz legend) is out with a new album, and yes, he’s got some side quests: playing for James Brown, still chasing that Dolly Parton collab. It’s a good read if you need some musical joy on a dreary day.
