Yesterday in Philly

Quick catch-up on what went down across the city — the stuff you actually care about, not just headlines for headline’s sake.

Bites and Big Openings

  • Philly had a food flex: Three local restaurants — Kalaya, Friday Saturday Sunday, and Royal Sushi & Izakaya — landed spots on North America’s 50 Best Restaurants list. Philly hasn’t seen this kind of national recognition in years.
  • More buzz: Jesse Ito’s Dancerobot drops the opening date. If the Fishtown sushi king’s new spot is half as good as Royal, people will be camping out front. Get the details.
  • Drinks, anyone? Tria opened a Rittenhouse event space for wine tastings and classes taught by actual sommeliers. Here’s the lowdown.
  • Not all food news is good: Iron Hill Brewery suddenly closed all 16 of its locations — if you’ve got a gift card, good luck.

Streets, Transit, and City Moves

  • The Parkway’s future (for real this time?) The century-old dream of making Ben Franklin Parkway less of a highway, more of a public space might actually happen. Massive redesign is back on the table — see what’s in store (and, yes, expect road closures this weekend).
  • SEPTA legal drama (again): A Philly lawyer is suing to stop fare hikes and service cuts, claiming SEPTA’s top brass haven’t done enough to fix what’s broken. Can he win? Stranger things have happened.
  • City Council moves on second chances: New hiring protections for jobseekers with criminal records are now official. Details here.

Neighborhood Notes & Good Stuff

  • Immigrants are keeping the region afloat and growing the economy, according to a fresh report. Read more if you want to see the numbers for yourself.
  • Art alert: The Fabric Workshop and Museum is showing new tapestries and wild sculpted heads from Swedish artists. Check it out — nice way to spend a rainy hour.
  • Ever think about where all that stadium grease goes? One Philly company recycled over 150,000 pounds of Eagles' cooking oil last year. That’s, uh, a lot of wings.

Sports — Quick Hits

  • Birds-Buccaneers drama on deck: Injury updates, painful Tampa flashbacks, betting odds — it’s all here.
  • Sixers: Some preseason hype from Media Day, but rookie Jared McCain’s out 4-6 weeks with thumb surgery. More here.
  • The Phillies topped Miami, but there’s still reason for a little worry before the postseason. Read why.

That’s it. See you out there. If you missed anything, here’s a quick audio recap.
