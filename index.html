Yesterday in Philly: Big Crowds, Hard News, Fresh Drama

Thousands Rally Downtown, Fewer Answers for Kada Scott

Center City was packed Saturday as thousands showed up for the second day of ‘No Kings’ protests against Trump outside Independence Mall and all over the country. Expected: huge signs, speeches, plenty of energy. Some roads and parking were a mess — classic protest aftermath. Philly’s usual political divide was on full display, but the city stayed mostly peaceful. Here’s which roads were closed, in case you’re planning your Sunday.

Meanwhile, the search for missing Philly woman Kada Scott took a tragic turn. Human remains were found Saturday in North Philly, and though police haven’t confirmed the ID officially, family and multiple sources say it’s likely Scott. The search nears two weeks. There’s now a $12,000 reward for information. More perspective from family here.

Transit + Neighborhood News

Another weekend, another SEPTA headline: a man was shot and killed at a busy Center City station, and a suspect’s already facing murder charges. The city’s still hashing out what safety on public transit should look like — but yeah, it’s tense.

In more positive city moves, the Philly Land Bank just bought 35 garden lots. This is not just a gardening nerd thing: Land preservation means more green space and fewer surprise developments next to your block. Win for neighborhoods.

Sports: Returns, Rookies, and Future Draft Dreams

The Sixers chopped down their preseason roster, waiving four players (details). But real news: Joel Embiid returned from injury for the preseason finale and looked promising — bringing hope and a little relief to a Philly fanbase that really needs it. New rookie VJ Edgecombe made a splash in his home debut, showing flashes you’ll want to keep an eye on once the games count.

Across the street, Cam York is back in the Flyers’ power play mix after missing time. If you believe in baby steps for a rebuild, this is one.

Eagles-wise: injury watch is in effect ahead of Vikings week. Jalen Carter and Quinyon Mitchell highlighted on the latest report. Also, if you’re already sick of the current Birds’ drama, here are five college guys to look out for in the 2026 Draft.

Other Stuff You Might've Missed

  • Cheltenham High School suspended its football season over hazing claims. Not just about sports — this one’s got the whole school community talking.
  • Over in West Norriton, a shootout at a Wawa involving rival biker gangs left at least six injured.
  • And volunteer crews gave some love to Memorial Grove, a unique space honoring young lives lost in the city.

That’s the latest. See you out there.
