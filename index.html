SNAP Panic, SEPTA Delays, and Philly TV Drama

Food Aid Crisis & Local Response

SNAP benefits in Pennsylvania are on life support while D.C. sorts out the funding mess. Governor Shapiro declared a disaster emergency and pledged $5 million in emergency state funds for food banks. If your EBT card's not getting refilled, some Philly-area restaurants and grocers are stepping up with free meals (see this list) while the courts battle out a more permanent fix. Not a great look, but at least the local response is real.

SEPTA: Still Not Running Like Clockwork

More Regional Rail delays, naturally. SEPTA just got two extra weeks to finish inspecting those ancient Silverliner IV cars after recent electrical fires (here's more). If you rely on the trains, build in "buffer time" until further notice.

Council's Doing What Now?

City Council is kicking around a new retirement program for non-city workers. Translation: Maybe a way for gig workers, contractors, and small business employees to save for retirement, but it's early days and will need oversight.

TV Blackout & Streaming Woes

Disney yanked 6ABC and a bunch of other channels off YouTube TV while they argue about money. No more Action News or Eagles games via stream for now, so start prepping your rabbit ears.

UPenn Email Freakout

Got a wild, angry email from UPenn? It wasn't real—a fraudulent blast went out to students and alums, leading to a security scramble. The university is on it, but change your password just in case (more here).

Philly Sues Big Pharma Middlemen

The city just sued pharmacy benefit managers (think CVS, Optum, Express Scripts) over their role in the ongoing opioid crisis here. Public health and budget headaches—this could take years to play out but it's good to know who's being dragged into court.

Quick Hits: What Else Happened?

  • Local media's going through a weird, experimental phase (again).
  • Greyhound's Center City bus terminal is reopening by May 2026—for anyone longing for that "Port Authority but sadder" vibe downtown.
  • New mural alert: Germantown school honored abolitionist Frances Ellen Watkins Harper with art on her 200th birthday.
  • Cirque du Soleil's big tent is back if you like your acrobatics extra whimsical.

Sports in Brief

  • Sixers staged a rowdy comeback win over the Wizards, and the NBA Cup is about to start (what's that?—details here).
  • Flyers are in their "change things up" phase (watch Zegras).
