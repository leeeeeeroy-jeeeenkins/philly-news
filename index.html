Market East Déjà Vu, SEPTA Snags, and an Old-School Sixers Win

Yesterday in Philly – November 10, 2025

Market East Drama: Same Story, New Cast?

Mayor Cherelle Parker says her plan for Market East is "open to input." But if you lived through the Sixers arena fight, you know a PR tour doesn’t exactly mean a true listening session. Residents are already skeptical, suspecting rough drafts and big promises might end with more of the same: projects that put landlords and developers first, and neighbors' worries last. Expect public meetings to get tense—again.

SEPTA: Start Your Monday Early (Or Not)

Heads up: SEPTA has already canceled a bunch of Regional Rail trains for the Monday morning commute. No cause given (classic), so if you rely on these lines, double-check schedules or budget extra time. It’s going to be one of those Mondays on the platform.

Sixers: Embiid Sitting, Throwback Vibes, Watford Shines

  • No Joel Embiid for Sunday’s Pistons game (resting on a back-to-back). Don’t panic.
  • But Trendon Watford dropped a triple-double in his first Sixers start. Not a typo. Retro jerseys, retro court—so if you love nostalgia and underdog stories, this squad is your vibe.

Winter Incoming (and FAA Flight Mess)

A cold front is on the way—classic La Niña, expect colder days soon. If you were thinking about getting that winter coat out of storage, now’s the time. Also, air travel is a mess: FAA cutbacks are canceling hundreds of flights, so check your Thanksgiving plans if you were counting on an easy getaway.

Quick Hits

  • ByHeart baby formula is under recall after botulism-causing bacteria was found in tests. If you use formula, double-check your stash.
  • SNAP recipients are worried after federal demands to roll back emergency food benefits. Local advocates warn that families are already stretched thin.
  • Week 10 NFC rooting guide is out if you want to do the weird playoff math for the Eagles’ run (and hate-watch a few rivals).

That’s the gist. Mondays are about to get colder, commutes sloppier, and the Market East talk louder. Stay tuned—and dress warm.
