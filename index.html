Injuries, Trade Talk, and SEPTA Surprises

Hey. Here’s what went down around Philly yesterday. This one’s got Eagles rumors, a Kensington raid, and a real-life SEPTA hero.

Eagles: Everyone’s Talking

  • Eagles owner Jeffrey Lurie says the A.J. Brown trade rumors are overblown (even though basically every fan is holding their breath). Don’t expect a blockbuster, but don’t turn off your phone either.
  • Meanwhile, the latest injury report isn’t ideal: Brown was questionable but is expected to suit up; Cam Jurgens is likely out.
  • If you want to look way ahead, here are five college prospects who could be Eagles targets in the 2026 Draft. (That’s not a typo.)

Elsewhere in Sports

  • Over in baseball, Bryce Harper is still elite, according to the Phillies brass—if he stays healthy.
  • The Sixers-Hornets preview asks: Can LaMelo Ball be the dude? (And do the Sixers have a real shot at consistently winning games that matter?)

SEPTA Follies & Local Drama

  • If you thought SEPTA couldn’t get weirder: Video caught a woman falling on the Market-Frankford Line tracks, and a bystander pulled her up seconds before a train rolled in. Seriously, shout out to that neighbor.
  • In less-heartwarming news, screws were left on driveways of houses with certain political signs in Abington. Because we can’t even have a yard sign war without someone getting weird about it.

Crime & Safety

  • FBI indicted 33 people after a big Kensington drug operation—so expect visible law enforcement around there this week.
  • One dead, several wounded after a shooting at Lincoln University in Chester County. No word yet on a motive.
  • A driver in Plymouth Twp faces attempted murder charges after hitting officers in a hotel parking lot. Thankfully, both will recover.

Neighborhood Stuff

  • If you’re in Upper Darby: Pica’s Restaurant is serving its last dine-in meal on Sunday. Takeout will still be a thing. Long live the square pie.
  • Philly high schoolers just got a boost: the "Launchpad" program aims to launch more students into tech careers. Actual workforce programs in the pipeline.
  • Weather: Not a cloud in sight. Enjoy it while it lasts.
