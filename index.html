SEPTA Strike Threat, Eagles Survive, Holiday Markets Bloom

Transit drama, tough wins, and Philly gift shopping season.

Transit Headaches: SEPTA Union Authorizes Strike

If your weekday mental health is tied to SEPTA, brace yourself. The transit workers union just voted to authorize a strike, right as Regional Rail lines are already a mess with cancellations. No walkout date set yet, but this could disrupt your commute—again. If you rely on Chestnut Hill West or Fox Chase, double-check schedules. Just in time for holiday crowds and marathon traffic.

Birds Survive, But Lane Johnson Down

Lots of defense, not much dazzle. The Eagles held off the Lions 16-9 in a game only a defensive coordinator could love. Three things to know: the Birds' D carried the day, Nakobe Dean and Jordan Davis stepped up, but right tackle Lane Johnson has a Lisfranc injury and will be out 4-6 weeks. Not great ahead of the playoff push. Oh, and Jalen Hurts managed one touchdown on an otherwise rough night for the offense.

Sixers: Paul George Debut Tonight

Elsewhere in sports, the Sixers officially welcome Paul George for his season debut—against his old team, the Clippers. Revenge game vibes incoming. For basketball analysis nerds, more details here.

Holiday Markets, Historic Streets, and Michelin Hopes

Gift season is officially here: Holiday markets are popping up all over town with handmade stuff, art, and plenty of mulled wine. Old City’s Historic Holidays returns with open-street shopping and tree lightings; Christmas Village is back too if you need your annual bratwurst. Also, Michelin inspectors are poking around: see which Philly restaurants might finally score a star.
