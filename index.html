Nor’easter Soaks the Shore, Center City IHOP Shooting, Sixers & Storm Updates

Yesterday in Philly: Let’s start with the stuff you probably noticed: rain. A coastal storm drenched the region all weekend, bringing more headaches for anyone headed toward the Jersey shore. Flooding and strong winds took out the Atlantic City Rail Line, forced tweaks to NJ Transit service, and even prompted a State of Emergency in the Garden State. Philly stayed mainly soggy but avoided the worst.

Headlines You Actually Care About

  • Center City IHOP Turns Deadly: A security guard shot and killed a customer inside the Walnut Street IHOP around 2 a.m. Police are investigating, but what happened and why isn’t fully clear yet.
  • Sunday Morning Violence: There was also a deadly shooting in Southwest Philly leaving a woman dead, a man injured in another incident, and a parking lot shooting at a South Jersey bar under investigation.

Transit & Commute

  • NJ Transit: If you cross the river for work, double-check your train. Flooding suspended the Atlantic City Rail Line and several NJT bus routes detoured. Details here.

Sports, Because This Is Philly

  • Sixers: Joel Embiid played live in public for the first time since February at the team’s open scrimmage. He looked solid—no knee drama, just some rust. Coach Nick Nurse called it “a good progression day.” Also, Sixers are expected to sign MarJon Beauchamp to an Exhibit 10 contract (think: tryout plus G League option). If you’re keeping tabs on the bench, Dominick Barlow is making a case for rotation minutes. Andre Drummond shot some threes, for what it’s worth.
  • Flyers: Not exactly the home opener vibes we wanted. A controversial overturned goal and another OT loss. Matvei Michkov didn’t get on the ice in overtime, which is probably not something you want to see if you’re hoping for young talent.
  • Penn State: If you pay attention to college football, it’s official: James Franklin is out as head coach after a pretty rough start for the Nittany Lions.
  • Eagles: No game, but you can check out who to root against this week, or if you want draft drama, see the Bryce Huff sack tracker.

Odds & Ends

  • Philly Music Fest 2025 is moving to a new venue, with back-to-back shows still championing local acts. More here.
  • Climate Watch: The Delaware River’s shad population is getting hammered by climate change and invasive species. If you care about fishing (or just shad sandwiches), keep an eye on this.
  • No mail delivery today (Monday). It’s a federal holiday—plan accordingly. Details here.

That’s it. If you stayed in, you missed rain, some chaos, and the faintest hope from Embiid’s knee. See you tomorrow.
