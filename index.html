Iron Hill Vanishes, Phillies Surge, and Philly Keeps It Weird

Yesterday in Philly - September 26, 2025

The Big Stuff

Iron Hill, Out of Nowhere, Is Gone
Iron Hill Brewery—the Delaware-born chain with 15 locations around Philly and the ‘burbs—abruptly closed every single spot yesterday and filed for bankruptcy. If you had a loyal mug or gift card, well, RIP. No word yet on what will happen to those giant, sprawling prime locations, but this one came outta nowhere.

Phillies Bash Their Way to NLDS
The Phillies clinched a first-round bye and are headed to the NLDS—and in style, setting a team record with eight homers in one game. If your group chat wasn’t blown up by “LET’S GOOOO” and dancing Bryce GIFs, are you even from here?

Civic Upheavals & City Hall Moves

City Council: New Protections for Returning Citizens
City Council just expanded hiring protections for jobseekers with criminal records. Philly has already led the way with its 'Ban the Box' law, but these new measures aim to make sure more folks get a fair shot instead of being shut out by old mistakes.

PA v. The U.S. DOJ
The Justice Department is suing Pennsylvania (and five other states) over access to voters’ personal data, while Harrisburg is fighting a whole separate legal battle on food-stamp info with the feds (details here). Just another day for PA at war with DC.

SEPTA: Trolley Mayhem, Again
Three people landed in the hospital after wires fell on a SEPTA trolley in Delaware County, briefly halting service. For anyone counting: yes, it’s been a particularly “eventful” week for transit.

City Life: Openings, Closings, and Oddities

Joan Shepp Is Back on Walnut
Center City’s most iconic fashion boutique reopened on Walnut Street after three years away, with a new space and a return to the OG address. The mannequins are looking sharp again.

Midtown Village Fall Fest Is Canceled
Sorry to anyone plotting their outfit for outdoor Sangria: the 2025 Midtown Village Fall Festival isn't happening. Organizers haven't said why. Meanwhile, Tria just opened a Rittenhouse event space with wine classes, so there's still something classy to do.

Pop Bagels Heads to the ‘Burbs
The viral bagel-sandwich chain that won’t slice or schmear (read: No slices, no sandwiches, no rules) is coming to Ardmore’s Suburban Square—cue your takes on regional bagel etiquette.

Quick Hits

  • Catfish & waffles are having a moment at Rex at the Royal, using invasive blue catfish to revive a classic dish—and help local rivers.
  • The Navy and Marine Corps are celebrating 250 years with parades and events across Philly and Camden all weekend.
  • Bring an umbrella: Thursday storms came with flash floods and power outages (recap here).
  • And for TV bingers: Joey Merlino, of all people, is coming to Netflix.

More tomorrow. Go Phils.
