Yesterday in Philly: What You Missed

SNAP Uncertainty: Food Aid At Risk

If you rely on SNAP (or know someone who does), heads up: funding is in limbo and a federal bill to keep the money flowing just failed. Pennsylvania is looking at plans to use rainy day funds for food banks, Delaware says it’ll cover SNAP for now (details), and nonprofits are doing what they can. If you need help or want to give some, here's a resource roundup. Also, with Halloween here, some groups are literally handing out food with candy (no shame about that).

SEPTA Inspections: Delays Incoming?

SEPTA is asking for more time to check its aging Silverliner IV train cars, which means possible schedule messes down the line. Haven’t heard about cuts yet, but don’t be shocked if the Regional Rail gets bumpy. Elsewhere, Philly's $91 million streetlight upgrade is underway. Good for safety, but expect some work trucks around at night.

Farewell to Philly Legends

Major loss for local radio: Pierre Robert, the long-haired, peace-and-love heart of WMMR, has died at 70. Also gone: Harry Donahue of KYW, a true Temple sports icon. The city’s airwaves are quieter today.

Weather & Commute

Expect heavy rain and wind Thursday morning. Your drive or ride isn’t going to be pleasant—plan for delays, and maybe keep the rain boots by the door.

Other Notables

  • Bucks County cold case cracked: After 62 years, detectives finally named the rapist and killer of Carol Ann Dougherty (read more).
  • Eagles moves: The Birds swapped WR John Metchie to the Jets for cornerback Michael Carter. Details here if you care about backfield depth.
  • Fentanyl fight ramping up: Officials say nearly 27 million doses have been seized in our area just this year. More busts (and press conferences) ahead.
  • Philly will use a record budget surplus to brace for whatever financial curveballs come next. Mayor Parker says the money will help fill gaps if federal cash dries up (source).
  • Dog park/bar coming to Manayunk! Fetch Park, a combo dog park and taproom, opens next month.

No shortage of change this week. If you want more on Philly sports (Sixers are on fire, Flyers have some promising prospects), or obscure city art collabs (Eagles-branded whiskey bottles?), read this.

That's it for now. Stay dry, stay fed, tell the DJ in your life you love them.
