Eagles Collapse, Flyers Wake Up, and Philly Gets Ready to Run

Morning, Philly. If you checked out for the weekend, here’s what you missed. Spoiler: Could’ve been a better Sunday for Birds fans…

Sports: Eagles' Faceplant, Flyers' Spark

  • Eagles totally blew it against the Cowboys, coughing up a 21-point lead and getting owned in Dallas. There’s a lot to unpack (embarrassing defense, banged-up starters, coaching basically melting down). If you want all the ugly details, here’s Roob’s post-game vent and the Week 12 inactives. Up next: lots of angry talk radio.
  • If you need to emotionally hedge, check out the Flyers, who finally remembered how to score and snagged a win over the Devils—shoutout to Tyson Foerster.
  • The Sixers lost to Miami, but at least rookie Jared McCain is looking more like himself. Maxey still kind of doing everything.

Marathon Alert: Road Closures Incoming

  • The 2025 Philadelphia Marathon took over the city, crowning winners and closing a ton of streets. If you’re driving or parking anywhere near Center City or the Parkway this week, double-check the restrictions or expect to get boxed in. More details (and the full list of closures and detours) here.

Neighborhood News

  • Philly's rolling out a new scholarship program for students and teachers to celebrate the nation's upcoming 250th anniversary, which could actually be kind of cool if you like our weird history.
  • Early holiday stuff: Christmas Village is back with some new weirdness, and the Brandywine Museum's model trains are running again. If you need a quick serotonin boost, go look at tiny trains.
  • An awful update out of Chester: A 3-year-old died days after a devastating house fire. The community is in mourning, and more info on how to help may come later this week.

Quick Hits

  • The new triennial art festival launching in 2026 aims to put Philly’s arts scene on the global map. Mark your calendars (seriously, this might be one your out-of-town friends want to crash).

Outlook for Monday: traffic headaches from lingering marathon detours, sports talk hangovers, and the Christmas lights are already everywhere. Go get some coffee. More tomorrow.
