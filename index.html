Yesterday in Philly: Water Main Drama & Eagles Hopes

Transit headaches, water in the streets, and everyone arguing over cheesesteaks. Here’s what mattered in Philly yesterday.

Getting Around: SEPTA + Flooding Woes

  • SEPTA Regional Rail riders woke up to signal issues causing delays again. SEPTA says things should be back to normal by late morning, but heads up if you’re rolling in early. (And yes, there was a train fire in Paoli, with one staffer injured, because it’s never boring.)
  • Meanwhile, folks in Northeast Philly got hit with yet another water main break on Cottman Ave, making a mess for drivers and slicing through commutes. Road closures continue while crews patch things up (alternate routes suggested).

Food, Real Estate, and New Openings

  • If you like standing in line for Angelo’s – congrats, you’ll soon have more places to do it. The South Philly icon is expanding to Conshy and South Jersey, so…yeah, bring a book.
  • Old City’s getting a new vintage spot: Snail opens soon for anyone hunting secondhand T-shirts, jeans, and leather. Keep an eye out for grand-opening deals.
  • Meanwhile, two Delancey Street townhouses duke it out for 'fanciest' bragging rights and definitely aren’t getting more affordable—but if you like to Zillow-snoop, this is pure candy.

Neighborhood & Civic: Housing, Healthcare, and the FDR Park Herons

  • Thousands of renters in West and Southwest Philly are potentially at risk of losing their homes next year if a deal isn’t struck for affordable units. No answers yet—just mounting pressure.
  • FDR Park wants you to help name its new fleet of bird-shaped boats. Philly can be trusted with this, right?
  • On campus: Penn’s postdocs and research assistants voted to unionize, joining a wave of higher-ed labor action.

Sports & Stage

  • Eagles camp is open, and the preseason roster math is swirling. Check the latest 53-man projections and what could get in the way of a Super Bowl repeat (spoiler: the NFC’s tough).
  • Phillies pitcher Joe Ross to the IL with back spasms. Rotation shuffle incoming (details here).
  • Low Cut Connie’s show in Wilkes-Barre got pulled over “political propaganda” worries. That’s a whole other can of worms.

Quick Hits

  • Driver sentenced up to 20 years for fatal Rittenhouse Square crash that killed CHOP doctor Peter DeLuca last year.
  • PAWS launches “paw it forward” to help low-income pet owners keep their animals fed and housed. Donate pet food if you’re able.
  • ICYMI: Ozzy Osbourne passed away at 76, and yes, Philly fans paid their respects.

Coming up: Christmas in July events hit town this weekend, and there’s still way too much road work everywhere. Hang in there.
