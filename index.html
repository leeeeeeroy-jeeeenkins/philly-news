Red Lights, More Bones, and the Eagles' Rain Delay

Traffic tickets, graveyard insanity, ICE protests, and playoff football — here's what you missed in Philly.

Getting Around: Watch the Red Lights

If you still cruise through Eakins Oval by the Art Museum without stopping, start looking for $100 in your glove box — red light cameras there just started ticketing for real. No more warnings, just actual fines going out, so don't get caught being that person.

Civic Stuff: Philly v. ICE

In the wake of ICE's fatal shooting of a woman in Minneapolis, protests hit Philly yesterday and city officials told ICE to basically get lost. Hundreds rallied, especially in Center City, emphasizing Philly’s status as a so-called "sanctuary city." More local coverage here.

True Crime, Weird Edition

Big story: A Delco man allegedly stole more than 100 human skeletons from Mount Moriah Cemetery — the remains were found in his home. Neighbors are all kinds of freaked out. If you need more gory details (or are checking on your ancestors), full rundown here.

Eagles: Weather and Hype

If you’re tailgating for Eagles-49ers this weekend, bring an umbrella for the dip into late Friday and Saturday — but it’s supposed to clear for game time. The city is fired up (rally busses, pep rallies) and every South Philly rowhome has at least two flags up. Game preview with the X’s and O’s is here for the hardcores.

Sixers and Phillies: Roster Moves

Sixers fans, Joel Embiid is questionable for Friday against Orlando, just after the team got healthy. Read the full update here and instant reactions here. Meanwhile, the Phillies are meeting with Bo Bichette, and a big shake-up could follow — get into the weeds here.
