Winter Storm Prep, School Closures, and Philly’s Dining Flex

Hey Philly — here’s what you missed while emptying shelves of milk, bread, and apparently cat food. The city is bracing for a real-deal snowstorm, public schools are contemplating some major changes, and there’s a lot happening on the food and sports front. Let’s get to it.

SNOW WATCH: The Great Grocery Grab

The big story: Philadelphia is bracing for a serious winter storm this weekend (think plows, emergency parking rules, and up to a foot of snow depending who you believe). Shoppers have already ransacked stores for staples and then some — cat food is apparently the new toilet paper. Salt is in short supply, so early birds got the good stuff. Crews around the region are prepping plows and routes; if you need to review exactly which snow emergency routes mess with your parking, here’s what an emergency declaration actually means for Philly.

EDUCATION: School Shake-Up Incoming?

In non-weather news: the School District just dropped a $2.8 billion plan that’d close or repurpose 20 public schools — likely one near you. Officials swear it’s about fixing up old buildings and "enhancing educational opportunities," but expect plenty of neighborhood pushback. The actual list of schools is coming soon.

GOVERNMENT: Housing $$$ and Trash Drama

City Council approved $800 million for housing programs, shifting more aid to the lowest-income residents. Meanwhile, a controversial bill to ban Philly from burning trash was put on hold before a vote — so for now, the city’s waste keeps heading to Chester.

AROUND TOWN: Villanova Threat, Pet Expo, More

  • Villanova shut down Thursday after a threat against an academic building (all clear by afternoon; campus safe).
  • Foodies, take a bow: Philly snagged 13 James Beard Award semifinalist nods, and Greg Vernick’s new spot opens in Kensington soon.
  • ReAnimator Coffee is bringing its sixth location to Mount Airy. Bad news for your New Year’s resolution.
  • Need plans? Free Winter Jawn concert this weekend (if you can dig out), and Greater Philly Pet Expo returns to Oaks.

SPORTS DESK: Offseason Shuffle & Flyers Pain

  • Mythical snow aside, the most consistent thing in town is chaos in the Eagles’ front office: they’re still hunting for a new offensive coordinator, with Bears OC Declan Doyle interviewed. Also, Christian Parker is reportedly leaving for Dallas (yep, the Cowboys).
  • Phillies still want to move Nick Castellanos; Harrison Bader rumors swirling.
  • Flyers surrendered a win in classic Flyers fashion (demoralizing OT loss).

ICYMI

  • Philly is suing the federal government over the removal of the slavery exhibit at Independence Mall.
  • South Street needs local artists for new lamppost banners.

Bunker down, charge your devices, and don’t forget to move your car off snow routes. Stay warm, Philly.
