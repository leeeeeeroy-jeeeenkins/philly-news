SNOW, SLUDGE, AND CITY DRAMA

Good morning. While you were dodging ice and missing SEPTA buses, here’s what Philly was up to yesterday.

Weather & City Services

  • Still digging out: Philly is facing a brutal cold snap and leftover snow/ice walls from Sunday’s storm. Residents are still shoveling out—and parked cars are only now breaking free in some neighborhoods (PHL Airport is a luggage graveyard).
  • SEPTA status: Regional rail is back, but bus service has delays and suspensions. If you’re waiting for a bus right now, bring a battery pack and podcasts.
  • More snow coming? Forecasters are hinting at another possible storm this weekend. Don’t trade out those boots just yet.
  • Code Blue packed: Shelters are full and there’s a spike in no-heat calls. If you can, check on your neighbors.

Politics & People Power

  • Preserving history (or not): The city and Governor Shapiro are fighting the feds over the missing slavery exhibit at the President’s House site. Activists are pushing for its return while the city battles what it calls “whitewashing history.” (More here.)
  • Prosecuting the feds? Philly DA Larry Krasner is joining a new national coalition aiming to hold ICE and other federal officers accountable if they break state laws.

Philly Schools & Closures

  • Tough news for schools: 20 schools could close next year. The district is still working out which ones, but it’s got plenty of parents and teachers on edge.

Sports Stuff Worth Knowing

  • Sixers (it’s hot and cold): Joel Embiid and Paul George are probable for Thursday’s game. The team is dead in the middle of the NBA pack for power rankings. On the bright side, Jared McCain is finally showing out and rookie VJ Edgecombe got picked for the Rising Stars game.
  • Phillies (not great): Outfield depth is officially a concern. Here’s how grim it looks by the numbers.
  • Eagles (offseason shuffle): The search for an OC is getting wild. Here’s who’s in (and out) so far and who the top free agent priorities look like.

Eat, Drink, Escape

  • Restaurant Week extended: More than 60 places are running deals through Feb. 4. Get out and test-drive somewhere you’d never pay full price for.
  • Bagel news: Call Your Mother, the DC bagel chain, is opening a Fishtown shop this year. Bagel wars incoming.
  • Food for thought: One local food writer wants to kill “vibe dining” in Philly. Not everyone needs a DJ and a glitter wall with their pasta.

That’s your catch-up. Text us when your block is finally plowed. See ya tomorrow.
