SEPTA Train Chaos, Art Museum Drama, and Eagles Pull One Out

City Life

SEPTA headaches continue for Regional Rail riders. Cancellations are rolling through both Monday and Tuesday thanks to federally-mandated inspections of the Silverliner IV fleet. If your commute runs on Regional Rail, check those schedules and brace for crowded platforms. It’s not just a Monday nuisance—same issues hit Tuesday morning. Meanwhile, red light cameras are firing up a 60-day warning period on Kelly Drive at Eakins Oval, so watch your speed if you’re in the area (read more).

SNAP ‘crisis’ & reignited housing debate—Philly food banks report crowded lines and meal shortages with SNAP benefits frozen again: the usual giving season spike in donations isn’t enough to meet demand. At City Hall, advocates say Mayor Parker’s new $2 billion housing plan won’t address the most vulnerable. Worth watching as the budget shuffle continues.

Art Museum mess: Sasha Suda, recently ousted Philly Art Museum CEO, is now suing for wrongful termination (and here’s the deeper dive), claiming her firing was more about power struggles than performance. No word yet on who’s next for the big seat overlooking the Parkway.

Weather: Get your real coat—arctic air is moving in and temps are dropping hard for the next two days. Folks in Lehigh and Berks might even see some snow flurries up north.

Politics

The Senate voted to end the government shutdown—but not without drama. John Fetterman, usually good for a surprise, folded and voted with the crowd after threatening a no. Local reps are still not thrilled with the Senate deal (here’s why). Disruptions at PHL Airport continue as long-term impact fears linger (travelers, heads up).
