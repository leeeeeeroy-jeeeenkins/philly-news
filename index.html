Yesterday in Philly

Subject: Snow Incoming, Drama Ongoing, and a Little Holiday Cheer

The gist: Snow's on the way, SEPTA’s prepping, and Philly’s got fresh City Hall drama, sports injuries, and a boost for the arts.

Weather: Brace Yourself

The city and the ‘burbs are hunkering down for the first real snow of the season—up to a few inches by Sunday morning, depending on where you are. PennDOT and hardware stores are slammed getting ready (details here). If you’re driving, give yourself extra time (and look out for sidewalk salt shortages). Across the river, Jersey just declared a state of emergency due to a propane shortage (of course they did). Oh, and if you’re heading into Jersey, a 10-car pileup shut down parts of the turnpike Saturday—drivers, beware.

City Moves: Arts, Guns, and Council Stuff

Good news for Philly’s creative scene—the Philadelphia Cultural Fund will hand out $13 million to local arts groups next year, a big deal for orgs hit hard by COVID cuts. Across the river, gun owners are again challenging Jersey’s bans in so-called 'sensitive places'—think bars, parks, and beaches (read more).

Meanwhile, healthcare’s about to get pricier: with some key ACA subsidies expiring soon, a lot of locals are bracing for higher bills and crappier coverage.

Sports: Banged Up and Down (Again)

Sixers fans: Don’t expect a full squad—Embiid is questionable and Maxey is doubtful for the Sunday game against Atlanta. On the plus side, Embiid and rookie VJ Edgecombe showed flashes in the win over the Pacers (game notes here).

Eagles watch: Eyes on the future as beat writers dream about 2026 college prospects, and Jalen Carter’s shoulder should be close to 100% for next year.

Community, Culture & Quick Hits

  • The city honored fallen Officer Andy Chan at a block party—his legacy’s still strong in Northeast Philly.
  • If you’re staying local, Walnut Brain is Philly’s experimental band of the moment. Worth a listen if you’re into weird soundscapes.
  • For the holiday spirit (while you still can): Smithville Park in Jersey goes all-in on lights and festivities.

Worth Noting

  • Lots of fires reported overnight around the region—South Philly, Norristown, Easttown—and at least two deaths confirmed. Stay alert and check your smoke detectors.
  • Police are warning about a string of armed robberies hitting neighborhoods citywide.
  • Still want snow updates? Live tracking here as the storm moves through.

— That’s all. Stay safe, shovel early, and keep your emergency hoagie close.
