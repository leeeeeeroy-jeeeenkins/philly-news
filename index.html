Trash Pickup Returns, Phillies Pause, and a New Way to Rat Out Parkers

Here’s what actually mattered in Philly yesterday — before you wade into another week of sweaty commutes and trash pileups. Let’s get into it:

🚚 Trash Woes: Slightly Less Woe-y

If you’ve been dodging bags on the sidewalk for days, good news: trash pickup resumes citywide today. The union is still voting on a new contract, but they’re back on the trucks, so cross your fingers for a return to something like normal curbside service.

🚆 Transit Tweaks: PATCO Service Changes

PATCO starts phasing in new schedules today for deep cleaning. Expect altered departure times and possible waits if you’re headed to Jersey. SEPTA is their usual selves.

⚾ Baseball: Phillies Hit the Break on Top

The Phillies slid into the All-Star break with a division lead after Sanchez pitched a near-perfect game vs. the Padres (recap here). But, as PhillyVoice puts it: they’re at a “crossroads” as the trade deadline nears. This will be a use-your-heartburn-meds kind of summer.

🏀 Hoops: Sixers Kids on Display

Tyrese Maxey is talking up new arrivals VJ Edgecombe and Trendon Watford (full Maxey interview here), even as the top draft picks sat out another Summer League game. Rookie Jared McCain says he’s "excited" to build chemistry with Edgecombe (hear from him). So, progress, but don’t expect fireworks just yet.

💡 Philly Living: New App Lets You Report Bad Parkers

Finally, something for the pettier side of Philly: a new app debuts that lets regular folks report parking violations with supposed "laser vision." Expect neighbor drama — and maybe a few less illegal hydrant parkers.

☔ Weather: Humid, Wet Start to the Week

If today feels especially sticky, you’re not wrong. Scattered storms are likely, and it should stay muggy through midweek. Not great for, you know, trash situations.

🎨 In the Neighborhood

  • Barnes on the Block brought arts, music, and food back to the Parkway (photos and recap).
  • Christmas in July invaded Montgomery County (it happened), proving we’ll throw a party for absolutely anything.

That’s the short version. More tomorrow — and don’t forget to haul out your trash if it’s still sitting there.
