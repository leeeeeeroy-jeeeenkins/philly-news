Yesterday in Philly

Subject: New Law Targets Dumpers, Phillies Make a Big Trade

Hey Philly, here's what went down yesterday:

Top Story: Shots Fired at West Philly Rec Center

Tragic news: five people, including two kids and a city worker, were shot near the playground at 57th and Haverford Wednesday afternoon. Everyone survived, but the city’s gun violence crisis clearly isn’t taking a summer break. The search for the shooter is ongoing.

City Hall Watch: New Crackdown on Illegal Dumping

Philly’s got a new anti-dumping law: if you get caught tossing trash illegally, cops can now impound your car. Officials are hoping this will finally make a dent in the mountain of trash plaguing way too many neighborhoods. Good luck to anyone rolling up with a U-Haul full of old mattresses.

Public Services: PECO Offering Help on Overdue Bills

Tough time paying for electricity? PECO just announced $10 million in relief grants, with up to $500 available for customers behind on payments. Details and application info are here if you or your neighbors need it.

Phillies Get a Flamethrower

Big move at the trade deadline: The Phils traded for reliever Jhoan Duran from the Twins, who regularly throws over 100 mph. They’re also rumored to be eyeing more bullpen arms and another outfielder (more here). October just got a little bit more interesting.

Weather Alert: Storms Incoming

Could get rough out there: storms are rolling through today with flooding and even tornado watches possible. Don’t park under any sketchy trees.

Quick Hits

  • Soft-serve ice cream is having a moment—map your options for the rest of the summer.
  • Kelly Drive will be closed overnight for repaving for nearly a month. Plan those late runs or commutes accordingly.
  • Final design unveiled for the Sadie T. M. Alexander statue, soon to stand outside the Municipal Services Building—an overdue tribute to one of Philly’s civil rights giants.
  • Fishtown thrift store finds old artwork left at UArts and is trying to get it back to the artists. Spread the word if you know any former UArts folks!
  • The BlackStar Film Festival starts soon with 92 films—all in Philly. Culture vultures, check the guide.

Stay safe out there, especially if you're near a pool (see above). More tomorrow.
