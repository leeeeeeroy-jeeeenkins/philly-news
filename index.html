Yesterday in Philly: Snow Day, City Stuff, and a Whole Lotta Closures

Welcome to your Sunday freeze: here’s everything Philly cared about while you were hunting for your gloves and cursing at shovel prices. Settle in, it’s basically a winter weather edition.

Snowpocalypse Incoming: Closures and Chaos

  • Schools & Courts: Philly schools and courts will be closed Monday thanks to the storm — plan accordingly if you’ve got kids or civic duties.
  • Transit: PATCO’s on a modified schedule, SEPTA’s likely to be slow, and flights at PHL are already getting canceled. If you absolutely have to travel, triple-check before you go.
  • Delaware State of Emergency: Both Delaware and Wilmington declared states of emergency — if you’re headed south, expect delays and restrictions.
  • Philly Bars/Restaurants: Some are staying open for the storm (because of course).
  • Helpful Stuff: Need to know who to call for fallen trees or outages? Not sure how to not injure yourself shoveling? Docs have your back.

If you want hour-by-hour snow intel, Fox29 has live updates going all storm long. And remember — if your rental’s sidewalk isn’t shoveled, it’s on your landlord. Technically.

Fires in the Freeze

  • Overnight blazes kept firefighters out in the cold:
    - Multiple overnight fires burned through ties in the city, and three separate fires left folks injured and homes damaged. Bitter cold made it all worse. Send some appreciation to anyone out working this weekend.

Sports in a Nutshell

  • Sixers Crash: Joel Embiid put on a show but the Sixers lost to the Knicks anyway. It’s that kind of season.
  • Eagles Carousel: If you’re still following the never-ending coordinator search, there’s news, rumors, and fresh questions. More breakdown here. Still a mess.
  • Phillies Prospects: Three Phils made MLB’s top 100 prospects list. Andrew Painter is no longer #1 for the club. Spring is coming (eventually).

Civic Weirdness, City Stuff

  • ICE Protests: State AG Shapiro and local legislators are calling out ICE, after the federal killing of a U.S. citizen in Minneapolis drew national heat.
  • Mütter Museum: Because Philly just has to be different — the Mütter’s hosting a pop-up event about real-life vampirism. Yes, medicine and myth and all that. Because why not?
  • President’s House Controversy: Protests and lawsuits are bubbling up after the removal of slavery exhibits at the Independence Mall site. Here’s the latest.

That’s it for Sunday. Stock up, hunker down, tip your delivery drivers, and check on your neighbors. Go Birds (even if they’re making us stress already).
