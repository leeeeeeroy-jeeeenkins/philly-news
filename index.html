Shakeups, Snow, and One Big Cheesesteak Debate

Weather check: Winter showed up early — a wintry mix hit parts of Philly and the burbs yesterday, and while Center City mostly saw rain, schools in the outer ‘burbs delayed opening. PennDOT was out salting and Lehigh Valley got a real taste of snow. More next week, so don’t retire that ice scraper.

City Stuff

Council moves: Philly City Council just advanced the $277M budget for Mayor Parker's new signature housing plan. It’s aiming to help more folks become homeowners, with a heavy focus on first-time buyers and those at risk of displacement. Also, there’s a push to make outdoor dining permits easier for restaurants, so you can expect more streeteries (and heated tents) this winter.

Philly makes headlines (again): Sorry, not for fine dining. Philly was just named America’s top dining destination… supposedly because of cheesesteaks. (Sigh.) Look, the hoagie discourse rolls on forever, but we all know this city’s food game is more than just red sauce and provolone.

Transit & Getting Around

SEPTA update: Trying to make your train on time? SEPTA is borrowing rail cars from Maryland this week to help with overcrowding while their own fleet is in rough shape. Don’t expect miracles, but maybe your commute will be a little less sardine-can-like.

Amazon: Prime, but faster. Philly is getting 30-minute Amazon delivery. Wild. Don’t ask how your old neighbors in Fishtown will react to even more branded vans, though.

Sports Drama

Eagles saga continues: The power rankings aren’t kind, and the NFL kicked the Eagles-Commanders game out of prime time. Meanwhile, offensive coordinator Kevin Patullo’s house was egged after Sunday’s bust. Nick Sirianni put the blame squarely on himself (for real), and Patullo isn’t losing his job just yet, so let’s see if that calms things.

Flyers lose more than just a game: After a loss to the Penguins, the Flyers are now down Tyson Foerster for 2–3 months with an injury.

Quick Hits

  • RIP Officer Andy Chan: The Philly officer critically hurt in a 2018 motorcycle crash passed away yesterday. City flags will be lowered — a tough loss for PPD.
  • Quinta Brunson is giving back: The Abbott Elementary star started a new fund for Philly public school kids to go on field trips. Love to see it.
  • Gas prices alert: Fill up—average gas is now below $3/gallon in the region for the first time since 2020. (Yes, really.)
  • New cruise ship terminal coming: Construction just started on a new Philly cruise terminal near the airport. Ready for those Norwegian sailings next spring?

That’s the Philly you missed yesterday. Stay warm, keep an eye on those clouds, and don’t let anyone tell you all we eat is cheesesteaks.
