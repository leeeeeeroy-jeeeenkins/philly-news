Sports, Trains, and a Mushroom Party: Monday in Philly

Let’s get to it:

⚾ Phillies Roll, but Trea Turner Pulls Up

The Phillies took another series from the Marlins, staying hot in the NL East race, but Trea Turner exited with a hamstring injury. He’s scheduled for scans today—prepare for some lineup shuffling and a lot of Rob Thomson logic if Turner misses time. Bryce Harper’s still mashing, so there’s that.

🏈 Eagles Go Big (in Theory)

Surprise rumor: The Birds reportedly offered a mountain of draft picks for Micah Parsons. The deal never happened, but let’s be real—Howie wants another defensive stud. Meanwhile, here’s your Week 1 rooting guide for non-Eagles games if you can’t quit football even on the bye.

🚆 Rail Service: Finally, Some Good News

The Chestnut Hill East SEPTA Regional Rail line is back open after months of repairs and track work. Commute from NW Philly just got less unpredictable (but don’t get too comfortable—this is still SEPTA).

🚨 Around the Streets

West and North Philly both saw overnight shootings. West Philly and Hunting Park are in the news, again. Police are also hunting for a Kensington hit-and-run driver (they just released footage). Stay alert—especially around busy intersections and at night.

🍄 Kennett Square’s 40th Mushroom Festival

If you escaped the city this weekend, hopefully you caught the Kennett Square Mushroom Festival (yes, we love a weird small-town food party). It’s even older than the average Temple freshman—props if you got your fill of fungi.

💰 Lotteries and Local Weather

No billion-dollar Powerball winner in PA, but someone in Jersey snagged a $1M ticket. Hope springs eternal. And in the meantime, it's sunny and cool today—finally, a Monday that’s not a swamp.

That’s the big stuff. Catch you tomorrow.
