SEPTA Gets a Lifeline, Eagles Collapse, and Parade Season Looms

Yesterday in Philly

SEPTA Saved (for Now)

Good news for Regional Rail commuters feeling abandoned: SEPTA just got a $220 million bail-out from PennDOT that should fully restore Regional Rail service by late December. The money is headed specifically toward fixing those ancient train cars so fewer surprise delays (let’s hope). Gov. Shapiro, meanwhile, framed the funding as a “must,” telling everyone, “We cannot let SEPTA fail.” So your New Year’s resolutions might actually be possible to reach by train.

Eagles Blow Massive Lead in Dallas

The Birds turned a 21–0 lead into a painful 24–21 loss against the Cowboys in a game that had everything—meltdowns, brutal play calls, and enough penalties to make you nauseous. Snap count breakdown here. Rookie safety Andrew Mukuba is out with a broken ankle, and the run game is officially in the gutter. If you’re looking for bright spots: A.J. Brown flashed. But that’s about it.

Thanksgiving Parade & Traffic, Oh My

The Philadelphia Thanksgiving Day Parade is Thursday and it’s going to be a big one, with more people traveling this year than last. Full list of road closures and parade performers here. TL;DR: Kelly Drive, much of the Parkway, and surrounding roads will be a mess all day. Pack patience if you’re driving or taking SEPTA near the Parkway.

And if you’re leaving town (or just picking someone up): Thanksgiving travel is expected to set new highs, so brace for airport crowds and more cars on the roads, not to mention classic Philly parking headaches.

Bonus: Weather’s looking dry for Thursday. Save the umbrella for Friday.

Other Stuff Worth Noting

  • Justice for Sgt. Mendez: Two men have been found guilty in the 2023 PHL airport shooting that killed Philly police sergeant Richard Mendez. They’re facing life in prison.
  • New life at WMMR: Matt Cord is taking over Pierre Robert’s midday slot on 93.3, meaning a new era for your lunchtime soundtrack.
  • Parker’s new hire: Philly’s mayor appointed a new director for Neighborhood Action Centers to try and break through City Hall red tape on local quality-of-life stuff.
  • Holiday markets & events: Fiber Craft Market is back in South Philly Dec. 6 with 50+ local vendors. Good chance to buy something actually made here.
  • Gritty’s at it again: Gritty dropped a new calendar—full of extremely Philly vibes, of course.

Stay tuned, and don’t click the turkey fryer tips unless you really like the fire department.
