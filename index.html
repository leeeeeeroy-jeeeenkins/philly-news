Yesterday in Philly

Subject: That Flooded Feeling, Union Peace, and Parking Narcs

City & Weather

Floods, delays, and road drama: Another round of heavy rain pounded the region Tuesday, causing flash floods in Jersey and southeast PA. Route 130 shut down for hours, and NJ declared a state of emergency (details). Philly International saw flight delays (again). Over in Mayfair, it was a different sort of hospitality: meet the local whose literal job is making the city welcoming for America250 and beyond.

City Hall & Civic Stuff

No citywide strike mess—DC47 locked in: Philly’s big white-collar union, District Council 47, came to a tentative contract deal with City Hall—so your trash day and permits probably won’t be any more of a mess than usual. Also, there’s more money coming for affordable housing projects with the new city budget (here’s what’s in it).

Parking narc culture just leveled up: The PPA quietly rolled out a new online tool to let regular folks upload evidence of parking violations. Got a photo of that jerk in the bike lane or someone with a fake disability placard? The PPA wants your tips. (Don’t say we didn’t warn you.)

Traffic & bikes: After two separate bike crashes near the South Street Bridge, neighbors are calling for a real fix to that intersection’s gnarly traffic pattern. No city action yet, but the pressure is up.

SEPTA & Getting Around

Good-ish news for your commute: Serious crime on SEPTA keeps dropping in 2025, though petty crimes are up. (Hide your snacks, hang onto your phone.) Trouble on the tracks downtown too—a man was stabbed at the Old City station, but trains kept moving.

Food & Nightlife

Sonny’s Cocktail Joint can’t catch a break: The fam-fave bar is closed indefinitely—again—after weekend flooding trashed their basement. No word on when they’ll be back, but, honestly, keep Sonny’s in your thoughts.

Sports

Sixers, Phillies, & Eagles check-in: VJ Edgecombe, this year’s #3 pick, is finally suiting up for Summer League. Meanwhile, the Phillies hit the All-Star break with solid marks but, yeah, there are rotation questions (here’s how the draft could shake things up). The Eagles' rookies officially report soon—cornerbacks are the camp story to watch.

Quick Hits

  • Fringe Festival 2025 dropped its lineup—320 acts across town in September.
  • Abbott Elementary and Colman Domingo (West Philly’s own) are up for Emmys.
  • Penn Medicine's new mobile unit is taking mammograms right to neighborhoods.
  • Trying to vote in the primaries as an independent? Court battle just got real.

That’s the gist. If you missed it, consider yourself caught up.
