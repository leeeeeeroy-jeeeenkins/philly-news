City Council Drama, SEPTA Uncertainty, and Why It Feels Like July in August

Yo Philly, here’s what you actually missed yesterday — from bus funding fights to the best cornerback at Eagles camp to Philly’s never-ending quest to function as a real city. Let's get into it:

SEPTA Funding Crunch: Lawmakers Shuttling Back to Harrisburg

So, it’s crunch time for SEPTA. State lawmakers are spending their weekend in Harrisburg because a major funding deal is still up in the air. If things don’t shake loose soon, we’re looking at service cuts and possible fare hikes. Nothing’s final, but if you ride regional rail or the El, keep an eye on this one: your commute could get even more interesting.

Weather Report: Still Hot, Still Weird

It’s not your imagination — this is the 7th-hottest summer since Philly started keeping records. Sunday’s a Code Orange Air Quality day, so if you’re sensitive to air pollution, maybe do that run tomorrow instead. Otherwise, expect more sun and heat for now, because Philly refuses to cool off in any sense.

Neighborhood Pulse: Kensington Block Party, Germantown Rallies

Shoutout to Kensington, where neighbors threw a big block party for revitalization on Kensington Ave — not just a party, but a statement against what the neighborhood's been through. Over in Germantown, hundreds rallied for Anovsack “Anou” Vongbandith, a café owner being held in ICE detention. Community’s showing up for its own, if not always city bureaucracy.

City Hall Moves: Zoning, Scooters, and... Columbus Day?

A judge overturned the executive order that swapped out Columbus Day, so the city’s holidays are back where they started. Meanwhile, Abby’s Law got introduced to regulate e-scooters — possibly less chaos in the bike lane, but we’ll see. And: Pat Dugan says he’s staying in the Philly D.A. race; add another name to your ballot.

Sports: Eagles Preseason, Phillies Keep Rolling

  • Eagles camp: QB Dorian Thompson-Robinson is turning heads (practice notes), and Eli Ricks made some noise with a pick six. Keep an eye on the cornerback battle (battle tracker).
  • Phillies: The outfield was clutch as the Phils beat the Rangers and grabbed the series win. Not a bad weekend for the bandwagoners.

Quick Hits

  • Kids in Northeast Philly got backpack giveaways ahead of school (nice one).

That’s the roundup. If you’re headed out, stay cool and maybe stash a backup transit card just in case.
