Yesterday in Philly: Streets, Sleet, & Snow Day Shifts

Winter finally showed up, and—surprise!—Philly didn't exactly stick the landing. Here’s what you actually need to know from Sunday:

Weather Mess & What’s Closed

Yes, that blizzard was legit. Heavy, wet snow blanketed the city and surrounding 'burbs, with totals making this maybe our biggest storm in a decade (Fox29). City officials declared a snow emergency, most schools are closed or pivoting to virtual today (Monday), including the School District of Philadelphia. Order more snacks, because it’s not just Philly: New Jersey imposed a travel ban, and PennDOT/PA Turnpike restricted vehicles on major roads (Fox29). And for all you optimists—here’s a full list of school closings and delays.

Parking heads up: PPA is offering $5 parking in Center City garages till the storm is done (Fox29). Take them up on it before you get buried.

Sports Hits: Snow, But No Slam Dunks

  • Sixers: Joel Embiid is still out (PhillyVoice), and the team looked lost in another ugly loss (PhillyVoice). If you skipped it, you missed nothing but pain.
  • Phillies: 16(!) players are off at the World Baseball Classic (PhillyVoice). Roster depth test incoming.
  • Eagles: No drama—stay-or-go talk for the defensive line is mostly business as usual (PhillyVoice), and yes, the Tush Push will live on into 2026 (NBC10).

Transit & Local Life

Work on the Hudson River rail tunnel is back on, but NJ Transit riders can still expect delays (WHYY). Nothing new, but worth knowing if you’re a regular.

Chinatown got their Lunar New Year parade done with major energy—hours before the sidewalks vanished under snow (WHYY).

Quick Hits

  • Pennsylvania is throwing extra resources at avian flu outbreaks—keep an eye on egg prices (PhillyVoice).
  • Camden’s under a boil water advisory after a water main break—if you’re crossing the river, know before you go (NBC10).
  • Winterfest is still scheduled in Kennett this weekend—50+ breweries lined up (PhillyVoice), assuming the snow doesn’t mess things up.

Stay warm, drive safe, and text your snow day crew—the city’s not moving much today anyway. Full rundown of snow totals and updates here.
