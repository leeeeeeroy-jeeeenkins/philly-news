Remembering Pat’s, Commuting Mess, and Housing Hope

Hey Philly, here’s what happened yesterday when you weren’t looking:

City Life & Closures

  • RIP Frank Olivieri Sr. – The longtime owner of Pat’s King of Steaks died at 87, leaving a serious hole in the city’s cheesesteak lore. Even if you’re more of a Geno’s person, this one stings. Related: Steak dinner just hits different when we’re saying goodbye.
  • In Kensington: Cantina la Martina is back in action with its first Philly event since closing: a community tamalada this Thursday. It’s a win for local food in a neighborhood that needs it.
  • Schools started late (or not at all) Tuesday thanks to bitter cold and icy roads. That also means possible delays for the rest of the week. Oh, and there’s talk of real snow coming this weekend.

Housing & Development

  • The huge, crumbling Brith Sholom House in West Philly secured $50M in public/private money for a "gut rehab"—meaning 360 affordable homes for seniors finally getting proper repairs. Not a bad start for Mayor Parker’s promises on housing.
  • Also: Philly’s biggest union-funded affordable housing project is moving forward in Wynnefield Heights. More homes, still a drop in the bucket, but better than nothing.

Transit & Roads

  • Chester County commuters: The Turnpike’s five-year, $338M widening project just kicked off. Get ready for detours, closures, and an endless parade of orange cones.
  • Montgomery County folks: there’s a major bridge replacement and new closure/detour plans you’ll want to check. Philly’s bad to drive in (literally ranked one of the worst states)… this is not helping.
  • Viral SEPTA moment: An altercation on a SEPTA bus made the rounds after a former WHYY intern was pepper sprayed. No one seriously hurt, but insert “public transit in Philly” meme here.

Protests, Politics & Philly Drama

  • Protesters blocked the ICE garage in Center City Tuesday, joining a national push for companies to cut ties with Israeli arms manufacturers. Traffic on the block was a mess.
  • In NJ, Mikie Sherrill was sworn in as governor and signed her first Executive Orders—including help for people facing crazy-high utility costs. Tips for Jersey e-bike riders: new licensing/insurance rules just arrived.

Sports & Culture

  • The Flyers snapped their losing streak, but good luck celebrating—now it’s a slog uphill in the standings.
  • Sixers pulled out a win thanks to Tyrese Maxey nabbing eight(!) steals and some actually-watchable defense. But heads up: Joel Embiid’s out again for the next one.
  • Finally, if you want to argue about which restaurant really is the best, Philly Mag dropped their ‘50 Best Restaurants’ podcast and spill the tea on how they rank your favorites. Let the debates begin.

Stay warm out there—single digits are in the forecast.
