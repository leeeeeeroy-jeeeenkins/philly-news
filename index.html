Yesterday in Philly

Subject: Snow on Deck, WMMR Shake-Up, and Sixers Drama

The city’s bracing for snow, a legendary radio station’s future is up in the air, and overnight closures are coming for I-76. Catch the important stuff you missed yesterday—here’s what’s going on:

Weather & Getting Around

You knew it was coming: Philly’s first real snow of the season is sliding in Tuesday. Expect a wintry mix—enough for some Pa. schools to delay or close (full list here), but probably just a reminder to make sure your boots don’t leak.

Heads up if you drive I-76 West near 30th Street: overnight closures are now a thing for the next six months. Plan for some detours and pain, especially if you thought you’d get home late and not hit traffic. Also, sidewalk and ramp upgrades on Ben Franklin Parkway near Logan Square start soon—expect some traffic headaches there too (details here).

Housing & City Life

Some good news on the housing front: a long-empty North Philly stretch is getting a new affordable housing project. Meanwhile, Southwest Philly is getting more community trash drop-offs—finally. (More here.)

DA Larry Krasner’s at City Council, asking for more cash—not to start a fight, but so his staff don’t all run off to chase ambulances. City worker retention is a thing right now.

Big Moves in Food & Philly Culture

Something’s up at WMMR. Philly’s legendary rock station is facing departures and uncertainty. For folks who grew up with Preston & Steve, this might be the end of an era.

The Michelin Guide is officially coming to Philly. As if our dining scene’s ego needed another boost. Expect even more out-of-towners fighting you for reservations.

South Philly update: Emei is moving into the old Marra's pizzeria. Sad to see Marra's close after nearly 100 years, but if you’re craving Sichuan, it’s a win. Also, Emmett was named to Esquire’s best new restaurants list, so good luck getting a table.

Sports: All Over the Place

  • Sixers are missing Joel Embiid (again, new injury) and just dropped an ugly game despite his return—don’t ask about the fourth quarter. Here’s a breakdown.
  • Eagles' run defense woes keep stacking up, and OC Kevin Patullo had his house egged after the Bears loss, because some people have too much time and not enough sense (yes, really).
  • Phillies are poking around for new outfield options, targeting Jake Meyers. Hot stove’s warming up.

Stuff to Do & Look Out For

Holiday markets and lightings are in full swing. South Philly gets some Swedish holiday vibes on Dec. 6. For all the tree lighting schedules, here’s your guide. And if you need more: here’s what’s happening in Philly this week.

Catch you back here tomorrow. Stay warm—snow’s about to make things "interesting."
