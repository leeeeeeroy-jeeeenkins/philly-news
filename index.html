Yesterday in Philly: Big Wins, Big Bills, and the Same Old SEPTA

Sports (Because This City Never Sleeps)

The Eagles pulled off a tough 20-17 win over the Chiefs at Arrowhead, avenging the Super Bowl and moving to a crisp 2-0 start. The defense did the heavy lifting—rookie Andrew Mukuba grabbed the big interception, and the Birds kept Mahomes in check (ugly win? Sure. Still counts.). Die-hards are already debating how long this Super Bowl window actually stays open. If you want the nerdy details, here's who’s rising and falling in the Eagles’ stock watch.

On the diamond, the Phillies officially clinched their playoff berth—that’s four seasons in a row—although they couldn’t quite lock down the NL East yet after a loss to the Royals. Still, with the division and first-round bye up for grabs, the next couple games actually matter. Fans are already losing their minds and/or looking for playoff tickets.

City Beats

If you ride public transit, brace yourself. SEPTA's running full service again—but your wallet’s getting hit. Fares officially went up yesterday, right in time for everyone’s Monday return. Maybe check your balance before you tap on.

If you noticed more chatter about the President’s House exhibits: activists are rallying to keep the slavery history displays at Independence Mall as a Trump administration deadline to remove them looms. Big implications for how Philly tells its own story—worth watching if you care about what history this city puts in front of visitors.

Also in civic news: A North Philly post office now bears the name of the fallen Temple University police officer who was killed last year. The dedication comes after a long push from his family and the community.

Quick Hits and Worth-Noting

  • Thousands hit Penn’s Landing for Mexican Independence Day. If you stayed home, you missed world-class food and the biggest salsa dance circle of the year.
  • If you heard sirens in Kensington: four-car crash sent nine to the hospital, but everyone is expected to survive.
  • And Philly Museum of Art is showing off the best art screenprinting you’re gonna see all year. Perfect for an indoor day.

That’s the gist: Eagles flying high, Phillies’ fate hanging, SEPTA asking for more, history at stake in Old City, and the city being Philly as always. Catch you tomorrow.
