Yesterday in Philly

The penny drops, and City Hall drama heats up

Hey Philly. It was one of those days: pennies in the rearview, Council in a mood, transit feeling unreliable, and the Sixers somehow beating the Celtics without knowing who’s playing on Friday. Here’s what happened.

End of an Era: Philly Mints the Last Penny

The U.S. Mint in Philly officially squashed penny production after 232 years. The last-ever 1-cent coin rolled off the line yesterday, so next time you dig in the couch cushions, you’ve got a little piece of history. Why? Production costs more than the coin’s worth and, really, does anyone use pennies at Wawa anymore? (More here if you’re feeling nostalgic.)

City Council Grills Mayor Parker on Housing Plan

So about that big affordable housing plan our new mayor keeps touting: City Council isn’t handing out high-fives just yet. Members are pressing Mayor Parker to make sure her H.O.M.E. initiative will *actually* help the city’s poorest residents, not just tick off political talking points. Watch this space—the debate’s just heating up. (Council is not letting this go easily)

SEPTA Drama (Again)

More than 20 Regional Rail trains just got canceled—again. If you’re one of the many riding the rails from the burbs, check the app (twice). SEPTA says it’s staffing issues, commuters say it’s exhausting. Nothing new, but still. (Full list here.)

Pa. Budget: Finally Passed

After a months-long impasse, Harrisburg actually passed that $50 billion state budget. There’s money for schools, public transit (could SEPTA use a little of that?), gun violence prevention, and the usual editorial eye-roll about how it took so long. Here’s what’s in it.

Sixers Ups & Eagles Odds

  • Sixers fans: Justin Edwards put on for his hometown in a comeback win over the Celtics. Embiid is day-to-day ahead of the Pistons, and Paul George is sitting for a re-evaluation.
  • Eagles are slight favorites vs. the Lions for Sunday night. If you’re betting, watch those injury reports: AJ Brown is still not apologizing, Jaire Alexander’s out, and Philly’s power ranking is holding steady.

Quick Hits

  • Pop-up saunas are coming back to FDR Park this winter. Warm up in a shipping container, Philly-style.
  • Want democracy in chip form? Herr’s is crowdsourcing flavors for the U.S. 250th birthday. Freedom tastes like...your vote?
  • A UArts grad just landed a regular spot on Law & Order: SVU, because apparently Philly gets everywhere.
  • Heads up: Herr’s chip voting is open. Make them weird. We dare you.
  • Still hungry? Philly’s own Hakim’s Bookstore gets a shoutout in a new book on Black-owned bookstores.

That’s it for yesterday. Same time tomorrow. And if you see a penny on the ground today—take it. They’re officially history.
