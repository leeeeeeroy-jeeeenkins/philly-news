Philly’s Water Rates Jump, SEPTA’s ‘Saved,’ and Birds Take Center Stage

City Life: Rates Up, Homicides Down

Big one for your wallet: Philly water rates quietly went up yesterday. Average folks can now expect to shell out almost $100 more a year. Meanwhile, Philly is actually on pace for its lowest homicide total since 1966. (Yes, you read that right — even with all the city’s mess, the numbers are trending way down.)

SEPTA and Eagles: A Last-Minute Save

If you’re trying to get to The Linc for the Eagles’ opener: FanDuel stepped in to sponsor SEPTA’s Broad Street Line express trains, so those crucial rides are back—for now. But don’t relax: SEPTA’s funding fight isn’t going away, and both pols and planners are saying the system still needs big help to avoid future cuts.

Birds Hype Squad, On Full Blast

The Cowboys come to town for Week 1, and all hell (and hype) breaks loose tonight. Preseason predictions are rolling in, the Eagles hype video is out, and the skyline is glowing midnight green. Can’t get tickets? Bar crawls and sidewalk TV-watchings await. (And heads up: your fantasy league rivals are already annoying.)

Also in sports bar history: Philly’s first-ever women’s sports bar, Marsha’s, opens Sept. 19th in Queen Village. Finally!

Looking Toward 2026 (Already? Yep.)

The city unveiled a $100 million plan to get every neighborhood into the U.S. 250th birthday party. Expect block parties, art, Liberty Bell installations, and a lot more stuff far outside Old City. Details (and debates) are just ramping up: here’s what’s in the works.
