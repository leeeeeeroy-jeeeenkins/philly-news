Thanksgiving Recap, Eagles Updates, and Holiday Gridlock

Hope you survived the turkey, the pie, and the parking. Here’s what went down in Philly while you were debating sweet potato vs. pumpkin and working out how to recycle all those takeout containers.

Parade, Holiday Cheer, and Giving Back

  • The 106th Thanksgiving Day Parade brought out thousands to the Parkway. In true Philly fashion, despite overcast skies and wind, people lined up before sunrise for balloons, bands, and, yes, more than one Gritty sighting. Photos here.
  • Christmas Village is humming: full holiday mode is now activated in LOVE Park through December 24. If you're looking to dodge the malls, plenty of local pop-ups and makers on site.
  • Shoutout to Chosen 300, MANNA, and the William Way LGBT Community Center for serving thousands of Thanksgiving meals—including to folks facing homelessness or chronic illness.

Eagles and Sports: The Usual Injury Churn

  • Eagles-Bears preview: DeVonta Smith is officially good to go, so expect the full offense on Black Friday. Injury round-up is here. If you’re putting money down, odds and staff picks (cautious optimism, what else) here.
  • No luck for Sixers fans: Joel Embiid missed his ninth straight game. Not what you want to see before December.

Holiday Weekend: Getting Around

  • SEPTA has gone full holiday mode too—with a pop-up merch shop and the 'Jingle Rails' train display at Suburban Station. If you want to distract your kids, or yourself, this is it.
  • Heads up for Eagles fans: The city is testing a new traffic plan after Friday’s game at the Linc. If you’re driving, allow extra time for some not-yet-perfected detours. More info here.
  • Sticking around for the holidays? Christmas tree spots in the city—no need to hoof it out to Delco unless you’re after an eight-footer.

One Weird Thing

  • Okay, not local, but AI might unlock whale language soon. If only it could translate PPA tickets next.

That’s it for now. Pace yourself through the leftovers—and the traffic. Back tomorrow with more Philly chaos.
