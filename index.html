Yesterday in Philly

Subject: The City Hunkers Down, Snow Edition

Let’s get real about what’s happening in Philly right now.

Snowpocalypse Incoming (For Real)
It’s not just hype—this is shaping up to be Philly’s biggest storm in a decade, and the city is bracing for double-digit totals. Mayor Parker already declared a snow emergency, schools are closed Monday (here’s the details), and state/local officials have issued emergency orders (summary), with Gov. Shapiro signing a disaster emergency for PA (more here). Jersey is doing the same (here’s their situation). Don’t expect an easy run to the hardware store—everyone’s already cleaned out the salt, bread, and whatever else isn’t nailed down.

Transit & Streets: Delays, Closures, and a Lot of Slush
Consider yourself warned: SEPTA expects delays and cancellations on basically everything—and Philly has parking restrictions everywhere. If you haven’t moved your car or checked the city’s prep guide, now’s the time (bonus: Human Robot Brewery will discount your beer by the inch). Also: don’t hurt yourself shoveling—the ER does not want your overambitious energy (shoveling tips here).

Food & Fun (and a Shark Tank Tryout!)
A little sunshine in the storm: the James Beard Awards semifinalists dropped, and Philly’s newcomers are showing up strong. Meanwhile, Northern Liberties Restaurant Week is expanding to two weeks—plan your post-snow outings now. Oh, and Shark Tank auditions are coming to Philly, so if you’ve got the next big idea (or just want to watch the show IRL), mark your calendar.

Politics, Parks, and Public Backlash
The feds and city are trading shots over the removal of slavery exhibits at President’s House at Independence Park. City leaders are suing the National Park Service, and the community’s not pleased (recap here).

Sports Roundup
The Sixers are fighting through injuries (yes, Embiid and Paul George are probable for Saturday, so maybe not all doom). Elsewhere, Eagles are still searching for coaches—and, of course, actual respect in awards voting (nobody cares, apparently).

That’s it—charge your phone, buy your snacks, and see you on the other side of the snow pile.
