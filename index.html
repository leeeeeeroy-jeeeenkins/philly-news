Yesterday in Philly

Playoff Winds, Protests, & All-Pros

Playoff Hype, All-Pro Honors, and Who’s Got Rocky’s Back

Eagles on the Edge

We’re deep in playoff mode, which means Philly is neon-green. Big shoutout to rookie cornerbacks Quinyon Mitchell and Cooper DeJean, who both landed on the NFL’s All-Pro team. Not bad for their first year (haven’t seen that kind of fresh blood in a minute). Meanwhile, Jalen Hurts might run it himself more this weekend. Coaches won’t say, but you know how Hurts likes to ‘surprise’ defenses when it counts. If you’re betting, here’s the expert picks for wild card weekend. (And—if we actually get through, we already know who’s up next.) Keep an eye on that Rocky statue, too; 49ers fans getting bold, but be smart, West Coast.

Sixers’ Scramble

Sixers got a real one last night: tough win over the Magic and the new lineup looks like it might have some legs heading into spring. Embiid is questionable tonight against the Raptors — his knee’s iffy, but not ruled out yet. Something to stress about while finishing your Wawa hoagie.

Protests in the Streets

Hundreds hit Center City yesterday to protest the ICE-involved shooting in Minnesota and recent U.S. military action abroad. Lots of noise, but no major incidents here. Local advocates are pushing city leaders to do more about immigration enforcement—read what Philly activists want here.

Weather & Weekend Nonsense

It’s January, so of course the wind is planning its own Philly special. Gusty winds hit Sunday afternoon—hold onto your hats, trashcans, and whatever you pretend counts as recycling. The Home + Garden Show and Fishing Show are both coming up if you want to daydream about spring already.

Quick Hits

  • Big fire at Camden County apartment complex leasing office, but no word of injuries.
  • Stolen U-Haul led to a police chase in the Northeast—so, just another Saturday.
  • Families in PA are furious after a string of grave robberies.

That’s the roundup. Go birds. Layer up.
