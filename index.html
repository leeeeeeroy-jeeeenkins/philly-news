Yesterday in Philly

Subject: Welcome Back, MLK Bridge (And Mussels Trying to Save the Schuylkill)

Good morning. Here’s what happened in Philly yesterday (and what you actually need to know):

🚧 Getting Around

  • The MLK Drive Bridge is finally back open to cars, bikes, and pedestrians. Long detours are over, for now. Cyclists and runners: rejoice.
  • The Piazza Land Development in Northern Liberties is about to break ground this fall. More units, more life, and probably… more rent.

🥾 City & State Moves

  • Mayor Cherelle Parker gave her State of the City address. Housing and crime got top billing, but don’t look for a magic fix. Budget gridlock in Harrisburg isn’t helping: schools and nonprofits are feeling it.
  • SNAP food assistance rules are changing with new work requirements, but plenty of Philly residents could qualify for exemptions. If that’s you (or someone you know), here’s what to check.
  • Delco just created a human relations commission, but it’s not clear how it’ll work yet. Bureaucracy remains undefeated.

🌊 Fighting for Cleaner Water

  • Philly’s waterways are a mess, but local scientists are betting on mussels to help filter out pollution. Turns out, the city’s future might depend on a bunch of hard-working shellfish. (No word on whether they want union representation.)

🏥 Labor & Safety

  • Temple Hospital nurses just authorized a strike. No walkout dates yet, but stay tuned—especially if you need care.
  • Four local prison workers were charged with covering up an inmate assault. Another day, another scandal in the correctional system.

🎲 In Other News

  • Big weekend for pop culture: The Greater Philly Expo Center in Oaks is hosting Game Con Live for the first time. Gamers, this one’s for you.
  • Italian food fans: Eataly opens Oct. 2 at King of Prussia Mall. Prepping now for carbs you didn't plan on eating.
  • And yes, the Night Market on Ogontz is back for the first time in 15 years. Food, music, crowds. The usual.

🏈 Sports Quick Hits

  • The NFL spotlight is on the Eagles injury report ahead of the Rams playoff rematch. Staff picks and odds here.
  • Phillies fans: weekend rooting guide is all about that postseason slot. Mets are, predictably, a problem.

That’s the essentials. Breathe easy, get outside, and watch out for detours (and rogue mussels).
