SEPTA headaches, police raises, and your water ice forecast

Hope you weren’t planning to ride SEPTA stress-free next week. The latest is here, along with new police contracts, Chinatown project setbacks, and a few reasons to leave the house. Here’s what Philly just pulled off while you were out living your life.

SEPTA: The Struggle Continues

Lawmakers blew the deadline to hammer out state funding for SEPTA, and now the agency is moving ahead with service cuts and fare hikes starting Aug. 24. If you ride Regional Rail, Bus, Trolley, or the El, expect less frequent service and pricier fares. SEPTA is warning everyone to plan ahead—so maybe start mapping your Plan B now. And if you’ve got SEPTA-dependent kids headed back to school, parents in West Philly are already organizing.

City Hall: Police Get a Boost

While SEPTA is pinching pennies, Philly police got a new contract worth raises, bonuses, and extra benefits over the next two years. There’s also a new process to let civilians handle some department jobs—meant to free up more officers for the street. Mayor Parker called it a win for both cops and the city, but there’s a price tag attached for the city budget.

Chinatown Stitch Stalls Out

Remember the plans to reconnect Chinatown across the Vine Street Expressway? The Chinatown Stitch project just lost $150 million in federal money. Organizers say it’s not dead, but they’ll need to hunt for new funding—don’t expect bulldozers soon.
