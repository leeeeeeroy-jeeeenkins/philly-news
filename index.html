Yesterday in Philly: Bar Closes, Sports Jitters, SEPTA Shakes

Good morning, Philly. Here’s what actually happened while you were enjoying that fleeting weekend weather:

Closings & City Life

  • Say Goodbye to McGlinchey’s: Yes, it’s real: Center City’s legendary dive bar McGlinchey’s is closing for good soon. If those smoke-stained walls could talk… well, they’d probably just cough. Grab one last beer before it’s gone.
  • SEPTA Gets Even Messier: Service cuts are kicking in this week, so expect more waiting and confusion. SEPTA will have ambassadors out today to help lost souls, but plan ahead if you rely on the bus or El.

Philly Sports: New Faces, Familiar Stress

  • Eagles Make a Move: The Birds traded for Texans wide receiver John Metchie (yes, it’s official), aiming for more depth after a “meh” preseason outing against the Browns (awards rundown here). Curious about why Howie Roseman made the call? Here’s a breakdown.
  • Draft & Training Camp Watch: Early word on 2026 draft picks (never too soon) and a battle tracker for 2025 camp spots.
  • Phillies Injury Panic: The Phils split a bumpy series with the Nationals. Aaron Nola wasn’t sharp, and there’s real concern about Zack Wheeler’s health (recap / how they survive if he misses time). Alec Bohm homered in his return (recap).
  • Bonus Mascot Trivia: The Phillie Phanatic stays cool with vodka and water. Relatable.

Events & Arts

  • Poll Worker Shout-Out: The City Commissioners launched a new effort to recruit younger poll workers. Democracy doesn’t run itself — and most folks running your polling place could use some backup.
  • Chalk & Crafts: Delco just did its first Chalk Fest, while Collingswood’s annual Crafts & Fine Arts Festival spotlighted local talent over the weekend.
  • Soccer Fever Starts Early: Expect more World Cup buzz — this weekend was the Fiesta Futbolera, getting Philly hyped for FIFA 2026.

Weather & Heads Up

  • Cooler Monday: Temps are dropping a bit, but plan for clouds. (Forecast)

Catch you tomorrow. And seriously — hit McGlinchey’s while you still can.
