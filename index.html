SEPTA Cuts Hit as School Starts, Main Street Floods, and Eagles Shuffle the Deck

Yesterday in Philly: What Actually Happened

Transit Trouble Just in Time for Back-to-School

If you rely on SEPTA to get anywhere, brace yourself—the first round of service cuts start this weekend. The timing could not be worse: with Philly public school students heading back Monday, it’s going to get messy. The district and local colleges are scrambling to come up with backup plans, but, honestly, everyone’s just hoping for the best. SEPTA's official advice? "Have a plan"—not exactly reassuring. Also, expect heightened safety measures for student riders, but that won't help if your bus never arrives.

Civic Curveballs: Floods and Policy Moves

Manayunk's Main Street is drying out (again) after a major water main break flooded businesses, closed roads, and generally made a mess of Friday commutes. If you’re headed that way, check updates before you go.
Elsewhere, food truck and bodega owners are putting the pressure on City Council to rethink expanding business curfews. Owners say it’ll crush small shops and corner stores—expect this debate to heat up.

Floods, Flyers, and False Alarms

Hurricane Erin pushed heavy rain and flooding down the shore, causing dramatic rescues in Margate. Meanwhile, Villanova went into lockdown thanks to a fake active shooter report—thankfully, all fake but a real headache for students and parents.

Eagles, Phillies, and (Almost) Real Football

Preseason continues, and the Eagles took on the Jets last night. The bottom of the roster is full musical chairs before Tuesday’s final cutdown; expect more moves. On the baseball side, the Phillies are finally home after sweeping the Mariners—a reminder why people buy those ridiculous rally towels.
