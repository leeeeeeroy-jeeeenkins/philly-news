Yesterday in Philly: Food Hype, Penn Pride & Farewells

September 24, 2025

Big Headlines

  • The Final Lap for AIDS Walk Philly: After 40 years, AIDS Walk Philly is calling it. The city’s signature fundraising walk is ending, leaving behind a legacy that changed how Philly fights HIV/AIDS and probably inspired a few sore feet along the way.
  • Penn Flexes, Again: Penn quietly hopped to #7 in the new US News rankings, and, oh yeah, also scored a mysterious $10 million donation to do urban research. Not a bad couple of days for West Philly’s finest.
  • High-Tech Thieves, Meet High-Tech Cops: Car thefts are up, and Philly Police have launched a new push to fend off keyless-carjackers—and the Bucks County ‘boost’ trend isn’t helping (details here).
  • Ransomware Hits Harrisburg: A global ransomware group took credit for a cyberattack on the Pennsylvania Attorney General’s Office. No news yet on what exactly was stolen or exposed. If you were hoping the state’s top lawyer had better IT, same.

What’s Opening, What’s Closing

  • Eataly Is Finally Here (Sort Of): If you somehow missed the food-world chatter, Eataly is opening in King of Prussia. Yes, it’s technically the suburbs, but for anyone craving imported pasta by the pallet, your moment awaits.
  • Restaurant Week…ish: No official Center City Restaurant Week this fall, but chef Townsend Wentz is running special prix fixe menus at his spots. Philly always finds a way when there’s a good meal at stake.

On the Move

  • Citizens Bank Park Sleepover (for a Cause): Hundreds will sleep outside at the Phillies stadium on Nov. 20, supporting youth homelessness programs. If you want an excuse to be cold on a baseball field for a good cause, this is it.
  • Boo at the Zoo Is Back: The Philly Zoo’s big Halloween bash runs for three weekends. Yes, it’s for kids, but, like—who doesn’t love giraffes in costumes?

In Brief

  • Camden: 0 Murders All Summer! For the first time in 50 years, Camden had a homicide-free summer. Not Philly, but still, worth a shout.
  • Pennsylvania Politics: Gov. Shapiro says if you need vaccine advice, maybe skip the CDC for now and check local groups instead. Tech innovation’s happening in Kensington, too: AI innovation hub just opened.

That’s it for yesterday. If you were hoping for less news about crime, sorry—there’s always tomorrow. (Or hit Eater Philly and just read about new food until you feel better.)
