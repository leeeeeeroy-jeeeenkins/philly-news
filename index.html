Yesterday in Philly: Rocky Moves, Eagles Shuffle, And Avenue Upgrades

Hey, here’s what you actually missed in Philly yesterday. No fluff, just the good stuff.

Center City Upgrades & Rocky’s Big Move

  • Streetscape glow-up: Avenue of the Arts is about to get a facelift — think wider sidewalks, new lighting, fancier bus shelters, bike racks, and landscaping. Work starts this month. Expect good things for foot traffic (and probably some construction headaches).
  • Rocky statue news: After years of standing awkwardly off to the side, the Rocky statue is finally heading to the top of the Art Museum steps — where all the tourists already act like it is anyway.

SEPTA, Homelessness & Renting Woes

  • SEPTA crime drops: Serious crime on SEPTA was at a 10-year low last year. Still, riders are more worried about 'quality-of-life' stuff (think harassment, not heists).
  • Census in the cold: The city's annual homeless count is coming up — yes, Philly does it in January on purpose, so the numbers aren't artificially low. Hard but (they say) necessary.
  • City Council returns: Issues to watch: affordable housing, tenant protections, tax breaks. No miracles promised, but at least they’re talking about it.

Sports Shuffle

  • Sixers’ big names (probably) back: Embiid and Paul George are both listed as probable for tomorrow vs. the Cavs (injury update). Barlow questionable, Sixers still got blown out last night (recap).
  • Eagles’ never-ending OC search: Yes, the Eagles are still hunting for an offensive coordinator, and Howie swears it's actually a good sign. (Sure it is.) Lots of names in the rumor mill. Meanwhile, questions linger over A.J. Brown’s future but most think he’s staying put.

Food, Fun, & Other City Bits

  • Pasta obsession: Chicken riggies from upstate New York are the new carb wave on Philly menus. Try them before they’re everywhere.
  • Alcohol-free hangs: Dry Vibes and more are tapping into Philly’s sober social scene. Good for Dry January…or any month.
  • Vintage finds: Fresh design market hits Crane Arts this weekend—maybe you’ll score that green velvet sofa you’ve been DM-ing strangers about.
  • MLK Weekend & Restaurant Week: Things to do, right here (plus a Sherlock Holmes play if you need to Sherlock your way out of the cold).

That’s yesterday. Stay warm, stay skeptical. If you want sunshine, Miami isn’t that far (temptation here).
